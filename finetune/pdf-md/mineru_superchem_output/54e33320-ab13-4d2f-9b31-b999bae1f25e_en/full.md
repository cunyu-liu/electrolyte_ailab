# Question

$\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  are short-period elements of the same main group.

The reaction between the chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$  and a compound containing approximately  $65\%$  of  $\mathbf{Y}$  can prepare  $\mathbf{A}$ ; alternatively, the chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$  and the hydride of  $\mathbf{Y}$  in a 3:5 ratio can also yield compound  $\mathbf{A}$  under high temperature. In  $\mathbf{A}$ , the coordination number of  $\mathbf{X}$  atoms is 4, while that of  $\mathbf{Y}$  atoms is 2 and 3. Under high temperature and a nitrogen atmosphere, the reaction between  $\mathbf{A}$  and salt  $\mathbf{B}$ , or the 1:2 reaction between the lithium salt of  $\mathbf{Y}$  and  $\mathrm{LiXY}_2$ , both produce compound  $\mathbf{P}$ . The anion of  $\mathbf{P}$  is isoelectronic with phosphorus pentoxide, while the anion of  $\mathbf{B}$  is isoelectronic with the perchlorate ion.

Which of the following statements best fits the context?

A. The anion of  $\mathbf{P}$  carries eight negative charges.  
B. The anion of  $\mathbf{P}$  possesses three fourfold rotation axes.  
C. The equation for the preparation of A from the chloride of X and a compound containing approximately  $65\%$  of Y, with the coefficients in the simplest whole number ratio such that the sum of the coefficients of the reactants is greater than 20.  
D. The lithium salt of  $\mathbf{Y}$  reacts with  $\mathrm{LiXY}_2$  to form compound  $\mathbf{P}$ . When the coefficients in the chemical equation for this reaction are in their simplest whole-number ratio, the sum of the coefficients of the reactants is 9.  
E. It is known that above  $1100\mathrm{K}$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$  decomposes through a two-step reaction, each step producing an elemental substance, and the decomposition proceeds via the intermediate  $\mathbf{O}$ ; considering the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{O}$  in its simplest integer ratio, its relative molecular mass is greater than or equal to 45.  
F. The ratio of  $\mathbf{Y}$  atoms with two-coordination and three-coordination in A is 2:3.

G. The chemical equation for the reaction between A and salt B, with the coefficients in the simplest whole-number ratio, has a sum of the product coefficients equal to 7.  
H. None of the above options is correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

X and Y are short-period elements in the same main group. Since Y forms a lithium salt, alkali metals and alkaline earth metals are first excluded.

The coordination numbers of the  $\mathbf{Y}$  atom are 2 and 3, suggesting that  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  are nitrogen and phosphorus.

1 PTS

$\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  are inferred to be nitrogen and phosphorus

Since the compound  $\mathrm{LiXY}_2$  exists,  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  cannot be chalcogens, halogens, or elements of Group 13, as no such compounds are known. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  must belong to the nitrogen group, with  $\mathbf{Y}$  having a lower coordination number, likely nitrogen, and  $\mathbf{X}$  being phosphorus. In this case,  $\mathrm{LiXY}_2$  corresponds to  $\mathrm{LiPN}_2$ , a known compound, confirming the deduction.

The hydride of  $\mathbf{Y}$  should be ammonia, which reacts with the chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$  in a 5:3 ratio. Based on elemental knowledge and coordination number information, the product  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{N}_5$ , indicating the chloride is phosphorus pentachloride. The equations for the formation of  $\mathbf{A}$  are:
