# Question

Mining minerals generates a large amount of wastewater. The steps for the highly selective iodometric determination of cobalt content are as follows:

$\times$  textcircled{1} Weigh  $0.2517 \mathrm{~g}$  of arsenic trioxide standard, dissolve in  $10 \% \mathrm{NaOH}$ , and dilute to  $250.0 \mathrm{~mL}$ .

\textcircled{2} Take  $500.0\mathrm{mL}$  of wastewater, evaporate and concentrate it, add a certain amount of nitric acid-hydrochloric acid, evaporate to dryness, dissolve soluble salts with an appropriate amount of water, add nitric acid and potassium chloride again, boil, filter the precipitate, and wash the precipitate.

$\mathrm{pH} = 9 - 9.5$ , transfer to  $50.0\mathrm{mL}$  of iodine standard solution, shake and let stand for a period of time.

\textcircled{4} Titrate the excess iodine with sodium arsenite solution. Add starch near the end point. Take the reading after the blue color disappears. The blank experiment consumed  $30.46\mathrm{mL}$  of sodium arsenite, and the titration experiment consumed  $14.65\mathrm{mL}$  of sodium arsenite.

Based on the above content, select the correct answer.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The chemical equation for the titration of iodine with arsenite solution in the system is as follows:  $\mathrm{AsO}_3^{3-} + \mathrm{I}_2 + 2\mathrm{NH}_3 + \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O} \rightarrow \mathrm{AsO}_4^{3-} + 2\mathrm{I}^- + 2\mathrm{NH}_4^+$  
C. It is possible to accurately weigh sublimed and purified iodine solid to prepare a standard iodine solution, and then use a  $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{~S}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{3}$  solution to replace the sodium arsenite solution for titration.  
D. The concentration of Co element in the sample is  $18.96 \mathrm{mg} / \mathrm{L}$

E. The concentration of Co element in the sample is  $9.48 \mathrm{mg} / \mathrm{L}$  
F. The concentration of Co element in the sample is  $37.93 \mathrm{mg} / \mathrm{L}$

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

In the chemical reaction equation, the pKa of the third ionization step of arsenic acid is 11.50, and the pKa of the second ionization step is 6.96. The pKa of the ammonium ion is 9.25.
