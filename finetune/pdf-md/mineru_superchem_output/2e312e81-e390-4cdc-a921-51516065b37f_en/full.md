# Question

Yellow solid A is composed of two elements, a metallic element X and a non-metallic element Y, and is one of the important minerals of element X. Calcination of A in air can produce solid B and colorless gas C. B contains  $69.94\%$  of element X. C reacts with  $\mathrm{Cl}_2$  to obtain a colorless fuming liquid D. D is easily hydrolyzed to produce E and F. C can react with  $\mathrm{O}_2$  to produce G under the action of a heated catalyst. G is soluble in water and also yields E.

C is an excellent Lewis acid. Researchers recently discovered that the reaction of  $0.5\mathrm{mL}$  of anhydrous C with  $0.02\mathrm{g}$  of a certain metal azide I at low temperature can produce an adduct H. The nitrogen content in H is  $13.87\%$ . If the orientation of C in H is ignored, its anionic symmetry is the same as that of water.

The following statements are correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. A is a 1:2 type ionic compound.  
C. The highest oxidation state that element  $\mathbf{X}$  can form is  $+4$  
D. D is a regular tetrahedral molecule  
E. G has a certain reducing property.  
F. The mass fraction of nitrogen in I is approximately  $28\%$  
G. The mass fraction of H cation is  $43.9\%$  
H. H is a 2:1 type ionic compound.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

$\mathbf{B}$  is obtained by calcining  $\mathbf{A}$ , it can be determined that  $\mathbf{B}$  is the stable oxide of element  $\mathbf{X}$ ; let the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{B}$  be  $\mathbf{XO}_{\mathrm{n}}$ , containing  $69.94\%$  of element  $\mathbf{X}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$\mathbf{B}$  is the stable oxide of element  $\mathbf{X}$

If it is  $\mathbf{XO}$ , the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $16 / (1 - 0.6994) - 16 = 37.2$ , no reasonable metal.

If it is  $\mathbf{X}_2\mathrm{O}_3$ , the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $(16\times 3 / (1 - 0.6994) - 16\times 3) / 2 = 55.9$ , which is Fe.

If it is  $\mathbf{XO}_2$ , the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $16 \times 2 / (1 - 0.6994) - 16 \times 2 = 74.5$ , no reasonable metal.

Other oxide types also have no reasonable results; therefore  $\mathbf{X}$  is Fe,  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $\mathrm{Fe_2O_3}$ . Fe exists in the  $+6$  valence state, option C is incorrect.
