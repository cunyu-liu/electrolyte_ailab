# Question

In liquid ammonia at  $238\mathrm{K}$ , sodium and white phosphorus react at a certain molar ratio to form a yellow crystal. Further analysis indicates that this yellow crystal is a  $1:1$  ionic compound; both its cation and anion are mononuclear sodium coordination ions, each with a coordination number of 5. # Answer

Correct Answer: T

# Detailed Explanation

In a system containing only the four elements Na, P, H, N, the phosphorus-containing ligand in the anion should consist solely of P, H, N elements. Based on molar mass, the possible chemical formulas for the ligand are  $\mathrm{PN}_4\mathrm{H}_9^-$ ,  $\mathrm{P}_2\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{H}_6$ , or  $\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3^{2-}$ . Considering the octet rule for phosphorus, the ligand cannot be  $\mathrm{PN}_4\mathrm{H}_9^-$ . If the ligand were  $\mathrm{P}_2\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{H}_6$ , the complex formed with sodium ions could not carry a negative charge. Therefore, the chemical formula of the ligand must be  $\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3^{2-}$ .

The ligand's chemical formula is  $\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3^{2-}$

Since the cation is five-coordinate, the cation is  $\mathrm{Na(NH_3)_5^+}$ .

The cation is  $\mathrm{Na(NH_3)_5^+}$

Given that the anion is five-coordinate and the  $\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3^{2-}$  ligand in the anion is chelating, the remaining three coordination sites are occupied by  $\mathrm{NH}_3$ , making the anion  $\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3)(\mathrm{NH}_3)_3^-$ .

The anion is  $\mathrm{Na(P_3H_3)(NH_3)_3^-}$

Thus, the chemical formula of the yellow crystal is  $\left[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{NH}_3)_5\right]\left[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3)(\mathrm{NH}_3)_3\right]$ . The chemical formula of white phosphorus is  $\mathbf{P}_4$ .

The chemical formula of the yellow crystal is  $\left[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{NH}_3)_5\right]\left[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3)(\mathrm{NH}_3)_3\right]$

The left side of the equation consists of  $\mathrm{Na},\mathrm{P}_4,\mathrm{NH}_3$  while the right side includes  $[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{NH}_3)_5][\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{P}_3\mathrm{H}_3)(\mathrm{NH}_3)_3]$  and  $\mathrm{NaNH_2}$ . Balancing the equation yields:

$$
2 0 \mathrm {N a} + 3 \mathrm {P} _ {4} + 4 4 \mathrm {N H} _ {3} \rightarrow 4 [ \mathrm {N a} (\mathrm {N H} _ {3}) _ {5} ] [ \mathrm {N a} (\mathrm {P} _ {3} \mathrm {H} _ {3}) (\mathrm {N H} _ {3}) _ {3} ] + 1 2 \mathrm {N a N H} _ {2}
$$

The equation is  $20\mathrm{Na} + 3\mathrm{P}_{4} + 44\mathrm{NH}_{3}\rightarrow 4[\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{NH}_{3})_{5}][\mathrm{Na}(\mathrm{P}_{3}\mathrm{H}_{3})(\mathrm{NH}_{3})_{3}] + 12\mathrm{NaNH}_{2}$

The sum of the coefficients is 83, so option T is correct.
