# Question

Graphdiyne is a novel carbon material. Similar to graphene, graphdiyne consists of a single-layer network structure composed of  $\mathfrak{sp}^2$  and  $\mathfrak{sp}$  hybridized carbon, with layers connected by van der Waals forces. Because  $\mathfrak{sp}^2$  hybridized carbon and  $\mathfrak{sp}$  hybridized carbon can have various arrangements, graphdiyne can have multiple structures. In one type of graphdiyne, each  $C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)$  is connected to three  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$ , and each  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$  is connected to one  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$  and one  $C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)$  respectively, which we temporarily name graphdiyne  $A$  here; in another type of graphdiyne, each  $C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)$  is connected to two  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$  and one  $C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)$  respectively, and each  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$  is connected to one  $C(\mathfrak{sp})$  and one  $C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)$  respectively, which we temporarily name graphdiyne  $B$  here; in addition, there is a graphdiyne structure consisting of a network structure composed of  $[C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)C(\mathfrak{sp})C(\mathfrak{sp})C(\mathfrak{sp}^2)]$ , but containing a benzene ring structure, which we temporarily name graphdiyne  $C$  here.

The graphdiyne  $C$  variant, graphdiynes, can be synthesized on any substrate using hexaethynylbenzene as a monomer. The method involves wrapping the substrate material in a metal mesh and immersing it in an acetone solution containing hexaethynylbenzene in pyridine and tetramethylethylenediamine, which can induce a Glaser-Hay alkyne coupling reaction on the substrate to obtain the grown graphdiynes.

Based on the above background information, which of the following statements is incorrect ( )?

B. Metal mesh elements have two stable ionic valence states.

# Answer

ABAB stacking means that the rings of adjacent layers (A and B) are not aligned, but the cocentric rings of different sizes are staggered, so the polygon projections of layers A and B should be staggered in the top view. The description "sodium ions are filled in the interlayer and the position of sodium ions in each layer is the same" indicates that sodium ions are located between the two layers. If the sodium ions are located in the center of the overlapping area, the overlapping area may not be centered after the AB layers are misaligned. "Sodium ions are

located in the center of the regular hexagon formed by the overlapping projections of layers A and B" is incorrect because the misalignment of AB stacking causes the overlapping area of the projection to no longer be the center of the original hexagon. Therefore, the interlayer sodium ions should occupy the projection of the intersection of three adjacent polygons, similar to the intercalation sites of lithium ions in graphite. Sodium ions are located at the trifurcated voids where the vertices of adjacent A and B layer polygons intersect, with one sodium ion at each trifurcation point.
