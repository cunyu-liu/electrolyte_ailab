# Question

Metal  $\mathbf{X}$  is soluble in aqua regia to obtain  $\mathbf{A}$ , which is then treated with  $SO_2$  to obtain  $\mathbf{B}$ . Saturated solution of  $\mathbf{Y}$  is added to the above mixture, and orange-red crystals of  $\mathbf{C}$  precipitate.  $\mathbf{C}$  can be reduced by  $H_2$  under heating conditions to obtain elemental  $\mathbf{X}$ . A dilute solution of  $\mathbf{C}$  is colorless. When a concentrated solution of the highly toxic salt  $\mathbf{Z}$  is added to it, a light yellow precipitate  $\mathbf{D}$  precipitates.  $1.00g$  of  $\mathbf{D}$  can absorb  $8.9mL$  ( $1atm$ ,  $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ) of chlorine gas, resulting in a non-stoichiometric compound  $\mathbf{E}$  with electrical conductivity, and the color changes from pale yellow to bronze.  $\mathbf{E}$  can also be obtained by "combining"  $\mathbf{D}$  and a certain complex  $\mathbf{F}$  in solution in an approximate 5:1 ratio and precipitating. (The valence state of the metal in  $\mathbf{F}$  is higher than that in  $\mathbf{E}$ )

The crystallographic information of  $\mathbf{C}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  is as follows:

C is a tetragonal unit cell with three types of atoms. The atomic coordinates of all three types of atoms are as follows:

The first type of atom is located at  $(0,0,0)$

The second type of atom is located at  $(0, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2})$  and  $(\frac{1}{2}, 0, \frac{1}{2})$

The third type of atom is located at  $(\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{4}, 0)$ ,  $(\frac{3}{4}, \frac{1}{4}, 0)$ ,  $(\frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4}, 0)$ ,  $(\frac{3}{4}, \frac{3}{4}, 0)$ .

The unit cell parameters of  $\mathbf{C}$  are  $a = b = 702.4\mathrm{pm}$ ,  $c = 414.7\mathrm{pm}$ , and the density is  $3.369~\mathrm{g / cm^3}$

$\mathbf{Y}$  has a rock salt structure with a unit cell parameter of  $a = 629.2\mathrm{pm}$  and a density of  $1.987~\mathrm{g / cm^3}$

The crystal of  $\mathbf{Z}$  is isostructural with  $\mathbf{Y}$  at room temperature, and its hot water solution has a bitter almond taste.

Determine the chemical formulas of  $\mathbf{X}$ ,  $\mathbf{Y}$ ,  $\mathbf{Z}$ , and  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $\mathbf{B}$ ,  $\mathbf{C}$ ,  $\mathbf{D}$ ,  $\mathbf{E}$ , and select the correct options from the following.

A. The sum of the reactant coefficients in the chemical equation for the dissolution of  $\mathbf{X}$  in aqua regia (note: not the ionic equation) is 23.

B. The sum of the reactant coefficients in the chemical equation for the dissolution of  $\mathbf{X}$  in aqua regia (note: not the ionic equation) is 24.  
C. The sum of the reactant coefficients in the chemical equation for the dissolution of  $\mathbf{X}$  in aqua regia (note: not the ionic equation) is 27.  
D. In the crystal structure of  $\mathbf{C}$ , the sum of the coordination numbers of the elements with positive valence is 10, and the difference in coordination numbers is 4.  
E. In the crystal structure of  $\mathbf{C}$ , the sum of the coordination numbers of the elements with positive valence is 16, and the difference in coordination numbers is 4.  
F. In the chemical reaction equation where  $\mathbf{E}$  is precipitated through  $\mathbf{D}$  and a certain complex  $\mathbf{F}$  in solution, the actual ratio of  $\mathbf{D}$  to  $\mathbf{F}$  is  $\frac{17}{3}$ .  
G. In the chemical reaction equation where  $\mathbf{E}$  is precipitated through  $\mathbf{D}$  and a certain complex  $\mathbf{F}$  in solution, the actual ratio of  $\mathbf{D}$  to  $\mathbf{F}$  is  $\frac{16}{3}$ .  
H. None of the above options is correct
