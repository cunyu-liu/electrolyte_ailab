# Question

Quantitative Analysis of Strong Oxidants

$\mathrm{NaBiO}_3$  and  $\mathrm{PbO}_2$  are strong oxidants commonly used in industry. Their redox properties can be utilized to conduct volumetric analysis studies.

$\mathrm{NaBiO}_3$  is a light yellow powder that is insoluble in cold water. Accurately weigh  $0.8671\mathrm{g}$  of sodium bismuthate sample, add an appropriate amount of dilute sulfuric acid and  $\mathrm{MnSO}_4$  solution to completely convert it to  $\mathrm{Bi}^{3+}$ , and then titrate with a  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  standard solution with a concentration of  $0.1080\mathrm{mol}\cdot\mathrm{L}^{-1}$ . When the purple-red color of the solution just disappears,  $24.89\mathrm{mL}$  of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  solution is consumed.

$\mathrm{PbO}_2$  is easily decomposed by heat. Heating a certain amount of  $\mathrm{PbO}_2$  yields some solid  $(x\mathrm{PbO}_2\cdot y\mathrm{PbO})$ . To investigate the composition of this solid, a certain mass of the solid is taken and added to  $30~\mathrm{mL}$ $\mathrm{CH}_3\mathrm{COOH} - \mathrm{CH}_3\mathrm{COONa}$  buffer solution, and then a sufficient amount of KI solid is added. The conical flask is shaken to completely dissolve the solid, resulting in a brown solution. Using starch as an indicator, the solution is titrated with  $0.1020\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  of  $\mathrm{Na_2S_2O_3}$  standard solution until the blue color of the solution just disappears, consuming  $24.70~\mathrm{mL}$  of  $\mathrm{Na_2S_2O_3}$  standard solution. The same mass of the above solid is mixed with dilute nitric acid to obtain a brownish-black powder. The filtrate obtained by filtration is added with a sufficient amount of  $\mathrm{K_2CrO_4}$  solution to convert it to  $\mathrm{PbCrO_4}$  precipitate. The precipitate is washed and dried, and the mass of the solid obtained is  $0.611\mathrm{g}$ .

Determine the correctness of the following statements

1. The purity of the sodium bismuthate sample (calculated as the mass fraction of  $\mathrm{NaBiO_3}$ ) is  $87.69\%$
