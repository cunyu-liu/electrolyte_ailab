# Question

Halogen chemistry is an important part of elemental chemistry, and  $\mathbf{X}$  element is a halogen. The simple ion  $\mathbf{X}^{+}$  is often considered unable to exist independently, but it was later discovered that compound A containing a monovalent positive X element can be obtained under the following conditions:  $\mathbf{X}_{2} + \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C} \rightarrow \mathbf{A}$ . Known: B is a common silver source, and its anion does not contain halogens and is an ionic cluster; C is a common base in organic synthesis and has a certain nucleophilicity, and is considered to be used as a nucleophilic catalyst. The molecular structure of C has multi-center delocalized bonds and contains only one  $N$  atom; oxidation-reduction and acid-base complexation reactions occur during the reaction to generate A.

Based on the above information, determine the correct option from the following statements.

A.  $\mathbf{X}$  is the element bromine,  $\mathbf{C}$  reacts with elemental bromine,  $B r_{2}$  acts as an electrophile and is attacked by the nitrogen atom of  $\mathbf{C}$ .  
B. A 's monovalent  $\mathbf{X}$  combines with one molecule of C, thereby obtaining a stable anion which is nitrate.  
C.  $\mathbf{X}$  is an iodine atom, because the electronegativity of the iodine atom is relatively small, and it combines with  $\mathbf{C}$  in a 1:1 ratio.  
D. B is silver nitrate, which is often used to test for halide anions. In the synthesis of A, the nitrate acts as an oxidizing agent.  
E. The ratio of  $\mathbf{X}$  atoms to  $\mathbf{C}$  molecules in  $\mathbf{A}$  is 1 to 2, because each  $\mathbf{C}$  molecule contains one  $N$  atom, so the ratio of  $\mathbf{X}$  atoms to  $N$  atoms is also 1 to 2. In addition to  $\mathbf{A}$ , the reaction also produces a product containing monovalent anionic  $\mathbf{X}$ .  
F. Hypervalent bonding exists in  $\mathbf{A}$ .

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

Halogen elements have high electronegativity and are difficult to form  $\mathbf{X}^{+}$ . Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  should be the  $I$  element with the largest radius and the smallest electronegativity;

C is a common base in organic chemistry that can be used as a nucleophilic catalyst and has only one nitrogen atom. It can be determined as pyridine  $(C_{5}H_{5}N)$ ;

$I^{+}$  has one empty orbital and three lone pairs of electrons. If it is 1:1 coordination, it will form an N-I single bond, which is unstable and has too high energy. If it is 1:2 coordination, the empty orbital of I forms a 3-center 4-electron bond with the lone pair electrons of N on two pyridines, which is more stable than a single bond. So  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $[I(C_{5}H_{5}N)_{2}]NO_{3}$

According to the above analysis, the correct statement in each option should be F.

The element in A is wrong, and the reaction mechanism is complex, not a simple nucleophilic attack reaction, which is wrong.

The ratio of X to pyridine in B is wrong.

The ratio of X to pyridine in C is wrong.

In D, nitrate does not act as an oxidant, which is wrong.

In E, nitrate also has  $N$  atoms, so the ratio of  $\mathbf{X}$  to  $N$  is 1:3.
