# Question

$\mathbf{X}$  is a basic carbonate of metal  $\mathbf{M}$ , possibly containing water of crystallization. Two samples of equal mass were taken and treated separately: one was thoroughly treated in hydrogen, collecting  $2.16\mathrm{~g}$  of water, and the remaining residue was the element of metal  $\mathbf{M}$ , weighing  $10.36\mathrm{~g}$ ; the other was treated in oxygen, and after complete reaction,  $0.448\mathrm{~L}$  of carbon dioxide was obtained ( $101.325\mathrm{kPa}$ ,  $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ), and the remaining residue was the highest oxide of  $\mathbf{M}$ . Then the metal  $\mathbf{M}$  and the carbon:oxygen ratio in  $\mathbf{X}$  are respectively:

A. All other options are incorrect.  
B. Cu,1:5  
C. Cu,1:8  
D. Cu,4:15

F. Mn,1:8  
G. Mn,4:15  
H. Zn,1:5

# Answer

Correct Answer: L

# Detailed Explanation

Assuming the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathbf{M}(\mathrm{OH})_{\mathrm{x}}(\mathrm{CO}_3)_{\mathrm{y}}\cdot \mathrm{zH}_2\mathrm{O}$ ,

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Set up unknowns to represent the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{X}$  as  $\mathbf{M}(\mathrm{OH})_{\mathrm{x}}(\mathrm{CO}_3)_{\mathrm{y}}\cdot \mathrm{zH}_2\mathrm{O}$

then the equations for the two reactions are respectively:

$$
\mathbf {M} (\mathrm {O H}) _ {\mathrm {x}} \left(\mathrm {C O} _ {3}\right) _ {\mathrm {y}} \cdot \mathrm {z H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \left(\frac {\mathrm {x}}{2} + \mathrm {y}\right) \mathrm {H} _ {2} \rightarrow \mathrm {y C O} _ {2} + \left(\mathrm {x} + \mathrm {y} + \mathrm {z}\right) \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathbf {M}
$$

and

$$
\mathbf {M} (\mathrm {O H}) _ {\mathrm {x}} \left(\mathrm {C O} _ {3}\right) _ {\mathrm {y}} \cdot \mathrm {z H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \left(\frac {2 \mathrm {a} - \mathrm {x} - 2 \mathrm {y}}{4}\right) \mathrm {O} _ {2} \rightarrow \left(\frac {\mathrm {x}}{2} + \mathrm {z}\right) \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathrm {y C O} _ {2} + \mathbf {M} O _ {a}
$$

Thus, we obtain the equation:

$$
\frac {x + y + z}{y} = \frac {2 . 1 6 / 1 8 . 0 1 6}{0 . 4 4 8 / 2 2 . 4}
$$

Simplifying, we get  $x + z = 5y$

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Deduce the relationship between unknowns  $x + z = 5y$

From the mass of the metal, we can list the second equation:

$$
{\frac {1 0 . 3 6}{\mathrm {M}}} = {\frac {2 . 1 6}{1 8 . 0 1 6 (x + y + z)}} = {\frac {2 . 1 6}{1 8 . 0 1 6 \cdot (6 y)}} = {\frac {1}{5 0 . 0 4 y}}
$$

Try to solve the equation. When  $y = 2 / 5$ , there is a reasonable solution  $\mathrm{Pb}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Obtain  $\mathbf{M}$  as  $\mathrm{Pb}$  by solving the indeterminate equation

At this time,  $x + z = 5y = 2$

The common valence states of  $\mathrm{Pb}$  are  $+2$  and  $+4$ . Here, it is obviously  $+2$ , so from the valence, we can calculate  $x = \frac{6}{5}$ , thus  $z = \frac{4}{5}$

Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  should be  $\mathrm{Pb(OH)}_{\frac{6}{5}}(\mathrm{CO}_3)_{\frac{2}{5}} \cdot \frac{4}{5} \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The chemical formula of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathrm{Pb(OH)}_{\frac{6}{5}}(\mathrm{CO}_3)_{\frac{2}{5}} \cdot \frac{4}{5}\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$

Therefore, choose option L
