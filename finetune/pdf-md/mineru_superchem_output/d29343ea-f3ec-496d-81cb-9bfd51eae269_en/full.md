# Question

# Known:

1. The triple point of water is  $273.16\mathrm{K}$ , and the freezing point is  $273.15\mathrm{K}$ .  
2. The relationship between the freezing point and pressure is  $\Delta \mathrm{T} / \Delta \mathrm{P} = -7.432 \times 10^{-8} \mathrm{~K} / \mathrm{Pa}$ .  
3. At the triple point, the vapor pressure of water is  $610.48\mathrm{Pa}$ .  
4. The volume fractions of nitrogen and oxygen in the air are  $79\%$  and  $21\%$ , and their Henry's constants are  $1.035 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1} / \mathrm{kPa}$  and  $2.154 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1} / \mathrm{kPa}$ , respectively.  
5. The atmospheric pressure is  $101325\mathrm{Pa}$ , and the acceleration due to gravity is taken as  $9.8~m / s^2$ .

Based on the known information, the problems to be calculated are:

(a). The numerical value of the freezing point depression constant of water when all units are in SI units (keep two significant figures).  
(b). Place a cup of water saturated with air on a mountain top at an altitude of  $5000m$ , what is the freezing point of this cup of water (in K, keep five significant figures)?

The following options all contain two values, the first is the answer to question (a), and the second is the answer to question (b). The option with both answers correct is (calculation error less than  $0.1\%$  is considered correct):

# Detailed Explanation

Problem (a):

The freezing point depression consists of two parts: the relationship between pressure and freezing point, and the colligative property of dilute solutions of air dissolved in water leading to freezing point depression.

According to Henry's law, the molar concentration  $c$  of dissolved gas is proportional to the partial pressure  $p$  of the gas

$$
c = k _ {H} \cdot p
$$

For dilute aqueous solutions, the molality  $b$  is numerically approximately equal to the molarity  $c$

Problem (b):

First, it is necessary to calculate the change in pressure with altitude according to the Boltzmann distribution:

$$
\ln (p _ {1} / p _ {2}) = - M g \Delta h / R T
$$
