# Question

$\mathbf{M}$  is an element of the sixth period. The flue gas obtained by leaching roasted molybdenite with water, the volatile oxide  $\mathbf{N}$  of  $\mathbf{M}$  (containing  $\mathbf{M} 76.9\%$ ) is absorbed, and the resulting solution contains acid  $\mathbf{A}$ ; the solution is purified, concentrated, and alkaline gas  $\mathbf{B}$  is passed into it, crystallization occurs, and  $\mathbf{A}$  is converted into ammonium salt  $\mathbf{C}$ ;  $\mathbf{C}$  is reduced by hydrogen at  $1000^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to obtain  $\mathbf{M}$  element. If  $\mathbf{C}$  is directly heated to decompose, then oxide  $\mathbf{D}$  is obtained.

Adding  $6\mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{L}$  hydrochloric acid to A and adding potassium iodide for reduction, a green paramagnetic potassium salt E can be prepared, its magnetic moment  $\mu = 3.25\mu_{\mathrm{B}}$  ; adding  $\mathrm{AgNO_3}$  to the saturated solution of E yields an orange precipitate F; F decomposes upon heating to give silver chloride and two other chlorides G,H; G,H are both chlorine-bridged polymers, where G is a dimer and the metal is six-coordinate; H is a trimer, containing metal-metal bonds, and the ratio of bridging chlorine to terminal chlorine is  $1:2$  . G can also be prepared by directly heating M element with chlorine gas to  $400^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  ; G undergoes a disproportionation reaction with water to obtain a hydrate precipitate of D and a solution of A (the molar ratio of the products is  $2:1$  ).

The following statement is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. Element M has an even number of unpaired electrons in its ground state.  
C. Element M does not contain common radioactive nuclides.  
D. The mass fraction of M in D is  $79.5\%$  
E. The oxidation state of  $\mathbf{M}$  in  $\mathbf{E}$  is different from that of  $\mathbf{D}$ .  
F. The mass fraction of M in F is  $34.2\%$

G. The mass fraction of  $\mathbf{M}$  in  $\mathbf{G}$  is greater than  $\mathbf{H}$  
H. The chemical equation for the reduction of  $\mathbf{A}$  to obtain  $\mathbf{E}$ , when the coefficients are balanced to the simplest integer ratio, has a sum of 37 for the coefficients of all substances.

# Answer

Correct Answer: H

# Detailed Explanation

The flue gas obtained from roasting molybdenite can yield the sixth-period  $\mathbf{M}$ , indicating that the position of  $\mathbf{M}$  on the periodic table is close to Mo, and it may be Ta, W, Re.

After roasting, elements are generally oxidized to their highest valence state. The volatile oxide  $\mathbf{N}$  of  $\mathbf{M}$  may be  $\mathrm{Ta_2O_5,WO_3,Re_2O_7}$ ; based on the mass fraction of  $76.9\%$ ,  $\mathbf{N}$  can be calculated to be  $\mathrm{Re_2O_7}$ . Therefore, element  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $\mathrm{Re}$ .
