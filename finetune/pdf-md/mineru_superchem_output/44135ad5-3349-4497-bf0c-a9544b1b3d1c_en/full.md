# Question

In general atomic fullerene inclusion compounds, atoms can either form bonds with the fullerene or have no bonding interaction with the fullerene. Reacting  $\mathrm{K}_3\mathrm{As}_7$ ,  $\mathrm{Ni}(\mathrm{COD})_2$ ,  $\mathrm{Bu}_4\mathrm{PBr}$  in ethylenediamine solution yields a precipitate of tetrabutylphosphonium salt. Single-crystal diffraction confirms that the structure of the anion in this salt can be represented as  $\left[\mathrm{As@Ni_m@As_n}\right]^{3-}$ . (The structure before the @ symbol is "included" within the structure after the symbol.) This anion has the same symmetry as a regular icosahedron and consists solely of As and Ni elements, with a nickel mass fraction of  $30.92\%$ . Treating the outermost As<sub>n</sub> layer as an independent molecule,

Given: the covalent radius of arsenic is  $119~\mathrm{pm}$ , and its van der Waals radius is  $185~\mathrm{pm}$ ; the nickel atoms in the  $\mathrm{Ni}_{\mathrm{m}}$  cluster are in contact with each other, and the distance between the farthest-spaced nickel atoms is  $516~\mathrm{pm}$ , while the van der Waals radius of Ni is  $163~\mathrm{pm}$ ; the As-As distance in  $\mathrm{As}_{\mathrm{n}}$  is  $275.2~\mathrm{pm}$ , and the distance between the farthest-spaced arsenic atoms is  $771.2~\mathrm{pm}$ .

Calculate the distance between each vertex Ni atom in the  $\mathrm{Ni}_{\mathrm{m}}$  cluster and the nearest As atom on the  $\mathrm{As}_{\mathrm{n}}$  layer, and select the closest answer.

A.  $239 \mathrm{pm}$  
B.  $234 \mathrm{pm}$  
C.  $306 \mathrm{pm}$  
D.  $307 \mathrm{pm}$  
E.  $613 \mathrm{pm}$  
F.  $163 \mathrm{pm}$

G.  $185 \mathrm{pm}$  
H.  $239 \mathrm{pm}$  
I. 246 pm

# Answer

Correct Answer: H
