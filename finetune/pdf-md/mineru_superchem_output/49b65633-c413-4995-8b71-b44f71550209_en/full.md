# Question

The element  $\mathbf{A}$  exists as a gray simple substance with relatively active chemical properties. Mixing the simple substance of  $\mathbf{A}$  with a binary compound  $\mathbf{B}$  containing  $35.77\%$  of  $\mathbf{A}$  in a sealed tube at a certain stoichiometric ratio and heating to  $393\mathrm{K}$  yields a brownish-red oily liquid  $\mathbf{C}$  containing  $69.01\%$  of  $\mathbf{A}$ ;  $\mathbf{B}$  can be prepared by passing  $\mathrm{Cl}_2$  into a solution of  $\mathbf{C}$  in  $\mathrm{CCl}_4$ . Anhydrous liquid ammonia can react with  $\mathbf{C}$  to produce the simple substance of  $\mathbf{A}$ , while an ammonia solution in diethyl ether reacts with  $\mathbf{C}$  at  $193\mathrm{K}$  to produce an explosive substance  $\mathbf{D}$  with an  $\mathbf{A}$  content of  $84.93\%$ .  $\mathbf{E}$ , which is isoelectronic with  $\mathbf{C}$  in a broader sense, can also react with  $\mathrm{NH}_3$  to produce a similarly explosive  $\mathbf{F}$  with a nitrogen content of  $30.40\%$ .

Reacting D with B in  $\mathrm{SOCl}_2$  at  $313\mathrm{K}$  yields a yellow precipitate. After washing and recrystallization, a yellow compound G is obtained. Its cation contains only one A and the A content in the cation is  $36.35\%$ , and the nitrogen content is  $19.35\%$ ; the overall A content in the entire compound is  $32.62\%$ .

Which of the following statements is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. A atomic ground state only has one single electron  
C. B and E chemical formulas have the same number of atoms.  
D. B and D have the same number of atoms in their chemical formulas.  
E. D and F have different numbers of atoms in their chemical formulas.  
F.  $\mathbf{G}$  is a 1:1 type ionic compound  
G. G anion possesses a tetrahedral configuration.

H. The chemical formula of  $\mathbf{G}$  contains 18 atoms after removing atom A.

# Answer

Correct Answer: H
