# Question

1. Under anaerobic conditions, a  $\mathrm{Cu}^{2+}$  containing solution can act as an oxidizing agent to react with pyrite, and the product contains sulfur in two oxidation states: -2 and +6; the oxidation state of iron remains unchanged.  
2.  $\mathrm{LiFePO_4}$  is prepared by mixing and grinding iron phosphate, lithium carbonate, and oxalic acid dihydrate, followed by high-temperature calcination.  
3. Under weakly acidic conditions, reacting  $\mathrm{LiFePO_4}$  with hydrogen peroxide aqueous solution can make all the lithium enter the aqueous solution, while iron and phosphorus remain in the solid phase.  
4. Elemental mercury, alum, and NaCl are mixed and heated in air to volatilize a compound containing mercury in the +1 oxidation state.  
5. In acidic aqueous solution, potassium hexathiocyanatochrome(III) is oxidized with excess  $\mathrm{I}_2$ , and carbon and nitrogen are only converted to ICN; no element is produced in the reaction.

Write out the equations of the above 5 reactions (1, 3, and 5 are ionic equations), requiring the coefficients of each substance to be the simplest integer ratio. Which of the following options is correct?

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The ionic equation for reaction 1, where the sum of the product coefficients is less than the sum of the reactant coefficients.  
C. The chemical equation for reaction 2, where the sum of the product coefficients is not a multiple of the sum of the reactant coefficients.  
D. The ionic equation for reaction 3, the sum of coefficients of all species is a composite number.  
E. The chemical equation for reaction 4, where the sum of the coefficients of all species is greater than 100.

F. The ionic equation for reaction 5, the sum of the coefficients of all species is greater than 150.

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

Reaction 1, the iron in pyrite is  $+2$  valence, the unchanged oxidation state indicates the generation of  $\mathrm{Fe}^{2+}$ , the  $+6$  valence sulfur should be sulfate, and the  $-2$  valence sulfur should form  $\mathrm{Cu}_2\mathrm{S}$  with the reduced copper ions. Thus, the balanced equation can be written as:

$$

$$
