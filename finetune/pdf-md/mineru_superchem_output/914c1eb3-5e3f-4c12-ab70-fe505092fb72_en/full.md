# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

The silyl group at the  $\beta$  position of an alkene can enhance the nucleophilicity of the carbon-carbon double bond through the hyperconjugation effect of the carbon-silicon single bond on the carbon-carbon double bond. Alkenyl sulfoxide A first generates a sulfonium ion under the action of trifluoroacetic anhydride, and then is attacked by the substrate alkene at the terminal carbon-carbon double bond conjugated to the sulfonium ion via an  $\mathrm{S_N2^{\prime}}$  mechanism, leaving the silyl group to obtain a carbon-carbon double bond at the end of the chain, as shown in the following structure:

$\mathrm{C = CCC(C1 = [S + ]CCCS1)C(F)(F)F}$
