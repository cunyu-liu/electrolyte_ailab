# Question

The yellow solid is a  $\mathrm{Cu(I)}$  polynuclear cluster with the chemical formula  $\left[\mathrm{Cu}_{8} \mathrm{H}\left(\mathrm{S}_{2} \mathrm{CNR}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{x}}\right] \left(\mathrm{PF}_{6}\right)_{\mathrm{y}}$ , and the hydride ion is encapsulated in the cluster; elemental analysis shows that the mass fractions of each element in the cluster are: C, 23.24%; H, 4.14%; N, 5.64%

The following statement that is correct is:

1. R is methyl  
2. In the cation, there are two kinds of environments for Cu (ratio is 1:1), one of which Cu is connected to 6 Cu, and the other is connected to 3 Cu. Then Cu constitutes a tetracapped tetrahedron.

# Answer

Correct Answer: K

# Detailed Explanation

The number of carbon atoms in the ligand is:  $14.01 / 5.64\% \times 23.24\% / 12.01 = 4.81$ , R contains 2 carbons

The number of hydrogen atoms in the ligand is much larger than that outside the ligand, approximately:  $14.01 / 5.64\% \times 4.14\% / 1.008 = 10.2$ , that is, R contains 5 hydrogens

So  $\mathrm{R} = \mathrm{Et}$ , statement 1 is incorrect
