# Question

A is an important compound of the element M with a point group of  $D_{2d}$ , where the mass fraction of M is  $70.0\%$ . The elemental form of M can be obtained by the fusion reaction of A with tin. Heating the elemental M in air yields B. Refluxing B with  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$  under chlorine gas produces the oily liquid C. If B is mixed with Zn and hydrochloric acid, one of the products reacts with silver nitrate solution to form a black precipitate D. Please determine which of the following options is correct.

A. Element  $\mathbf{M}$  is an element from the fifth period.  
B. There are 5 atoms in compound A  
C. The mass fraction of oxygen in compound B is  $43.66\%$ .  
D. The molar mass of compound C is approximately  $181.27 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{mol}$  
E. The sum of the molar masses of  $\mathbf{M}$  and  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $304.9\mathrm{g / mol}$  
F. In the reaction equation where  $\mathbf{B}$  is heated under reflux with  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$  under chlorine gas, there are three products.

# Answer

Correct Answer: D

# Detailed Explanation

Determine the element  $\mathbf{M}$ :

1. Heating the simple substance  $\mathbf{M}$  in air can yield  $\mathbf{B}$ , then  $\mathbf{B}$  should be the oxide of  $\mathbf{M}$ .  
2. Mixing B with Zn and hydrochloric acid, one of the products reacts with silver nitrate solution, and the formation of a black precipitate D can be observed. Zn-HCl is a reducing system, then the oxide B will be reduced when mixed with Zn and hydrochloric acid, and this product reacts with silver nitrate solution, and the formation of a black precipitate D can be observed. If no redox reaction occurs, the black precipitate may be  $\mathrm{Ag_2S}$  ( $\mathrm{Ag_2O}$  cannot be produced in this way, and  $\mathrm{Ag_2Se}$  is a gray crystalline powder); if a redox reaction occurs, the black precipitate may be elemental  $\mathrm{Ag}$ , and B may be the reducing gas  $\mathrm{PH_3}$ ,  $\mathrm{AsH_3}$ ,  $\mathrm{SbH_3}$ .  
3. Heating  $\mathbf{B}$  with  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$  under chlorine gas reflux can obtain an oily liquid  $\mathbf{C}$ . This information can rule out the possibility that  $\mathbf{M}$  is sulfur, so the black precipitate  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{Ag}$ . Considering that  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$  is generally used as a chlorinating reagent, and the chloride is liquid, it can be locked that  $\mathbf{M}$  is one of P, As, and Sb.

4. The point group  $D_{2d}$  is common in tetrahedral twisted structures. Let  $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{M}\mathbf{X}_{\mathbf{n}}$ , the molar mass of  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $M(\mathbf{M})$ , and the molar mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $M(\mathbf{X})$ , then the mass fraction expression is:  $w = \frac{M(\mathbf{M})}{M(\mathbf{M}) + nM(\mathbf{X})}$
