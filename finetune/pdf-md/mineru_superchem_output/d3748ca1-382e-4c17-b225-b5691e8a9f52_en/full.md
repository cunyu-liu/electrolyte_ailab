# Question

A is a common element. B is a disodium salt of a common oxyacid of A, containing water of crystallization, and the formula weight of B does not exceed 300. When B is decomposed by heating, it loses  $36.3\%$  of its weight, corresponding to the loss of all water of crystallization. When an appropriate amount of  $Cl_2$  is passed into the NaOH solution of B, concentrated and crystallized, a sodium salt crystal C is obtained. If excess  $Cl_2$  is passed into the solution and then concentrated, a crystal D containing A is obtained. C loses  $14.9\%$  of its weight at  $130^{\circ}C$  and transforms into E. The anion of E has two lengths of covalent bonds. E is further heated to  $300^{\circ}C$  and transforms into F, and the anion of F has only one length of covalent bond.

The following are several propositions about the above elements and substances:

1. The element  $\mathbf{A}$  exists as a liquid in its elemental form at room temperature and pressure.  
2. B has strong oxidizing properties.  
3. When  $\mathbf{B}$  is converted to  $\mathbf{D}$ , the theoretical molar ratio of  $\mathbf{B}$  to  $Cl_{2}$  is  $1:1$ .  
4. The most stable conformation of the anion of  $\mathbf{C}$  does not have a center of symmetry.  
5. The process of converting  $\mathbf{E}$  to  $\mathbf{F}$  produces a colorless and odorless gas.  
6. The most stable conformation of the anion in  $\mathbf{F}$  contains a center of symmetry.  
7. In the anions of  $\mathbf{B},\mathbf{C},\mathbf{D},\mathbf{F}$  respectively, all elements have only one chemical environment.  
8. The formula weight of the anion in  $\mathbf{B}$  is greater than the formula weight of the anion in  $\mathbf{D}$ .

Given that the number of true propositions is not a prime number, select the correct option below.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The number of incorrect descriptions is even.

C. The product of the indices of all error descriptions does not exceed 2000  
D. The sum of the indices of all correctly described items does not exceed 8.  
E. Among the indices of correct descriptions, there are more odd numbers than even numbers.  
F. The difference between the largest index of incorrect descriptions and the smallest index of correct descriptions is an even number.

# Answer

Correct Answer: D

# Detailed Explanation

Let the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{B}$  be  $N a_{2} \mathbf{A}_{x} O_{y} \cdot z H_{2} O$ , then the formula weight

$$
M(\mathbf{B}) = 18.02z / 36.3\% = 49.6z, M(\mathbf{A}_xO_y) = 31.6z - 46.
$$

# CHECKPOINT

1.5 PTS

$$
M (\mathbf {A} _ {x} O _ {y}) = 3 1. 6 z - 4 6
$$

Since  $M(\mathbf{B}) < 300$ , then  $2 \leq z \leq 6$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$$
2 \leq z \leq 6
$$

Trying in sequence, we find that when  $z = 4$ ,  $\mathbf{B}$  has a reasonable solution  $Na_{2}SO_{3} \cdot 4H_{2}O$ , and when  $z = 5$ , it has a reasonable solution  $Na_{2}S_{2}O_{3} \cdot 5H_{2}O$ . There are no other reasonable solutions.

Regardless of which of the above two cases  $\mathbf{B}$  is, it can be known that  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $Na_2SO_4\cdot 10H_2O$ . The weight loss of  $\mathbf{C}$  to  $\mathbf{E}$  is also a water loss process. Combined with calculation,  $\mathbf{E}$  is  $Na_2S_2O_6$ , so  $\mathbf{C}$  is  $Na_2S_2O_6\cdot 2H_2O$ .  $\mathbf{F}$  is  $Na_2SO_4$ .

If  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $Na_2S_2O_3\cdot 5H_2O$ , then only 8 is the correct description. Therefore,  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $Na_2S_2O_3\cdot 5H_2O$ .
