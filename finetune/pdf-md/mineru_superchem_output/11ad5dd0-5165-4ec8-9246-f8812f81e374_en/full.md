# Question

The organic extractant HA can extract  $\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$  from an aqueous solution to the organic phase. HA is almost insoluble in water, but exists in the organic phase only as a dimer of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{A}_2$ . To determine the specific form of this cerium complex in the organic phase, the following experiment was performed:

A tracer amount of  $^{144}\mathrm{Ce}(\mathrm{NO}_3)_3$  was dissolved in water and acidified with a small amount of nitric acid to prepare an aqueous phase containing  $^{144}\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$ . 2.50 mL of the above aqueous phase and 2.50 mL of the organic phase containing HA were taken and mixed thoroughly for extraction at room temperature. After equilibrium was reached, 1.00 mL each of the aqueous phase and the organic phase were taken, and the ratio of their radioactivity intensities was measured. Another portion of the equilibrated aqueous phase was taken and its pH value was measured. When the initial concentration of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{A}_2$  is  $c_0(\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{A}_2) = 0.240 \, \mathrm{mol/L}$ , the ratio of radioactivity intensity of the organic phase to the aqueous phase at equilibrium was measured to be 2929:416, and the aqueous phase  $[\mathrm{H}^+] = 3.39 \times 10^{-2} \, \mathrm{mol/L}$ . When  $c_0(\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{A}_2) = 0.120 \, \mathrm{mol/L}$ , the ratio of radioactivity intensity of the organic phase to the aqueous phase at equilibrium was measured to be 1542:1682, and the aqueous phase  $[\mathrm{H}^+] = 3.42 \times 10^{-2} \, \mathrm{mol/L}$ . It is known that the organic phase does not contain nitrate ions.

Please determine the equilibrium constant of the extraction reaction through calculation and reasoning, and select the correct equilibrium constant from the following options:

(Hydrolysis of  $\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$  and complexation of  $\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$  with  $\mathrm{NO}_3^-$  are not considered. A calculation error within  $5\%$  is considered correct, and take two significant figures).

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. 0.010  
C. 0.014  
D. 0.025

E. 0.30  
F. 1.5  
G. 0.52  
H. 0.030  
1. 0.0022

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

The extracted substance is electrically neutral because there are no nitrate radicals, and  $\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$  is coordinated with at least three  $\mathrm{A}^{-}$ ions.

The extracted substance is electrically neutral because there are no nitrate radicals, and  $\mathrm{Ce}^{3+}$  is coordinated with at least three  $\mathrm{A}^{-}$ ions.

Consider the possible extraction reaction equation:

$$
\mathrm {C e} ^ {3 +} + 1. 5 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {A} _ {2} = \mathrm {C e} (\mathrm {A}) _ {3} + 3 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$

From the two extraction experiments, the ratio of radiation intensities is the ratio of  $\mathrm{Ce(III)}$  concentrations, so the equilibrium constant can be listed:

$$
\mathrm {K} = \frac {[ \mathrm {H} ^ {+} ] ^ {3} [ \mathrm {C e (A)} _ {3} ]}{[ \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {A} _ {2} ] ^ {1 . 5} [ \mathrm {C e} ^ {3 +} ]}
$$
