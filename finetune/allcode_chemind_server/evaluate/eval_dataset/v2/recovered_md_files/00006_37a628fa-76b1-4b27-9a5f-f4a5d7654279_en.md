# Question

In the ion  $\left[\mathrm{Fe}_{5} \mathrm{O}\left(\mathrm{CH}_{3} \mathrm{COO}\right)_{12}\right]^{+}$ , iron exhibits two coordination numbers and two chemical environments, with a ratio of  $2:3$ . The acetate groups act as bridging or monodentate ligands.

Among the following statements about the structure of this compound, identify the correct one.

A. In this complex, the O monoatomic ligand is connected to four Fe atoms.  
B. In this complex, there is a 5-coordinated Fe.  
C. In this complex, a five-membered ring structure is present.  
D. In this complex, the bridging acetate ligand has only one chemical environment.  
E. There are no atoms at the geometric center of this complex ion.  
F. In this complex, there exists a Y-shaped structure formed with the participation of Fe atoms, which is coplanar or nearly coplanar.  
G. In this complex, if all freely rotatable parts connected by single bonds are considered spherical and minor deviations are neglected, the order of the point group to which the complex belongs is 3.  
H. In this complex, the monodentate acetate ligand and the monatomic O ligand can occupy adjacent positions in the coordination polyhedron of the Fe atom.  
1. None of the above options is correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

Examining the composition of  $\left[\mathrm{Fe}_{5} \mathrm{O}\left(\mathrm{CH}_{3} \mathrm{COO}\right)_{12}\right]^{+}$ , there is one single-atom O ligand, which is preferentially placed at the center. The five Fe atoms are divided into two chemical environments in a 2:3 ratio, likely forming a  $\mathrm{Fe}_{5} \mathrm{O}$  trigonal bipyramidal structure around O, where two Fe atoms occupy axial positions and three Fe atoms occupy equatorial positions.

Among the 12 acetate ligands, there are two coordination modes: monodentate and bridging. Due to the core Fe - O - Fe structure, the acetate ligands can form stable six-membered rings when bridging. Therefore, when arranging the acetate ligands, bridging should be prioritized.

If each FeFe pair is bridged by one acetate, nine acetates are consumed, leaving three terminal acetates, which can be placed on the equatorial Fe atoms.
