# Question

It is known that  $\mathbf{M}$  and  $\mathbf{N}$  are adjacent elements in the periodic table with increasing atomic numbers, and they can form elemental gases  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$ , respectively. At  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , heating a mixture of gold, elemental gas  $\mathbf{A}$ , and elemental gas  $\mathbf{B}$  yields a 1:1 type salt  $\mathbf{X}$ . The cation of  $\mathbf{X}$  is a dinuclear cation exhibiting paramagnetism, while the anion is a diamagnetic octahedral ion. When  $\mathbf{X}$  is heated under vacuum conditions, it decomposes into  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $\mathbf{B}$ , and  $\mathbf{C}$ , where  $\mathbf{C}$  is a dinuclear complex in which the oxidation state of gold is the same as in  $\mathbf{X}$ .

Based on the above information, select the correct option.

A. The bond order of the  $\mathbf{X}$  cation is 1.5  
B. The sum of the atomic numbers of  $\mathbf{M}$  and  $\mathbf{N}$  is 19.  
C. According to the crystal field theory, the electron configuration of the central atom in the  $\mathbf{X}$  anion is  $t_{2g}^{4}e_{g}^{2}$ .  
D. The molecular point group of the C molecule is  $D_{2d}$  
E. The relative molecular mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  molecule is  $343g / mol$  
F. In the reaction equation for the high-temperature decomposition of  $\mathbf{X}$ , assuming the equation coefficients have been reduced to their simplest form, the sum of the coefficients of reactants and products is 5.  
G. None of the above options is correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

Considering the chemical properties of gold, it can be inferred that these two gases are highly oxidizing. It can be speculated that  $\mathbf{N}$  is the element  $F$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $O_2$ , and  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $F_2$ . The sum of the atomic numbers of these two elements is 17, making option B incorrect.
