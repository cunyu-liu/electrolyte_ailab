# Question

Oxalate is a good ligand and can form complexes with transition metals. Since oxalate solutions are strongly alkaline, metal ions that are prone to hydrolysis, such as  $\mathrm{Fe}^{3+}$ ,  $\mathrm{Al}^{3+}$ , need to be converted to hydroxides first, then dissolved in oxalic acid, and neutralized with a base to obtain the corresponding oxalate complexes. This lengthy procedure is time-consuming, it is difficult to filter the hydroxides, the operation results in a lot of loss, and the yield is often not ideal.

Someone proposed the following experimental scheme to directly prepare the complex of oxalate with ferric iron: mixing  $20\mathrm{mL}3\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  potassium oxalate hot solution and  $8\mathrm{mL}1.5\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  ferric chloride solution, cooling to room temperature to obtain green needle-shaped crystals  $\mathrm{K_3Fe(C_2O_4)_3\cdot 3H_2O}$ , and measuring the volume of the solution at this time as  $30\mathrm{mL}$ . Now we theoretically calculate the feasibility of this scheme.

The secondary hydrolysis of oxalate is ignored in the calculation of this problem.

Which of the following options regarding this experimental scheme is correct (calculation error within  $1\%$  is considered correct):

A. When mixing potassium oxalate solution and ferric chloride solution, one can adopt the method of slowly and gradually adding the potassium oxalate solution to the ferric chloride solution, or the method of rapidly adding the ferric chloride solution to the potassium oxalate solution.  
B. At room temperature, the expected yield of this experimental protocol is approximately  $5.9 \mathrm{~g}$ .  
C. Assuming iron hydroxide is not formed, the iron ion concentration is approximately  $10^{-10} \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  when the mixture is cooled to room temperature.

D. Assuming that iron hydroxide is not formed, when the mixture is cooled to room temperature, the concentration of hydrogen oxalate ion is approximately  $10^{-10} \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$ .  
E. The mass of iron-containing species precipitate, excluding the product, generated by this experimental protocol is approximately  $0.3 \mathrm{mg}$ .  
F. This experimental protocol is completely infeasible.  
G. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

In this experiment, it is necessary to ensure that ferric ions do not hydrolyze to form ferric hydroxide, which would affect the yield of potassium ferrioxalate; therefore, it is necessary to ensure that oxalate is in excess or locally in excess to avoid local overconcentration of ferric ions and hydrolysis; therefore, the ferric solution needs to be slowly dripped into the oxalate solution, or the oxalate solution needs to be quickly dripped into the ferric solution, so option A is incorrect.
