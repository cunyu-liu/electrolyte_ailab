# Question

Dissolving  $S$  mol  $Ag_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  in  $1L$  of pure water results in saturation, meaning there is no precipitate, and the solution volume remains unchanged. The following data are known (activity coefficients are not considered):

$$
K _ {s p} \left(A g _ {2} C r _ {2} O _ {7}\right) = 1. 9 9 \times 1 0 ^ {- 7}
$$

$$
K _ {s p} \left(A g _ {2} C r O _ {4}\right) = 1. 1 2 \times 1 0 ^ {- 1 2}
$$

$$
K _ {s p} (A g O H) = 2. 0 1 \times 1 0 ^ {- 8}
$$

$$
K _ {a} \left(H C r O _ {4} ^ {-}\right) = 3. 2 3 \times 1 0 ^ {- 7}
$$

$$
2 H C r O _ {4} ^ {-} = C r _ {2} O _ {7} ^ {2 -} + H _ {2} O, K _ {f} = 1. 1 4 \times 1 0 ^ {2}
$$

$H_{2}CrO_{4}$  is considered a strong acid upon its first ionization and a weak acid upon its second ionization;  $H_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  is considered a diprotic strong acid.

Select the correct option(s) below.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. at equilibrium,  $4.20 \leq pH \leq 4.50$  
C. At equilibrium,  $0.95 \leq [Ag^{+}]^{2}[Cr_{2}O_{7}^{2-}] / K_{sp}(Ag_{2}CrO_{4}) \leq 1.05$  (Note: Not a typo, the denominator is indeed  $Ag_{2}CrO_{4}$ )  
D. The mass  $m(g)$  of  $Ag_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  added at exact saturation satisfies  $5.5 \times 10^{-2} \leq m \leq 5.8 \times 10^{-2}$  
E. Adding  $2S$  mol of  $Ag_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  to  $1L$  of pure water, at equilibrium the precipitate contains both  $Ag_{2}CrO_{4}$  and  $Ag_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$ .  
F. Adding  $S \, mol \, Ag_{2}CrO_{4}$  to  $1 \, L$  of pure water, it can still be completely dissolved.

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

At equilibrium, the solution is obviously weakly acidic. We can first not consider  $AgOH$  saturation. Then either  $Ag_2Cr_2O_7$  or  $Ag_2CrO_4$  is saturated, that is, one of the following two equations holds as an equality, and the other equation holds as a less than:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ A g ^ {+} \right] ^ {2} \left[ C r _ {2} O _ {7} ^ {2 -} \right] = K _ {s p} \left(A g _ {2} C r _ {2} O _ {7}\right) (\text {E q u a t i o n 1}) \\ \left[ A g ^ {+} \right] ^ {2} \left[ C r O _ {4} ^ {2 -} \right] = K _ {s p} \left(A g _ {2} C r O _ {4}\right) (\text {E q u a t i o n 2}) \\ \end{array}
$$

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

One of  $[Ag^{+}]^{2}[Cr_{2}O_{7}^{2-}] = K_{sp}(Ag_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7})$  and  $[Ag^{+}]^{2}[CrO_{4}^{2-}] = K_{sp}(Ag_{2}CrO_{4})$  holds, and the other equation holds as a less than

When completely dissolved, according to the conservation of atoms  $[Ag] = [Cr]$ , we have

$$
\left[ A g ^ {+} \right] = 2 \left[ C r _ {2} O _ {7} ^ {2 -} \right] + \left[ H C r O _ {4} ^ {-} \right] + \left[ C r O _ {4} ^ {2 -} \right] \text {(E q u a t i o n 3)}
$$

$$

$$

According to charge conservation, we have

$$
\left[ A g ^ {+} \right] + \left[ H ^ {+} \right] = 2 \left[ C r _ {2} O _ {7} ^ {2 -} \right] + \left[ H C r O _ {4} ^ {-} \right] + 2 \left[ C r O _ {4} ^ {2 -} \right] + \left[ O H ^ {-} \right] (\text {E q u a t i o n 4 ,} \left[ O H ^ {-} \right] \text {c a n a l s o b e i g n o r e d})
$$
