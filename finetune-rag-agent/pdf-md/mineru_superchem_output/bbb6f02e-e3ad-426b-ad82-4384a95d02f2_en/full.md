# Question

The following five chemical processes exist:

1. Liquid  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  is a common non-aqueous solvent. Sodium azide can undergo a "strong base displacing a weak base" reaction in this solvent, similar to that of sodium ethoxide in water.  
2. Determination of formaldehyde content in solution using iodine in sodium hydroxide alkaline conditions.  
3. Silver nitrate reacts with a halogen element in potassium chloride solution to produce a mononuclear diamagnetic substance containing  $48.39\%$  silver.  
4. Sodium reacts with molten sodium hydroxide to obtain a strong base commonly used in organic synthesis.  
5. Industrially,  $\mathrm{PH}_3$  is prepared by the disproportionation reaction of white phosphorus in alkaline sodium hydroxide solution. In addition, another product is obtained, with a ratio to  $\mathrm{PH}_3$  of 3:1.

For the above processes, the sum of the simplest integer ratios of the coefficients of the balanced chemical equations are a, b, c, d, and e, respectively. Which of the following statements is correct for these five numbers?

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. Odd numbers have 3.  
C. Even numbers have 2.  
D. All five numbers are greater than 5.

E. Prime numbers have 3.

F. All five numbers are less than 10.  
G. The sum of five numbers is 40.  
H. The smallest number among five numbers is 3.  
1. There exist 2 numbers among 5 numbers that are powers of 2.

# Answer

Correct Answer: I
