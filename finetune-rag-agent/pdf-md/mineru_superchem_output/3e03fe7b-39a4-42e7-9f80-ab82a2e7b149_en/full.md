# Question

Given  $E^{\circ}(\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}/\mathrm{Pb}) = -0.1266 \, \mathrm{V}, E^{\circ}(\mathrm{PbO}_{2}/\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}) = 1.458 \, \mathrm{V}, K_{sp}(\mathrm{PbSO}_{4}) = 1.8 \times 10^{-8},$ $K_{\mathrm{a2}}(\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{SO}_4) = 1.0 \times 10^{-2}$  
Calculate the standard electromotive force of a lead-acid battery and select the closest option.  
A.  $1.5 \mathrm{~V}$  
B.  $1.6 \mathrm{~V}$  
C.  $1.7 \mathrm{~V}$  
D.  $1.8 \mathrm{~V}$  
E.  $1.9 \mathrm{~V}$  
F.  $2.0 \mathrm{~V}$  
G.  $2.1 \mathrm{~V}$  
H.  $2.2 \mathrm{~V}$

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

This is a physical chemistry problem, examining the calculation of the Nernst equation in electrochemistry.

First, write out the half-reactions at the positive and negative electrodes:

Negative electrode:  $\mathrm{Pb(s) + HSO_4^- (aq)\rightarrow PbSO_4(s) + H^+(aq) + 2e^-}$

Positive electrode:  $\mathrm{PbO}_2$  (s) +  $\mathrm{HSO}_4^-$  (aq) +  $3\mathrm{H}^{+}$  (aq) +  $2\mathrm{e}^{-}\rightarrow \mathrm{PbSO}_4$  (s) + 2H2O (l)

First, discuss the negative electrode. Given  $E^{\circ}(\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}/\mathrm{Pb}) = -0.1266 \mathrm{~V}$ , corresponding to the half-reaction equation  $\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}(\mathrm{aq}) + 2\mathrm{e}^{-} = \mathrm{Pb}(\mathrm{s})$ , then  $E^{\circ}(\mathrm{PbSO}_{4}/\mathrm{Pb}) = E^{\circ}(\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}/\mathrm{Pb}) + \frac{RT}{2F}\ln[\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}]$

Now solve for  $\left[\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}\right]$ :

$$
\left[ \mathrm {P b} ^ {2 +} \right] = \frac {K _ {s p} (\mathrm {P b S O} _ {4})}{\left[ \mathrm {S O} _ {4} ^ {2 -} \right]}
$$

Also,  $\left[\mathrm{SO}_4^{2-}\right] = \frac{\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{a2}}\left[\mathrm{HSO}_4^-\right]}{\left[\mathrm{H}^+\right]} = 1.0 \times 10^{-2} \mathrm{~mol/L}$
