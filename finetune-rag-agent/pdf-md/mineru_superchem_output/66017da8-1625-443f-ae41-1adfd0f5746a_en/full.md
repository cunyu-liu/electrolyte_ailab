# Question

A certain compound  $\mathbf{X}$  contains only two elements,  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$ .

Under high pressure,  $\mathbf{X}$  forms an atomic crystal. This crystal is orthorhombic, in which  $\mathbf{A}$  is four-coordinated and  $\mathbf{B}$  is two-coordinated,  $a = 622\mathrm{pm},\mathrm{b} = 435\mathrm{pm},\mathrm{c} = 607\mathrm{pm},\rho = 3.56\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{cm}^{-3},\mathrm{Z} = 8.$

X can form a cubic molecular crystal under low temperature and high pressure,  $Z = 4$ , and the molecules cannot rotate freely.

Under high pressure,  $\mathbf{X}$  can also form a tetragonal molecular crystal with cell parameters  $a = 354\mathrm{pm},c = 413\mathrm{pm},Z = 2$  . In a proper unit cell, if the A atoms are at the vertices of the unit cell, then the coordinates of two B atoms are (0.266, 0.266, 0) and (0.766, 0.234, 0.5), respectively.

Please select all the correct options from the following propositions:

1.  $\mathbf{X}$  molecule has a center of symmetry  
2. The lattice type of the cubic crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  is simple cubic  
3. The tetragonal crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  has a center of symmetry  
4. The tetragonal crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  has a twofold rotation axis passing through the B atoms  
5. The tetragonal crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  does not have a twofold rotation axis not passing through the A atoms

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

First, deduce the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{X}$ :

Unit cell volume  $V = a \times b \times c = 622 \, \mathrm{pm} \times 435 \, \mathrm{pm} \times 607 \, \mathrm{pm} = (1.643 \times 10^{8}) \, \mathrm{pm}^{3} \approx (1.643 \times 10^{-22}) \, \mathrm{cm}^{3}$

Molar mass of  $\mathbf{X}$ $M = \frac{\rho\times V\times N_A}{Z} = \frac{3.56\times 1.643\times 10^{-22}\times 6.022\times 10^{23}}{8}\approx 44.0\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{mol}^{-1}$

Since the total number of chemical bonds is conserved, the coordination number of  $\mathbf{A}$  to  $\mathbf{B}$  is equal to the coordination number of  $\mathbf{B}$  to  $\mathbf{A}$ , hence  $4 \times n_{A} = 2 \times n_{B}$ .

Therefore, the atomic ratio of  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  is 1:2

If it is a face-centered cubic lattice, the spatial orientation of the  $4\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  molecules must be consistent, which will lead to the loss of symmetry of the cubic crystal system. Therefore, under the conditions of the problem, the cubic crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  can only be a simple cubic lattice. Proposition 2 is correct.
