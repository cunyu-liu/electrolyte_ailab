# Question

At  $-78^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ,  $\mathrm{Cl}_2$ ,  $\mathrm{NCl}_3$ , and  $\mathrm{AsF}_5$  react in liquid  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  solvent to produce a salt  $\mathbf{A}$ . Both the cation and anion of  $\mathbf{A}$  exhibit high symmetry, and the molar ratio of  $\mathrm{Cl}_2$  to  $\mathrm{NCl}_3$  in the reaction is 1:2. Upon heating,  $\mathbf{A}$  decomposes without yielding any elemental substances. When  $\mathbf{A}$  reacts with  $\mathrm{CF}_3\mathrm{SCl}$ , it forms salt  $\mathbf{B}$  and several small-molecule binary compounds, with the original  $\mathrm{S}-\mathrm{Cl}$  bond remaining intact. The symmetry of the cation in  $\mathbf{B}$  is identical to that of  $\mathrm{NH}_3$ .

Regarding the above process, which of the following statements is incorrect?

A. The anions of  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  are identical, and both belong to the  $\mathrm{O_h}$  point group.  
B. The cation of  $\mathbf{A}$  belongs to the  $\mathrm{T_d}$  point group.  
C. During the generation of  $\mathbf{A}$ , the product of the simplest integer ratio of the balanced chemical equation coefficients is 12.  
D. The gas produced during the decomposition of A corrodes glass containers in a moist state, so it is necessary to ensure the experiment is strictly anhydrous.  
E. During the generation of  $\mathbf{B}$ , the cations in  $\mathbf{B}$  and one of the products are isoelectronic.  
F. During the generation of  $\mathbf{B}$ , the sum of the simplest integer ratio of coefficients in the balanced chemical equation is 8.  
G. All of the above statements are correct  
H. All other statements are incorrect

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

This is an inorganic compound inference problem. The question states that "both the cation and anion of  $\mathbf{A}$  possess high symmetry." Considering that the only reactant capable of forming the anion is  $\mathrm{AsF}_5$ , the anion should be  $\mathrm{AsF}_6^-$ . Since a reaction must occur, the cation should not contain As. Therefore, the reactant involved in forming the cation is  $\mathrm{NCl}_3$ , and the resulting cation should be  $\mathrm{NCl}_4^+$ .
