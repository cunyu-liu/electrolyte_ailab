# Question

$\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  emits a popping sound when it reacts with  $\mathrm{CS}_2$ . To investigate the products of this reaction, an interest group conducted the following experiment:

A dry and clean test tube was weighed, and its weight was  $31.33\mathrm{g}$ . It was then filled with  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  under normal pressure. A few drops of  $\mathrm{CS}_2$  were added. After the  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  had reacted almost completely, the generated gas was passed sequentially through a. saturated  $\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}$  solution, concentrated sulfuric acid; b.  $\mathrm{I}_2\mathrm{O}_5$  solid; c. saturated  $\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}$  solution, concentrated sulfuric acid. Finally, the volume of the collected gas was similar to the volume of the initial  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  gas in the test tube. It is known that the mass increase in a. is approximately 4 times the mass decrease in b. After the reaction is complete, the unreacted  $\mathrm{CS}_2$  in the test tube is removed and weighed, and the weight is  $31.70\mathrm{g}$ .

It is known that there are 4 kinds of molecules in the products. According to the above information, write the equation for the reaction, paying attention to the actual existing forms of each substance's molecules. Regarding the product of the coefficients in the equation, the correct option is:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. 294912  
C. 9  
D. 8  
E. 521420800  
F. 11855241216  
G. 300

H. 16384

# Answer

Correct Answer: B

# Detailed Explanation

First, based on the reactants and experimental phenomena, speculate on the possible products:

The reaction between  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  and  $\mathrm{CS}_2$  is a typical redox reaction. The nitrogen element in  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}$  has oxidizing properties, while the sulfur element in  $\mathrm{CS}_2$  has reducing properties, possibly producing  $\mathrm{N}_2$  and elemental sulfur. The generated gas is passed into a saturated  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$  and concentrated sulfuric acid solution, indicating the generation of acidic gas, possibly  $\mathrm{SO}_2$ . During the reaction,  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  is completely absorbed, and the weight increase corresponds to the mass of  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  in the gas:  $\mathrm{m(SO_2)} = \Delta \mathrm{m(a)}$ .

Passed into b.  $\mathrm{I}_2\mathrm{O}_5$  solid, causing its mass to decrease, indicating that the generated gas also contains reducing gas, and oxygen atoms are transferred to the reducing gas during the reaction with  $\mathrm{I}_2\mathrm{O}_5$ . Considering that  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  has been removed by  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$ , the reducing gas may be CO, and the reaction equation is:

$$
\mathrm {I} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {5} + 5 \mathrm {C O} \rightarrow \mathrm {I} _ {2} + 5 \mathrm {C O} _ {2}
$$

The weight loss is the decrease of oxygen atoms, and the relationship between the mass of CO in the gas and the weight loss is:

$$
\mathrm {m} (\mathrm {C O}) = 2 8. 0 1 \times \frac {\Delta \mathrm {m} (\mathrm {b})}{1 6 . 0 0}
$$

The unbalanced reaction equation can be written first:

$$
\mathrm {x N} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathrm {z C S} _ {2} \rightarrow \mathrm {x N} _ {2} + \mathrm {y S O} _ {2} + \mathrm {z C O} + \mathrm {n S} _ {8}
$$

The weight increase in a. is approximately 4 times the weight loss in b.:

$$
\mathrm {m} (\mathrm {C O}) = 2 8. 0 1 \times \frac {\Delta \mathrm {m} (\mathrm {b})}{1 6 . 0 0} = 2 8. 0 1 \times \frac {\Delta \mathrm {m} (\mathrm {a})}{4 \times 1 6 . 0 0} = 0. 4 3 7 7 \Delta \mathrm {m} (\mathrm {a}) = 0. 4 3 7 7 \mathrm {m} (\mathrm {S O} _ {2})
$$

$$
\mathrm {n (C O)} = \mathrm {m (C O) / M (C O)} = 0. 4 3 7 7 \mathrm {m (S O _ {2}) / M (C O)} = 0. 4 3 7 7 \mathrm {n (S O _ {2})} \times \mathrm {M (S O _ {2}) / M (C O)} = 1. 0 0 1 \times \mathrm {n (S O _ {2})}
$$

That is, the number of moles of  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  and CO in the product are the same,  $y = z$ .
