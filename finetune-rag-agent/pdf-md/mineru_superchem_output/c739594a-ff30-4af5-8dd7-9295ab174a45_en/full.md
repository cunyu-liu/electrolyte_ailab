# Question

It is known that the chemical formula of a cluster compound  $\mathbf{W}$  containing a p-block non-metallic element can be expressed as  $\mathbf{A}_2\mathbf{B}_{14}C l_{14}$ , and its structure contains an electrically neutral fragment of  $\mathbf{B}_{10}$  in the middle. The transition metal  $\mathbf{A}$  is coordinated by 6  $\mathbf{B}$  atoms.

It is known that  $5.346\mathrm{g}$  of the cluster compound  $\mathbf{W}$  can be precisely obtained from  $0.768\mathrm{g}$  of the transition metal element  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $2.688\mathrm{g}$  of the element  $\mathbf{B}$ , and a certain amount of chloride  $\mathbf{C}$  of  $\mathbf{B}$  through a combination reaction at  $250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . If  $\mathbf{W}$  is heated to  $280^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in a hydrogen atmosphere, then  $\mathbf{W}$  will decompose into the element  $\mathbf{B}$ ,  $2.133\mathrm{g}$  of  $\mathbf{D}$ , and  $0.597\mathrm{g}$  of  $\mathbf{E}$ , and  $0.4032\mathrm{L}$  of gas  $\mathbf{F}$  ( $341.1^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, 1$  atm).  $\mathbf{D}$  is a coordination compound of  $\mathbf{A}$ , belonging to the  $\mathrm{D}_{3d}$  point group. After separating the element  $\mathbf{B}$  from the above decomposition products, the remaining solid residue is further heated to a higher temperature to obtain  $1.792\mathrm{g}$  of a ternary compound  $\mathbf{G}$ , and two gases  $\mathbf{C}$  and  $\mathbf{F}$  are produced in a 1:1 ratio.  $\mathbf{C}$  and  $\mathbf{F}$  have the same elemental composition, and the volume of the mixed gas at  $409^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and 1 atm is  $0.224\mathrm{L}$ . The molar mass of  $\mathbf{E}$  is 0.28 times that of  $\mathbf{D}$ . Then the following options are correct.

A. W decomposition yields chloride C of B containing 6 chlorines  
B. The molecular weight of  $\mathbf{F}$  is higher than that of  $\mathbf{C}$  
C. D contains 6 chlorine atoms.  
D.  $\mathbf{G}$  can be represented as  $\mathbf{A}_{2} \mathbf{B}$  
E.  $5.346 \mathrm{~g} \mathrm{W}$  decomposes to produce  $2.04 \mathrm{~g}$  of elemental B  
F. The ratio of  $\mathbf{G}$ ,  $\mathbf{C}$ , and  $\mathbf{F}$  generated by heating after removing  $\mathbf{B}$  is 2:1:1.  
G. The ratio of  $\mathbf{B}$  and  $\mathbf{C}$  generated by the  $\mathbf{W}$  decomposition is 2:1

H. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

Solution process (Keep the original format, only change the uppercase letters to format):

The reaction of synthesizing  $\mathbf{W}$  is a combination reaction. Let the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{C}$  be  $\mathbf{B} C l_{x}$ . We can try to write the equation of the reaction:

$$
2 \mathbf {A} + (1 4 - 1 4 / x) \mathbf {B} + (1 4 / x) \mathbf {B} C l _ {x} \rightarrow \mathbf {A _ {2} B _ {1 4}} C l _ {1 4}
$$

Obviously, the mass of  $\mathbf{C}$  is  $5.346 - 0.768 - 2.688 = 1.890(g)$

So we have:

$$
\frac {2 . 6 8 8}{M (\mathbf {B})} \cdot \frac {1 4}{x} = \frac {1 . 8 9 0}{M (\mathbf {B}) + 3 5 . 4 5 x} \times (1 4 - 1 4 / x)
$$

Simplifying, we get:

$$
M (\mathbf {B}) = \frac {3 5 . 4 5 x}{0 . 7 0 3 (x - 1) - 1}
$$
