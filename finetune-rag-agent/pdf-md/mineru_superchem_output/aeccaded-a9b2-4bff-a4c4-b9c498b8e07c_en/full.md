# Question

We denote the position of the amino acid residue at the  $C$ -terminus of a peptide chain cleaved by a protease as 0, and sequentially forward as -1, -2, -3, and backward as +1, +2, +3, and so on. For example, the substrate recognized by trypsin is a residue at position 0 that is lysine or arginine, and the residue at position +1 is not proline.

Subtilisin is a serine/threonine protease. Subtilisin has the ability to recognize longer substrate series and has certain selectivity for different residues at positions -3 to 0. We denote the substrate pocket recognizing the 0 residue as S1, the pocket for the -1 residue as S2, -2 as S3, and -3 as S4.

In wild-type subtilisin, the S4 pocket residue is Tyr104, the S2 pocket is Asn62, and the S1 pocket is Gly166. Scientists tested the selectivity of wild-type subtilisin for different substrates using a C-terminally substituted tetrapeptide substrate XXXX-p-nitroanilide, and found that the fastest reacting sequence was Ala-Ala-Pro-Phe-p-nitroanilide.

Now we consider a batch of subtilisin mutants:

A. G166D, N62D  
B. G166L, Y104D  
C. N62V, Y104K

And some substrates:

1. AAPF-p-nitroanilide  
2. AAPA-p-nitroanilide  
3. AAPQ-p-nitroanilide  
4. AAPY-p-nitroanilide  
5. DAKF-p-nitroanilide  
6. RAKR-p-nitroanilide

12. AAKR-p-nitroanilide  
13. RGAA-p-nitroanilide  
14. DAHF-p-nitroanilide  
15. RGAG-p-nitroanilide

Then, matching these mutants with the substrates with which they react fastest, which of the following options has the numbers from front to back representing the substrate numbers corresponding to subtilisin A, B, and C, respectively, with completely correct results?

# Answer

Correct Answer: O
