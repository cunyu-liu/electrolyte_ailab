# Question

In 1995, Professor Kim's research group reported the following reaction:

![](images/92d3d9ce18c135dec287297220172c27017d89fb7fe0551f1509a079c7bdbf30.jpg)  
N1(CC1C2=CC=CC=C2)/N=C/C3C(C4=CC=CC=C4)O3 yields C#CC(C1=CC=CC=C1)O under heating conditions

Mechanistic studies have shown that, under heating conditions, the substrate molecule first decomposes to give  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$ , the molecular weight of  $\mathbf{A}$  is approximately 104,  $\mathbf{B}$  undergoes ring-opening to give  $\mathbf{C}$ ,  $\mathbf{C}$  undergoes intramolecular proton transfer to give  $\mathbf{D}$ ,  $\mathbf{D}$  then releases a molecule of gas to give  $\mathbf{E}$ , and  $\mathbf{E}$  undergoes a 1,2-H shift to give the product.

The following statements are made:

1. The degree of unsaturation of  $\mathbf{A}$  is 5  
2. B is a 1,3-dipole  
3. C has a charge-separated structure and the types of atoms bearing the positive and negative formal charges are different  
4. The gas released by  $\mathbf{D}$  is not a common gas in the atmosphere  
5. All atoms in  $\mathbf{E}$  (except hydrogen atoms) satisfy the octet rule

Which of the following options contains the most correct statements?

# Answer

Correct Answer: B

# Detailed Explanation

The 2-phenylaziridine ring in the substrate easily undergoes a ring-opening reaction upon heating, eliminating styrene. The molecular weight of styrene is exactly 104, therefore  $\mathbf{A}$  is styrene.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Elimination of the 2-phenylaziridine ring produces  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$  is styrene, with an unsaturation degree of 5, statement 1 is correct

The structure of the remaining  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $[\mathrm{N - }] = [\mathrm{N + }] = \mathrm{CC1C}(\mathrm{C2} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{C2})\mathrm{O1}$  , which is a 1,3-dipole.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The structure of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $[\mathrm{N - }] = [\mathrm{N + }] = \mathrm{CC1C}(\mathrm{C2} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{C2})\mathrm{O1}$ , which is a 1,3-dipole, statement 2 is correct

B undergoes ring-opening, and the negative charge on the nitrogen attacks and opens the epoxide ring, yielding C : `[O-]C(C1=CC=CC=C1)/C=C\{N+\}#N', with formal charges located on nitrogen and oxygen, respectively.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The structure of C is  $\mathrm{[O - ]C(C1 = CC = CC = C1) / C = C\backslash [N + ]\# N^{\prime}}$  , with formal charges located on nitrogen and oxygen, respectively, statement 3 is correct

C undergoes intramolecular proton transfer to yield D. The oxygen anion can abstract the hydrogen on the carbon atom attached to the diazo group, yielding  $\mathrm{OC}(\mathrm{C}1 = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{C}1) / \mathrm{C} = [\mathrm{C}-]\backslash[\mathrm{N}+] \# \mathrm{N}^{-}$ . Under heating conditions, the diazo group can decompose to produce nitrogen gas, which is the gas produced in the step from D to E.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

D releases nitrogen gas when forming  $\mathbf{E}$ , which is a common gas in the atmosphere, statement 4 is incorrect

After the departure of nitrogen gas, carbene  $\mathbf{E}$  is generated in situ, with the structure  $\mathrm{OC}(\mathrm{C}1 = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{CC} = \mathrm{C}1)\mathrm{C} =$  [C]. Subsequently, a hydrogen migration occurs, with an alkenyl hydrogen migrating to the carbene.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

E contains a carbene, and the carbene carbon atom does not satisfy the octet rule, statement 5 is incorrect.

Therefore, the correct statements are 1, 2, and 3. Choose option B.
