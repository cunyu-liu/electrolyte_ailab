# Question

A is a metallic element, and its powder is grayish-black. The element A reacts with fluorine gas upon heating to generate a fluoride molecule B with an octahedral configuration. B disproportionate in water to produce C and D. C is a dark brown solid with a rutile structure; D is a diamagnetic light yellow solid with a low melting point and is easily volatile. C can also be obtained by heating in air. In potassium hydroxide solution, D reacts with an equimolar amount of potassium nitrite to produce a 2:1 type potassium salt E; E can be heated in nitric acid to obtain D. B reacts with an equimolar amount of D at  $150^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to produce a compound F (containing A  $68.1\%$ ). Structural determination shows that F is a polymer with two chemical environments of A atoms, A1 and A2 (both octahedrally coordinated), and the bond lengths of the chemical bonds they form are as follows:

A1: 170.7pm, 182.0pm, 182.8pm, 185.5pm, 202.9pm, 204.4pm  
A2: 170.6pm, 171.1pm, 171.1pm, 183.4pm, 214.0pm, 214.2pm

Which of the following statements is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The atomic ground state of element  $\mathbf{A}$  has four unpaired electrons.  
C. D is non-toxic  
D. The chemical equation that generates  $\mathbf{E}$ , when the coefficients are balanced to the simplest integer ratio, has a sum of coefficients of all species greater than 10.  
E. The chemical equation for  $\mathbf{E}$  heated in nitric acid, when the coefficients are balanced to the simplest integer ratio, the sum of the reactant coefficients and the sum of the product coefficients are both not prime numbers.

F. B molecule has an octahedral configuration.  
G. According to the information in the question, the magnetic moment of the  $\mathbf{F}$  molecule can be predicted to be almost 0.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G
