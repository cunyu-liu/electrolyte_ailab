# Question

A compound with a garnet crystal structure contains 4 elements, with mass fractions of A  $18.55\%$ , B  $14.52\%$ , C  $5.60\%$ , D  $61.33\%$ , without considering non-stoichiometric compounds. The following descriptions exist:

1. The sum of the outermost electron numbers of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B},\mathbf{C},\mathbf{D}$  is 12.  
2. There are 12 A atoms in a proper unit cell.  
3. There are 16 A atoms in a proper unit cell.  
4. There are 24 C atoms in a proper unit cell.  
5. There are 96 B atoms in a proper unit cell.  
6. According to Pauling's second rule, the charge number of the anion is equal to the sum of the surrounding ionic bond strengths.  
7. There are transition elements among the four elements.  
8. The sum of the outermost electron numbers of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{D}$  is less than 8.  
9. The coordination polyhedra of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B},\mathbf{C},\mathbf{D}$  in the unit cell do not have regular polyhedra.  
10. The molecular weight of this compound is greater than 400.

Which of the following options describes a combination that does not contain incorrect descriptions?

# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

The general chemical formula of garnet is  $\mathrm{X}_3\mathrm{Y}_2\mathrm{Z}_3\mathrm{M}_{12}$ , where  $\mathrm{X},\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{Z}$  are cations, and  $\mathbf{M}$  is an anion. This general formula precisely contains 4 elements, and the ratio of their number of atoms (mole ratio) is fixed at  $3:2:3:12$ .
