# Question

A is a binary ionic compound containing the anion  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$ , which can be synthesized from the corresponding elemental substances under normal conditions via a combination reaction, with a formula weight not exceeding  $80g\cdot mol^{-1}$ .

The quaternary compound  $\mathbf{B}$  is a hydrate of a strongly alkaline ionic compound, with mass fractions of C, H, and O being  $26.51\%$ ,  $12.79\%$ , and  $52.97\%$ , respectively.

When solid  $\mathbf{B}$  reacts with excess solid  $\mathbf{A}$  under vacuum, three products are obtained after extraction and separation with liquid ammonia: a salt  $\mathbf{M}$  containing the anion  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$ , a strongly alkaline ionic compound  $\mathbf{C}$ , and a colorless gas  $\mathbf{D}$ . The anion  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$  is derived from  $\mathbf{D}$  by gaining electrons.

Determine the sum of the stoichiometric coefficients (in their simplest whole-number ratios) for all substances in the overall reaction equation for the formation of compound  $\mathbf{M}$  from compounds  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$ .

A. 63  
B. 4  
C. 20  
D. 11  
E. 12  
F. 8  
G. None of the above options are correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Since compound  $\mathbf{A}$  is a binary ionic compound, it is highly likely that its cation and anion correspond to a single element each. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$  contains only one element, and since  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$  is obtained by the electron gain of the colorless gas  $\mathbf{D}$ ,  $\mathbf{D}$  must be a colorless gaseous elemental substance, either  $O_{2}$  or  $N_{2}$ . As  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$  carries only a single negative charge,  $\mathbf{X}^{-}$  can only be  $O_{2}^{-}$  or  $N_{3}^{-}$ , and the cation must be an alkali metal ion.
