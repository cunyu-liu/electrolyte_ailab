# Question

Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a certain ternary compound  $\mathbf{G}$  containing Be, and  $1.000\mathrm{g}$  of the compound was heated under an inert atmosphere:

- The first weight loss occurred at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , with  $0.789\mathrm{g}$  of solid remaining;  
- The second weight loss occurred at  $150^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , with  $0.683\mathrm{g}$  of solid remaining;  
- The third weight loss occurred at  $400^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , with  $0.149\mathrm{g}$  of solid remaining;  
- Further heating to higher temperatures resulted in continued weight loss, but it was not significant.

It is known that only two small molecules  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  are considered to be produced during the weight loss process ( $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  may continue to decompose later, but this is not relevant to the question). Both  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  are composed of two elements,  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$ . The ratio of the number of  $\mathbf{X}$  to  $\mathbf{Y}$  in  $\mathbf{A}$  is the same as the ratio of the number of  $\mathbf{Y}$  to  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{B}$ , and the mass fraction of element  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{A}$  does not exceed  $3\%$ .

Deduce the element symbols or chemical formulas of  $\mathbf{X}$ ,  $\mathbf{Y}$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $\mathbf{B}$ , and  $\mathbf{G}$ , and select the correct option from the following options.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The mass fraction of  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{B}$  does not exceed 7 times the mass fraction of  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{A}$  
C. Both A and B are highly soluble in water, and their aqueous solutions exhibit alkalinity.  
D. The first two instances of weightlessness produced only the same small molecule, whereas the third instance of weight loss yielded two different small molecules.  
E. The ratio of the numbers of  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  in  $\mathbf{G}$  is  $4:5$  
F. The ratio of the numbers of  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  in  $\mathbf{G}$  is  $5:4$

G. If  $\mathbf{G}$  is an ionic compound, the ratio of anions to cations is  $2:1$  
H. If  $\mathbf{G}$  is an ionic compound, then the cation has two different ligands.  
I. After the third weightlessness, the residual compound is a hydrolyzable binary compound.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

The mass fraction of  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{A}$  does not exceed  $3\%$ . Assuming the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathbf{XY}_{\mathrm{a}}$  (where a may not be an integer), the following relationship can be calculated: (where  $M$  represents atomic/molecular weight)

$$
\frac {M (\mathbf {Y})}{M (\mathbf {X})} > 3 2. 3 3 \times \frac {1}{a}
$$

This indicates that either  $M(\mathbf{Y})$  is much larger than  $M(\mathbf{X})$ , or  $a$  is particularly large. Since  $\mathbf{A}$  is a small molecule, and considering the first three periods of elements, it can be inferred that  $\mathbf{X}$  is most likely  $\mathbf{H}$ , otherwise the formula weight of  $\mathbf{Y}$  would be particularly large, which is obviously not a common small molecule. Therefore, first consider that  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathbf{H}$ .

Next, infer  $\mathbf{Y}$ . Since the first three periods of elements are preferred, it can be known that  $a > 1$ . Since common small molecules are preferred, and considering that this molecule is volatilized during thermogravimetric analysis, exclude  $\mathbf{Y}$  being a metal. Among the hydrides of nonmetals in the first three periods, the common hydrides that satisfy  $a > 1$  and also have both  $\mathbf{X} \mathbf{Y}_{\mathrm{a}}$  and  $\mathbf{X}_{\mathrm{a}} \mathbf{Y}$  are only  $\mathrm{HN}_3$  and  $\mathrm{H}_3\mathrm{N}$ , and  $\mathrm{HO}_2$  and  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ . It is also found that the mass fraction of  $\mathrm{H}$  in  $\mathrm{HO}_2$  is  $3.05\% > 3\%$ , excluding the latter group. Finally, it is deduced that  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathbf{H}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  is  $\mathbf{N}$ .

A is  $\mathrm{HN}_3$ , B is  $\mathrm{NH}_3$

Next, analyze options B and C. Calculate that the mass fraction of H in  $\mathrm{NH}_3$  is approximately 7.6 times the mass fraction of H in  $\mathrm{HN}_3$ , which is greater than 7 times, so B is incorrect. Both  $\mathrm{HN}_3$  and  $\mathrm{NH}_3$  are easily soluble in water, but the former is weakly acidic when dissolved in water, and the latter is alkaline, so C is incorrect.

Next, perform thermogravimetric analysis. The total weight loss is  $0.851\mathrm{g}$ . Let the amount of substance of  $\mathrm{HN}_3$  be  $m\mathrm{mol}$ , the amount of substance of  $\mathrm{NH}_3$  be  $n\mathrm{mol}$ , and the initial amount of substance of the ternary compound be  $b\mathrm{mol}$ . Then the following molecular weight relationship can be obtained:

$$
\frac {0 . 8 5 1}{b} = m \times 4 3. 0 3 + n \times 1 7. 0 1
$$

The mass of the three weight losses are  $0.211\mathrm{g}$ ,  $0.106\mathrm{g}$ , and  $0.534\mathrm{g}$ , respectively. It can be found that the ratio of the first and second weight loss mass is approximately  $2:1$ , which suggests that the two weight losses are the same substance, and the molar ratio is  $2:1$ . Try whether this substance is  $\mathrm{HN}_3$  or  $\mathrm{NH}_3$  respectively, and  $b$  can be calculated, and then substituted into the above formula. After enumeration, it can be found that when the substance is  $\mathrm{NH}_3$ ,  $n = 3$ ,  $m = 2$  is a reasonable solution. Therefore, it is preliminarily determined that this is the inference result.
