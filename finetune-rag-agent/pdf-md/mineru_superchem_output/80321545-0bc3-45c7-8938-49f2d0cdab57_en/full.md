# Question

A certain short-period element  $\mathbf{X}$  is often used in nuclear power reactors. A certain halide of it reacts fully in  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  to obtain a white crystal  $\mathbf{A}$ .

Now, thermogravimetric analysis is performed on A. It is known that there are two weight loss peaks. First, it loses  $58.04\%$  of its weight to obtain a white substance B. Continuing heating, it loses another  $8.52\%$  of its weight to obtain a substance C with a special structure. C contains 4  $\mathrm{C}_3$  axes, all X chemical environments are the same, and the coordination number is 4.

Coincidentally, people have also discovered substances with structures similar to  $\mathbf{C}$  in many transition metals. In a similar particle  $\mathbf{M}$  composed of a certain metal from the fourth period, the ligand contains acetate, and the mass fraction of the metal is  $26.88\%$ . All metal atoms chemical environments are the the same, and all are 6-coordinated. It is known that this particle  $\mathbf{M}$  can be prepared in aqueous solution.

The following statements are correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. X atom's ground state contains two unpaired electrons.  
C. The number of atoms of  $\mathbf{A}$  is greater than C.  
D. The mass fraction of oxygen in  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $39.5 \%$  
E. The mass fraction of oxygen in  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $44.1\%$  
F. The symmetry of  $\mathbf{M}$  is greater than  $\mathbf{C}$  
G. M is a neutral particle

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

Short-period element  $\mathbf{X}$  is often used in nuclear power reactors. In nuclear reactors, Be is often used as a neutron moderator and reflector, and B is often used as a neutron absorber. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  may be B, Be.

If  $\mathbf{X}$  is B,  $\mathbf{A}$  can be written as  $\mathrm{B(NO_3)_3\cdot xN_2O_4}$ . First of all, nitrates of B are not common; secondly, no matter what value x takes, it cannot correspond to this weight loss peak. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  is not B.

Based on the weight loss ratio, it is determined that  $\mathbf{X}$  is not B

If  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathrm{Be}$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$  can be written as  $\mathrm{Be}(\mathrm{NO}_3)_2 \cdot \mathrm{xN}_2\mathrm{O}_4$ . At this time, when  $\mathbf{x}$  is 2, the first step weight loss ratio is  $184 / 317 = 58.0\%$ , which meets the requirements of the question.

Therefore,  $\mathbf{x}$  is 2, and the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{Be(NO_3)_2\cdot 2N_2O_4}$ .  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathrm{Be}$ , option B is incorrect.

C contains  $4\mathrm{C}_3$  axes, all  $\mathbf{X}$  chemical environments are the same, and the coordination number is 4. First, it is judged that the compound has a tetrahedral structure; therefore, it is highly probable that it contains four Be. It is easy to think of the classic structure of Be compounds  $\mathrm{Be_4O(NO_3)_6}$ . According to the weight loss peak, it can be judged that the chemical formula is correct. Therefore, C is  $\mathrm{Be_4O(NO_3)_6}$ . According to the chemical formula, option C is incorrect.
