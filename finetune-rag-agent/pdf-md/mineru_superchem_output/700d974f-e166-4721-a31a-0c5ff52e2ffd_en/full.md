# Question

It is known that for  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{S}$ ,  $K_{\mathrm{a1}} = 1.1 \times 10^{-7}$ ,  $K_{\mathrm{a2}} = 1.3 \times 10^{-13}$ ;  $K_{\mathrm{sp}}(\mathrm{CuS}) = 6.3 \times 10^{-36}$

$$
K _ {\mathrm {s p}} \left(\mathrm {C u} _ {2} \mathrm {S}\right) = 2. 5 \times 1 0 ^ {- 4 8}. \quad \varphi_ {\mathrm {A}} ^ {\theta} (\mathrm {S} / \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {S}) = 0. 1 4 2 \mathrm {V}, \quad \varphi_ {\mathrm {B}} ^ {\theta} (\mathrm {S} / \mathrm {S} ^ {2 -}) = - 0. 4 7 6 \mathrm {V};
$$

$$
\varphi_ {\mathrm {A}} ^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {N O} _ {3} ^ {-} / \mathrm {N O}\right) = 0. 9 4 2 \mathrm {V}, \varphi^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {C u} ^ {2 +} / \mathrm {C u}\right) = 0. 3 4 2 \mathrm {V}, \varphi^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {C u} ^ {+} / \mathrm {C u}\right) = 0. 5 2 1 \mathrm {V}.
$$

Based on the above conditions, write the ionic equation for the dissolution of  $\mathrm{Cu_2S}$  in dilute nitric acid to form a blue solution and a yellow precipitate, and calculate the standard equilibrium constant of this reaction using Hess's law. Select the option closest to your calculated equilibrium constant.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B.  $K^{\theta} = 1.1 \times 10^{80}$  
C.  $K^{\theta} = 2.0 \times 10^{130}$  
D.  $K^{\theta} = 2.4 \times 10^{8}$  
E.  $K^{\theta} = 5.1 \times 10^{43}$

# Answer

Correct Answer: B

# Detailed Explanation

First, calculate the standard potential for the reduction of cupric ion to cuprous ion:

$$
\varphi_ {\mathrm {B}} ^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {C u} ^ {2 +} / \mathrm {C u} ^ {+}\right) = 2 \varphi^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {C u} ^ {2 +} / \mathrm {C u}\right) - \varphi^ {\theta} \left(\mathrm {C u} ^ {+} / \mathrm {C u}\right) = 0. 1 6 3 \mathrm {V}
$$

The chemical equation mentioned in the question is:

$$
3 \mathrm {C u} _ {2} \mathrm {S} + 4 \mathrm {N O} _ {3} ^ {-} + 1 6 \mathrm {H} ^ {+} = 6 \mathrm {C u} ^ {2 +} + 3 \mathrm {S} + 4 \mathrm {N O} + 8 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}
$$
