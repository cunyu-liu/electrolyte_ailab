# Question

Trace element  $\mathbf{X}$  is named after a Western European country. Extraction of  $\mathbf{X}$  from minerals of elements from the same group requires a complex process, in which the key step is the formation of a precipitate  $\mathbf{A}$  containing  $\mathbf{X}$  and a precipitate  $\mathbf{B}$  of the same type but without  $\mathbf{X}$  to enrich  $\mathbf{X}$ .

The element  $\mathbf{X}$  can react with a colorless irritating gas  $\mathbf{C}$  to form a gray semi-metallic solid  $\mathbf{D}$ , and  $\mathbf{D}$ -type compounds have important industrial applications.

A monovalent anion  $\mathbf{E}$  containing a mass fraction of  $37.11\%$  of  $\mathbf{X}$  has a  $C_2$  axis and 4 bridging bonds, while the neutral compound  $\mathbf{F}$  with the same elemental composition as  $\mathbf{E}$  exhibits a  $+2$  apparent valence for  $\mathbf{X}$ , but in reality, there is an anion-cation pair.  $\mathbf{F}$  dissolves in diethyl ether to obtain a solvate  $\mathbf{G}$ , which also contains discrete anions and cations with the same coordination number for the anions and cations.

The reaction of  $LiBH_{4}$  with a high-valent halide of  $\mathbf{X}$  at  $-45^{\circ}C$  yields a ternary compound  $\mathbf{H}$ , where  $\omega_{\mathbf{X}} = 69.43\%$ , which can be approximately considered to have  $C_{2v}$  symmetry.

A binary compound  $\mathbf{I}$  of  $\mathbf{X}$  can be prepared below  $308K$ . Its monomer is a viscous liquid with slight polarity, while the polymer is a white solid. Both have strong reducing properties. The  $1:1$  adduct formed by  $\mathbf{I}$  with ligand  $\mathbf{J}$  can be used to prepare  $\mathbf{I}$  and can also form a  $1:2$  complex  $\mathbf{K}$ , whose configuration is different from  $\mathbf{H}$ .

C, J, D contain the same element, and J can be prepared by complete methylation of C.

Given the following options for the above process, let  $x$  be the product of the serial numbers of all incorrect statements, and  $y$  be the sum of the serial numbers of all correct statements. Find  $\frac{\sqrt{x}}{y + 1}$  and select the option with an absolute error  $< 0.001$ , if there is no option that meets the condition, choose  $A$ .

1. The acidity of precipitate  $\mathbf{A}$  is stronger than that of precipitate  $\mathbf{B}$  partly due to the effect of electron shielding on the effective nuclear charge  
2. Gas C dissolves in water to give an acidic solution  
3. Compound D reacts violently with water at room temperature

4. One  $C_2$  axis of anion  $\mathbf{E}$  passes through a hexacoordinate atom and two ligands  
5. Compound  $\mathbf{F}$  has a molecular weight of 281.2  
6. The anion and cation of solvate  $\mathbf{G}$  have the same stereoconfiguration  
7. One molecule of compound  $\mathbf{H}$  contains 13 atoms  
8. Compound I is extremely prone to decompose into its elemental form at room temperature  
9. The number of atoms of each element in ligand  $\mathbf{J}$  constitutes a geometric sequence  
10. The configuration of complex  $\mathbf{K}$  is square pyramidal

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

$\mathbf{X}$  is both a rare element and named after a Western European country. Consider the three elements  $Ga(Gallia), Ge(Germenia), Sc(Scandia)$ .

Consider the reaction of  $LiBH_{4}$  with a high-valent halide of  $\mathbf{X}$  to obtain a ternary compound  $\mathbf{H}$ . The strong reducing power of  $LiBH_{4}$  completely replaces the halogen and provides two kinds of ions,  $BH_{4}^{-}, H^{-}$ . Therefore, the other two elements besides  $\mathbf{X}$  should be  $B, H$ . Let the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{H}$  be  $\mathbf{X}H_{x}(BH_{4})_{y}$ , then  $\omega_{\mathbf{X}} = \frac{Ar(\mathbf{X})}{Ar(\mathbf{X}) + 1.008x + 14.84y} = 69.43\%$ .

Consider the element  $\mathbf{X} = G\mathbf{a}$ , substitute to get the equation  $1.008x + 14.84y = 30.70$ , the approximate nonnegative integer solution is  $x = 1, y = 2$ , corresponding to  $\mathbf{H} = G a H(B H_{4})_{2}$ , actually  $\omega_{G a} = \frac{69.72}{100.408} = 69.43\%$ .

Consider the element  $\mathbf{X} = G\mathbf{e}$ , substitute to get the equation  $1.008x + 14.84y = 31.97$ , the approximate nonnegative integer solution is  $x = 2, y = 2$ , corresponding to  $\mathbf{H} = G e H_{2}(B H_{4})_{2}$ , actually  $\omega_{G e} = \frac{72.61}{104.306} = 69.61\%$ .

Consider the element  $\mathbf{X} = Sc$ , substitute to get the equation  $1.008x + 14.84y = 19.80$ , the approximate nonnegative integer solution is  $x = 5, y = 1$ , corresponding to  $\mathbf{H} = ScH_{5}(BH_{4})$ , actually  $\omega_{Sc} = \frac{44.96}{64.84} = 69.34\%$ .

Therefore,  $\mathbf{X} = G a, \mathbf{H} = G a H(BH_{4})_{2}$ , approximately has  $C_{2v}$  symmetry. The number of atoms in one molecule of compound  $\mathbf{H}$  is 12, statement 7 is wrong.
