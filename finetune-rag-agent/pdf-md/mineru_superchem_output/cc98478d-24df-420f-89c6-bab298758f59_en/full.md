# Question

The element  $\mathbf{M}$  of the first transition series dissolves in dilute sulfuric acid and crystallizes to obtain a hydrated sulfate crystal  $\mathbf{A}$ , in which the mass fraction of  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $20.97\%$ . Element  $\mathbf{M}$  has only one nonradioactive stable isotope.

The THF solvate of the simple chloride of  $\mathbf{M}$  reacts with 1-lithiated bicyclo[2.2.1]heptane in pentane to produce  $\mathbf{M}$  element and a mononuclear, electrically neutral complex  $\mathbf{D}$  with a carbon content of  $76.50\%$ .

Reduction of  $\mathbf{D}$  with lithium element in tetrahydrofuran yields a salt  $\mathbf{E}$  with a cation-anion ratio of 1:1, while oxidation of  $\mathbf{D}$  with silver tetrafluoroborate yields a salt  $\mathbf{F}$  with a cation-anion ratio of 1:1.

Regarding the above content, there are the following conclusions:

Conclusion 1: Isotope  $\mathbf{M}_2$  whose mass number is 1 greater than that of the stable isotope of  $\mathbf{M}$  emits  $\beta$  rays when decaying.

Conclusion 2: The molecular weight of  $\mathbf{D}$  is 583.77.

Conclusion 3:  $\mathbf{D}$ ,  $\mathbf{E}$ , and  $\mathbf{F}$  all contain  $\mathbf{M}$  with an octahedral coordination configuration.

Conclusion 4: The theoretically calculated spin-only magnetic moment of  $\mathbf{E}$  is 2.83 B.M..

Conclusion 5:  $\mathbf{F}$  is a diamagnetic substance.

Select the option corresponding to all the correct conclusions.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. Option 1, Option 2, Option 3

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

Only  $Sc$ ,  $Mn$ , and  $Co$  are elements in the first transition series that have only one stable isotope. Examine the mass fraction of the metal in their hydrated sulfates as follows:

- If  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $Sc$ :  $Sc$  usually forms  $+3$  valent ions in acid, so the salt is  $Sc_2(SO_4)_3 \cdot nH_2O$ . The relative molecular mass  $M_r(\mathbf{A}) = 2 \times 44.96 \div 0.2097 = 428.8$ , the number of moles of water  $n = (428.8 - (2 \times 44.96 + 3 \times (32.07 + 4 \times 16.00))) \div 18.02 = 2.81$ , which is not an integer, so  $Sc$  is excluded.  
- If  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $Mn$ :  $Mn$  usually forms  $+2$  valent ions in dilute sulfuric acid, so the salt is  $MnSO_4 \cdot nH_2O$ .  $M_r(\mathbf{A}) = 54.94 \div 0.2097 = 262.0$ , the number of moles of water  $n = (262.0 - (54.94 + 32.07 + 4 \times 16.00)) \div 18.02 = 6.16$ , which is not an integer, so  $Mn$  is excluded.  
- If  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $C_0$ : Co forms +2 valent ions in dilute sulfuric acid, so the salt is  $C_0SO_4 \cdot nH_2O$ .  
[ M_r(\mathbf{A}) = 58.93 \div 0.2097 = 281.0, \quad \text{the number of moles of water} ]
[ n = (281.0 - (58.93 + 32.07 + 4 \times 16.00)) \div 18.02 = 7.00 ] which is an integer, so it may be  $C_0$ .  
Therefore, the metal  $\mathbf{M}$  is cobalt ( $C_o$ ), and its only stable isotope is  $^{59}C_o$ .
