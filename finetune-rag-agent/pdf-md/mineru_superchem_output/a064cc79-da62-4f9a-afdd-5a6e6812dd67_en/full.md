# Question

In the following two variants of cubic ZnS, the metal atoms form alternating layered structures, with only one type of metal atom within the same layer, and they are all on the same plane.  $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{F}$  represent six different elements.

In  $\mathbf{ABC}_2$ , A and B together occupy the Zn position in cubic ZnS, and there is no three-fold rotation axis in this crystal.

In  $\mathbf{DEF}_2$ ,  $\mathbf{D}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$  together occupy the Zn position in cubic ZnS, and there is a three-fold rotation axis in this crystal.

How many atoms are there in a primitive unit cell of  $\mathbf{ABC}_2$ ? How many atoms are there in a structural motif?

How many atoms are there in a primitive unit cell of  $\mathbf{DEF}_2$ ? How many atoms are there in a structural motif?

A. 4; 4; 12; 12  
B. 8; 4; 12; 12  
C. 4; 4; 12; 4  
D. 8; 4; 12; 4  
E. 12; 12; 4; 4  
F. 12; 12; 8; 4  
G. 12; 4; 4; 4

H. 12; 4; 8; 4  
1. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: C

# Detailed Explanation

In  $\mathbf{ABC}_2$ ,  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  jointly occupy the position of  $\mathrm{Zn}$  in the cubic  $\mathrm{ZnS}$ . There is no threefold rotation axis in this crystal, so in  $\mathbf{ABC}_2$  it should be that  $\mathbf{A}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  replace the two layers of  $\mathrm{Zn}$  at  $z = 0$  and  $z = 0.5$  in the cubic  $\mathrm{ZnS}$  unit cell with  $\mathrm{Zn}$  as the vertex, instead of replacing the close-packed layers (which would lead to a threefold rotation axis).
