# Question

$\mathbf{M}$  is a metallic element that can form a salt  $\mathbf{MX}_2\cdot \mathrm{nH}_2\mathrm{O}$  (n is an integer) with a monobasic organic acid HX. The following thermogravimetric analysis experiment was performed on the salt (all data are experimental values):

(1)  $\mathbf{MX}_2\cdot \mathrm{nH}_2\mathrm{O}$  is heated to  $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in nitrogen, with a weight loss of  $14.2\%$ , corresponding to the loss of all crystal water; heating is continued to  $280^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , with a further weight loss of  $13.4\%$  (Reaction 1); heating is continued to above  $400^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and after cooling, the remaining solid is metal  $\mathbf{M}$ , with a mass of  $54.8\%$  of the original mass.  
(2)  $\mathbf{MX}_2 \cdot \mathrm{nH}_2\mathrm{O}$  is heated to above  $400^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in air, and after cooling, the remaining solid is oxide  $\mathbf{Y}$ , with a mass of  $58.7\%$  of the original mass.

Through calculation, deduce what metal element  $\mathbf{M}$  is, and deduce what substances the salt  $\mathbf{MX}_2\cdot \mathrm{nH}_2\mathrm{O}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  are. (Hint: The ratio of the number of metal atoms to oxygen atoms in  $\mathbf{Y}$  is a simple integer ratio)

Which of the following statements are correct:

1. If  $\mathbf{M}$  is in the a-th period and the b-th column from left to right, then  $(a + b) / ab > 0.25$  
2.  $\mathbf{MX}_2$  is a strong electrolyte  
3.  $\mathbf{Y}$  is insoluble in NaOH  
4. The sum of the number of all atoms in the chemical formula of the solid product of Reaction 1 is less than or equal to 15

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Let the molar mass of  $\mathbf{M}$  be  $\mathrm{xg / mol}$ , then:  $18.02\mathrm{n}:\mathrm{x} = 14.2:54.8$

When  $\mathrm{n} = 1$ ,  $\mathrm{x} = 69.5$  is Ga (oxidation state does not match, discard)

When  $\mathrm{n} = 2$ ,  $\mathrm{x} = 139$  is La (oxidation state does not match, discard) or Ba

When  $n = 3$ ,  $x = 208.6$  is Bi (oxidation state does not match, discard) or Pb

Combined with oxide data, let the chemical formula of oxide  $\mathbf{Y}$  be  $\mathrm{MO_y}$ , then:

$$
(x + 1 6. 0 0 y): x = 5 8. 7: 5 4. 8
$$

When  $\mathbf{M}$  is Ba, solving for  $y = 0.60$ , which is unreasonable, discard;

When  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $\mathrm{Pb}$ , solving for  $\mathrm{y} = 0.92 \approx 1$ , corresponding to  $\mathrm{PbO}$ ;

Therefore, metal  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $\mathrm{Pb}$ ,  $\mathbf{Y}$  is  $\mathrm{PbO}$

CHECKPOINT

M is Pb, Y is PbO
