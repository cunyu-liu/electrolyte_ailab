# Question

Elements A, B, and C are in the same period with increasing atomic numbers and are adjacent in the periodic table. They can all form ions with the general formula  $\mathrm{XO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$ . The value of m is the same for these three ions, and the value of n is also the same. None of these three ions can exist in large quantities in acidic aqueous solutions. It is known that the mass fraction of these three elements in  $\mathrm{XO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  is strictly less than  $50\%$ .

Regarding these three elements, which of the following statements is correct?

A. Element A has paired valence electrons.  
B. The  $\mathrm{AO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  ion of element A is colorless.  
C. Acidification of the  $\mathrm{BO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  ion of element B releases bubbles.  
D. Acidification of  $\mathrm{BO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  ions of element B can produce a white precipitate.  
E. The standard electrode potential of the half-reaction for the oxidation of element C in the  $\mathrm{CO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  ion under acidic conditions is greater than  $2.0V$ .  
F. Ions of element  $\mathbf{M}$  with the formula  $\mathrm{MO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  can be used to test for phosphate ions, then element  $\mathbf{M}$  belongs to the same group as element  $\mathbf{C}$ .

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

First forming  $\mathrm{XO}_{\mathrm{m}}^{\mathrm{n}-}$  implies that these three adjacent elements in the same period have the same valence, which basically locks it down to a transition metal, because it is difficult for three consecutive main group nonmetals to have the same valence.

To verify this hypothesis, consider the values of  $m$  and  $n$ :

When  $m$  is 1, the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  must be less than 16, and it is impossible to have three consecutive  $\mathrm{XO^{n - }}$ .

When  $m$  is 2, the relative atomic mass of  $X$  must be less than 32. At this time, because the atomic mass of sulfur is 32.06, it is impossible. The most likely candidates are Al, Si, P, but since  $n$  can only be 1 (metaaluminate) at this time, and there is no such species as  $\mathrm{SiO}_2^-$ ,  $m$  cannot be 2.

When m is 3, the relative atomic mass of X must be less than 48, and the element must be before Ti. Consider P, S, Cl. When n is 1, sulfur does not satisfy; when n is 2, chlorine does not satisfy; when n is 3, sulfur does not satisfy; when it is greater than 3, phosphorus does not satisfy. Therefore, m does not take 3 either.

Therefore, the elements Cr, Mn, Fe meet the requirements of the question. A is Cr, B is Mn, C is Fe.
