# Question

A、B、C、D are four non-metallic simple substances, where the corresponding elements of three of them are located in the same period, and the corresponding elements of two of them are located in the same group. A reacts with oxygen under high temperature conditions to obtain oxide E. E reacts with urea under high temperature conditions, or E reacts with  $\mathrm{CaB_6}$  under high temperature conditions and a nitrogen atmosphere to obtain a binary compound F, and F can be used as a component material of rocket engines. At  $-45^{\circ}C$ , C and D react in  $\mathrm{CFCl}_3$  to obtain a yellow solid G. At  $-78^{\circ}C$ , D and G react in  $\mathrm{CFCl}_3$  to obtain H. Heating H to above  $0^{\circ}C$  causes decomposition, yielding D and I. I can also be prepared by directly heating C and D. Mixing C and I at  $90^{\circ}C$ , and then heating sufficiently, yields J. J decomposes at temperatures above  $450^{\circ}C$ , yielding equimolar amounts of C and I. At low temperatures, F reacts with H to obtain K and a common Lewis acid L. K decomposes to form B and D.

Please provide the chemical formulas of each substance and choose the correct option.

A. All other options are incorrect.  
B. Element A is a gas at room temperature.  
C. In the process of generating  $\mathbf{F}$ , the roles of urea and  $\mathrm{CaB_6}$  are completely consistent.  
D. E is an amphoteric oxide  
E. The central atom of  $\mathbf{J}$  is  $sp^3 d$  hybridized.  
F.  $\mathbf{F}$  and  $\mathbf{H}$  react in a 1:1 ratio.  
G. The boiling point of  $\mathbf{I}$  is higher than  $\mathbf{J}$ .

H. A, B, C, D, the elements corresponding to the four kinds of simple substances all have only one stable hydride.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

Since  $\mathbf{L}$  is a common Lewis acid and the elements involved are all non-metallic elements, it can be known that it should contain boron and halogen elements. Moreover, it is generated by the reaction of  $\mathbf{H}$  and  $\mathbf{F}$ , and  $\mathbf{G}$ ,  $\mathbf{J}$ , and  $\mathbf{I}$  have the same constituent elements as the compound of  $\mathbf{H}$ . It does not conform to the properties of boron, so  $\mathbf{F}$  should contain boron. Next, analyze the preparation process of the binary compound  $\mathbf{F}$ : One way is the reaction of oxide  $\mathbf{E}$  with urea. Since there is no other source of boron other than  $\mathbf{E}$ , it indicates that it is boron oxide  $B_{2}O_{3}$ , and the role of urea in the reaction is usually a carbon source or a nitrogen source, and it is more reasonable to act as a nitrogen source here; another way is the reaction of  $B_{2}O_{3}$  with  $\mathrm{CaB}_{6}$  in a nitrogen atmosphere at high temperature. Combining the reaction conditions, it can be known that the existence form of the  $\mathrm{Ca}$  element in the product is  $\mathrm{CaO}$ , then the corresponding binary compound is a boron-nitrogen compound. In summary, starting from multiple conditions, it can be concluded that  $\mathbf{F}$  should be  $BN$ .  $BN$  has a high melting point and high hardness, which is also consistent with the statement in the question "used in rocket engines".

In the process of generating  $BN$ , urea acts as a nitrogen source, and  $\mathrm{CaB}_6$  acts as a reducing agent (and also as a partial boron source), so the functions are different, and option C is incorrect.

$B_{2}O_{3}$  is a typical acidic oxide, and option D is incorrect.
