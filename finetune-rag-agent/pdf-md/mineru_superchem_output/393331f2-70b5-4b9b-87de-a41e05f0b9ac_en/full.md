# Question

At  $273\mathrm{K}$ , a mass  $m$  of NaCl solid (whose specific heat can be neglected) is sprinkled into a mixture containing  $100\mathrm{g}$  of ice and  $100\mathrm{g}$  of water. Assuming the formed solution is an ideal mixture, neglecting the thermal effect of NaCl dissolution and heat exchange between the mixture and the surroundings, calculate the required mass  $m$  of NaCl and the mass of ice at this point to ensure the final temperature of the ice-water mixture is  $268\mathrm{K}$ .

Data that may be used for this problem:

The specific heat capacity of water is  $4.18\mathrm{J / (g\cdot K)}$

The specific heat capacity of ice is  $2.10\mathrm{J / (g*K)}$

The enthalpy of fusion of ice at 298K is  $6008\mathrm{J / mol}$ .

Hint: When the amount of NaCl added is large, using approximate formulas may lead to errors.

A. The mass of NaCl required is  $7.78 \mathrm{~g}$ , and the mass of ice is  $88.5 \mathrm{~g}$ .  
B. The required mass of NaCl is  $17.3 \mathrm{~g}$ , and the mass of ice is  $90.3 \mathrm{~g}$ .  
C. The required mass of NaCl is  $15.5 \mathrm{~g}$ , and the mass of ice is  $88.5 \mathrm{~g}$ .  
D. The required mass of NaCl is  $9.78 \mathrm{~g}$ , and the mass of ice is  $68.5 \mathrm{~g}$ .  
E. The required mass of NaCl is  $8.61 \, \mathrm{g}$ , and the mass of ice is  $90.3 \, \mathrm{g}$ .  
F. None of the above results are correct (with an error greater than  $5\%$ ).

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

In this problem, due to the large mass of NaCl, directly applying the freezing point depression law would cause errors!

$$
\mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} (l) = \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} (s)
