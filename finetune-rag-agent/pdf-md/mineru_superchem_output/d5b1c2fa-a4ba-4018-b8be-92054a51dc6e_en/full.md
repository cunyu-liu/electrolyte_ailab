# Question

Sodium cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl Ferrate(0) reacts with 2-chloroethyl methyl sulfide to generate compound A with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C_{10}H_{12}FeO_2S}$ ; A generates B upon heating. B exhibits two carbonyl stretching vibrational infrared absorption peaks. It is known that sodium cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl Ferrate(0) can be obtained by reacting an iron complex X that satisfies the 18-electron rule with sodium, and sodium cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl Ferrate(0) is the only product. The following statements are made:

1. The cyclopentadienyl dicarbonylferrate(0) anion carries 2 negative charges.  
2. Iron in both A and B satisfies the 18-electron rule.  
3. B has a bridging carbonyl structure.  
4.  $\mathbf{X}$  is a mononuclear complex.

What is the sum of the numbers of the correct statements?

A. 2  
B. 5  
C. 7  
D. 9  
E. 10

F. 11  
G. 13

H. None of the above answers are correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Sodium cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl iron(0) satisfies the 18-electron rule. The charge carried by the negative ion is  $18 - 8 - 5 - 2 \times 2 = 1$ . Statement 1 is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

Cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl iron(0) anion carries a 1- negative charge

A is the product of the  $\mathrm{S_N2}$  reaction of cyclopentadienyl dicarbonyl iron(0) anion with 2-chloroethyl methyl sulfide  $(\mathrm{ClCH}_2\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{SCH}_3)$ , i.e.,  $(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})_2\mathrm{FeCH}_2\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{SCH}_3$ . If sulfur does not coordinate to iron, then there are  $8 + 5 + 2 \times 2 + 1 = 18$  electrons around iron, satisfying the 18-electron rule.

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

Iron in A satisfies the 18-electron rule

B has two different carbon-oxygen bonds, and bridging carbonyls may exist. B is a dimer of A. However, A has no driving force for dimerization, so this reaction pathway is excluded. In addition,  $\beta$ -elimination is uncommon when iron is the central metal, so it is excluded. Another possibility is that the alkyl ligand of A migrates to CO to form an acyl ligand, and the product is  $(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})\mathrm{Fe}[\mathrm{C}(\mathrm{O})\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{SCH}_3]$ . At this time, there are only 16 electrons around iron, and the sulfur atom of the acyl ligand can coordinate to iron, making iron satisfy the 18-electron rule, and the stability is improved. Statement 2 is correct, and statement 3 is incorrect.

Iron in B satisfies the 18-electron rule

$\mathrm{Na}[\mathrm{Fe}(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})_2]$  is obtained by the reaction of sodium and  $\mathbf{X}$ , and the product is unique, so the simplest formula of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $[\mathrm{Fe}(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})_2]$ . At this time, there are  $8 + 5 + 2 \times 2 = 17$  electrons around iron, which does not satisfy the 18-electron rule, so there is a metal-metal bond in  $\mathbf{X}$ , which should be  $[\mathrm{Fe}(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})_2]_2$ . Statement 4 is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

X is  $\left[\mathrm{Fe}(\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_5)(\mathrm{CO})_2\right]_2$

In summary, the correct statement is 2, choose option A.
