# Question

For the following organic reaction, select the incorrect description regarding its organic product.

![](images/8a6e156227d440f12c11cc05a0f41ae8896d1a576cccfe7ef4ebcf781a1cd892.jpg)

The image shows a rightward horizontal arrow. On the left side of the arrow is an organic molecule with the structure c1ccccccccc. Above the arrow is the text [Et_2N=SF_2][BF_4], and below the arrow is the text Et_3N.

A. Under this reaction condition, the mass fraction of the halogen in the generated halogenated hydrocarbon molecule is  $13.75\%$ .  
B. Under this reaction condition, the two atoms with the longest shortest path in the generated ether pass through 16 chemical bonds.  
C. Under this reaction condition, an organic molecule with a 4-coordinate sulfur atom was generated, and the total number of atoms in the molecule is 45.  
D. Under this reaction condition, an organic compound without a benzene ring was generated, which contains atoms of six different elements.  
E. Under these reaction conditions, among the benzene-ring-containing products generated, the product with the highest relative molecular mass exceeds 300.  
F. Under these reaction conditions, among the generated products, there are organic compounds devoid of benzene rings, with relative molecular masses ranging between 130 and 140.

G. Under these reaction conditions, there are no two organic products with a relative molecular mass difference of less than 10 between them.  
H. If the reaction is carried out in acetonitrile solvent, a byproduct containing nitrogen, oxygen, and benzene rings but no fluorine or sulfur will be obtained.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

In this reaction system, triethylamine acts as a base to deprotonate the hydroxyl group of the alcohol, yielding an alkoxide intermediate:

![](images/fce371bb22694b203674c33289d34a9273916bb4d111fa4728d281646c99b02e.jpg)  
c1cccccc1CCC[O-]

In the cation of  $\left[\mathrm{Et}_2\mathrm{N} = \mathrm{SF}_2\right]\left[\mathrm{BF}_4\right]$ , the sulfur atom is electrophilic. Upon attack by the alkoxide of the substrate, a neutral intermediate containing tetracoordinated sulfur—with both nitrogen and oxygen ligands—is formed:

![](images/cf516aed577944a30ed5de27482fadd0b1f030a7ce2dfb257ce32ab8debec595.jpg)  
CCN(CC)S(F)(F)OCCCc1cccccc1

This intermediate subsequently undergoes an intramolecular substitution reaction. The possible mechanism involves the lone pair on O attacking the antibonding orbital of the S - F bond, cleaving the S - F bond to form a tight ion pair. The fluoride ion then attacks the carbon bonded to oxygen, replacing the oxygen of the alkoxide to yield a monofluorinated product:
