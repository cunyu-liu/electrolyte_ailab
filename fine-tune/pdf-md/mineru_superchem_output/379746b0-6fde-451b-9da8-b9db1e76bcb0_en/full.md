$$
F E = \frac {n _ {C O} \times z _ {C O} \times F}{Q} \times 100 \%

During the electrochemical reduction process, the reactions producing  $1\mathrm{mol}$  of  $\mathrm{H}_{2}$  and CO both involve the transfer of  $2\mathrm{mol}$  of electrons:

$$
\mathrm {C O} _ {2} + 2 \mathrm {e} + 2 \mathrm {H} ^ {+} \rightarrow \mathrm {C O} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}

However, the question emphasizes the importance of the airtightness of the device, and the previous experiment with Ar atmosphere in KOH was to test this. Since only  $\mathrm{H}_{2}$  is produced under these conditions, the theoretical FE should be  $100\%$  if there is no leakage, which is a common method for calibrating airtightness in electrochemistry.

To be precise, under Ar protection and KOH electrolyte conditions, the electroreduction reaction can only produce hydrogen, so the Faradaic efficiency of hydrogen can be used to test the airtightness of the device

$$
n _ {H _ {2}} = \frac {1 0 1 . 3 2 5 \times 1 6 \times 1 . 5 8 4 / 1 0 0 0 0 0}{8 . 3 1 4 \times 2 9 8 . 1 5} = 1. 0 3 6 \times 1 0 ^ {- 5} m o l

The airtightness of the device is greater than  $90\%$ , and the overall airtightness is good.
