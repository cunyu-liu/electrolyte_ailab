# Question

The hexahydrate nitrate of a fifth-period metal element  $\mathbf{M}$  is mixed with  $\mathrm{Cr(NO_3)_3\cdot 9H_2O}$  in stoichiometric ratio and gradually heated in an oxygen stream to produce compound  $\mathbf{X}$  and release a reddish-brown mixed gas (Reaction 1). Further studies have shown that  $\mathbf{X}$  is a paramagnetic solid with the chemical formula  $\mathrm{MCrO_4}$ ;  $0.2786\mathrm{g}$  of solid  $\mathbf{X}$  is dissolved in  $0.10\mathrm{mol/L}$ $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{SO}_4$  without gas evolution (Reaction 2); the resulting solution is titrated to the endpoint with  $23.76\mathrm{mL}$  of  $0.1144\mathrm{mol/L}$ $\mathrm{Fe}^{2+}$  solution (Reaction 3). In the above process, the oxidation state of the metal element  $\mathbf{M}$  remains unchanged.

Let the atomic number of  $\mathbf{M}$  be a, the valence of Cr in  $\mathbf{X}$  be b, and the absolute values of the difference between the number of reactant molecules and the number of product molecules in reactions 1, 2, and 3 (Reaction 1 is a chemical equation, 2 and 3 are ionic equations with the simplest integer ratio coefficients) be c, d, and e, respectively. Then the value of  $\log_{\min\{c,d,e\}} \frac{\max\{c,d,e\} + b}{a}$  is (an error within 0.01 is considered correct):

A. All other options are incorrect

# Answer
