# Question

$\mathrm{CuSO}_4$ ,  $\mathrm{H}_5\mathrm{IO}_6$ ,  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{O}_8$ , and KOH were mixed in a certain proportion,  $200~\mathrm{mL}$  of distilled water was added, heated to boiling for  $30\mathrm{min}$ , cooled, and filtered to obtain a blue-green solution (Reaction 1); an appropriate amount of  $10\%$  NaOH solution was added to it, allowed to stand for  $48\mathrm{h}$ , and blue-green crystals precipitated. Partial analysis results of the blue-green crystals are as follows:

(1) X-ray diffraction analysis showed that the crystal is a complex salt with crystal water, the cations are  $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$  and  $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ , and the anion is a mononuclear coordination ion centered on copper, with  $\mathrm{HIO}_{6}^{4-}$  as the ligand.  
(2)  $0.2001\mathrm{g}$  of the crystal was weighed,  $10~\mathrm{mL}$  of hydrochloric acid was added, and heated to dissolve the sample, then diluted with  $50~\mathrm{mL}$  of distilled water, buffer solution and PAN indicator were added, and titrated with  $0.01973\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  EDTA solution, consuming  $11.81~\mathrm{mL}$ .  
(3)  $0.2059 \, \mathrm{g}$  of the crystal was weighed,  $10 \, \mathrm{mL} \, 25\%$  KI solution and  $10 \, \mathrm{mL} \, 25\%$  sulfuric acid solution were added (Reaction 2), after full reaction, with starch as an indicator, titrated with  $0.1024 \, \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  sodium thiosulfate solution, consuming  $42.16 \, \mathrm{mL}$ .  
(4) Thermogravimetric analysis of the crystal showed a weight loss of  $25.18\%$ , corresponding to the loss of all crystal water.

# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

0.2001 g The copper content in the crystal is: 0.01973 mol  $\cdot$  L $^{-1}$ $\times$  11.81 mL = 2.330  $\times$  10 $^{-4}$  mol

Converted to copper content in  $1\mathrm{g}$  crystal:  $2.330 \times 10^{-4} \times 1 / 0.2001 = 1.164 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~mol} \cdot \mathrm{g}^{-1}$

The amount of electrons transferred by 1 g crystal is:

$$
0. 1 0 2 4 \mathrm {m o l} \cdot \mathrm {L} ^ {- 1} \times 4 2. 1 6 \mathrm {m L} \times 1 / 0. 2 0 5 9 = 2. 0 9 7 \times 1 0 ^ {- 2} \mathrm {m o l} \cdot \mathrm {g} ^ {- 1}
$$

The number of electrons transferred per mole of crystal reacting with  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  is:

$$
(2. 0 9 7 \times 1 0 ^ {- 2}) / (1. 1 6 4 \times 1 0 ^ {- 3}) = 1 8. 0 2 \approx 1 8
$$

Each mole of  $\mathrm{HIO}_6^{4-}$  is reduced to  $\mathrm{I}^-$  transferring 8 mol electrons, so each mole of crystal contains 2 mol ligand  $\mathrm{HIO}_6^{4-}$
