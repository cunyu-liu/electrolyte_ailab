When  $\mathbf{H}$  is dissolved in anhydrous hydrogen fluoride, and colloidal  $\mathbf{M}$  element is added, a brown solid  $\mathbf{I}$  precipitates,  $\mathbf{I}$  has a cadmium iodide inverse structure;  $\mathbf{I}$  undergoes disproportionation upon contact with water, yielding  $\mathbf{M}$  element and solid  $\mathbf{H}$  again.

It is known that  $\mathbf{B}$  exhibits a chain structure.

H. The  $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{xy}}$  orbital of the M element in  $\mathbf{B}$  is in a half-filled state.
