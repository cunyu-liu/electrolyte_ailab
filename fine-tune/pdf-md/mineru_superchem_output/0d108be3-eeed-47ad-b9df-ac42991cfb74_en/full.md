Residues are denoted in the format PeptideChain-ResidueNumber, e.g., A3 represents the third residue of Chain A, and B9 represents the ninth residue of Chain B. Assuming only standard residues and their modifications are considered, and no unnatural amino acids are present, which of the following statements about this peptide compound is correct?

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

We first write down the products of the two peptide chains in Experiment 1 after trypsin digestion and match the sequences with the molecular weights from Experiment 2.

The reason this peptide chain was not sequenced may be due to cyclization, N-terminal post-translational modifications, or other factors. Since Edman degradation relies on reacting with the free N-terminal amino group of the protein, it cannot sequence the peptide when the amino group is blocked.

However, the first residue with a molecular weight of 159.04 does not correspond to any standard residue. Additionally, as mentioned earlier, Edman degradation could not sequence this peptide chain because its N-terminal amino group was blocked. Therefore, 159.04 corresponds to an amino acid with a blocked N-terminal amino group. Cyclic peptides can be ruled out here because i and j have only one cleavage site (the C-terminal R of j). If it were a cyclic peptide, digestion would yield a single linear peptide, not two. Furthermore, the mass spectrometry results also indicate that this peptide could not be linked to other peptide chains via isopeptide bonds, as the digestion products would otherwise appear together with those sequenced in Experiment 1.
