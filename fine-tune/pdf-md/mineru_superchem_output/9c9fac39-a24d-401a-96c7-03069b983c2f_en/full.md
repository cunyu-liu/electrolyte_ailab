# Question

Dissolve  $\mathrm{MnCl}_2\cdot 4\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  in ethanol, and add an aqueous solution of Htopca (a carboxylic acid derivative with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_6\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{O}_3$ ). After mixing and stirring at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $2\mathrm{~h}$ , filter. The filtrate is slowly evaporated at room temperature, and a yellow blocky crystal precipitates from the solution. Thermogravimetric analysis of  $\mathbf{X}$  under a nitrogen atmosphere shows that  $\mathbf{X}$  loses a% of its weight between 65 and  $120^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , corresponding to the loss of all crystal water and structural water; continue heating to  $250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the weight loss is b%; continue heating to  $850^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the weight loss is further  $14.04\%$ , and the subsequent solid mass no longer changes. Chemical analysis of the residual solid shows that the solid contains two substances, and the mass fraction of  $\mathbf{Mn}$  in the mixture is  $46.17\%$ . The mixed gas released during the second weight loss is analyzed, and the mixed gas contains three gas molecules, of which only propyne contains carbon. After cooling the mixed gas, it is fully absorbed by bromine water. The mass of the remaining gas is  $37.94\%$  of the original gas mass, and the remaining gas changes from colorless to reddish-brown in the air. It is known that  $\mathbf{X}$  is a mononuclear Mn complex, and the oxidation state of  $\mathbf{Mn}$  does not change during the thermogravimetric analysis.

Given  $a + b = 58.07$ , which of the following statements are correct:

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Since the remaining gas changes from colorless to reddish-brown in air, and considering that Htopca contains nitrogen, it is speculated that the remaining gas contains NO; since the gas first underwent cooling, it is speculated that it may also contain  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  before cooling.

Therefore, the composition of the mixed gas is likely propyne  $+\mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ , or propyne  $+\mathrm{NO} +$  another gas.

Since the mass of the remaining gas is  $37.94\%$  of the original gas, assuming it is all NO, then:

$$
\operatorname {M r} (\text {r e m a i n i n g} 1) = (1 4. 0 1 + 1 6. 0 0) / 0. 3 7 9 4 - (1 4. 0 1 + 1 6. 0 0) = 4 9. 0 9
$$

Subtracting the relative molecular mass of one molecule of propyne yields:

$$
\operatorname {M r} (\text {r e m a i n i n g 2}) = 4 9. 0 9 - 4 0. 0 6 = 9. 0 3
$$

This corresponds exactly to  $0.5\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ , which is consistent with the prediction.

Therefore, the gas lost in the second weight loss is  $2\mathrm{C}_3\mathrm{H}_4 + 2\mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ , so statement 2 is incorrect.

2 PTS

The ratio of the gas lost in the second weight loss is  $\mathrm{C}_3\mathrm{H}_4:\mathrm{NO}:\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O} = 2:2:1$

Since Mn does not change during the process, the Mn in the residual solid should be an oxide of the corresponding valence state, namely MnO. Combined with the mass fraction, we have  $\mathrm{Mr}(\text{remaining}) = 54.94 / 0.4617 - 54.94 - 16 = 48.06$ , which is found to be exactly the relative mass of 4 carbons, so the molecular composition of the residual solid is MnO + 4C. The reaction of elemental carbon with concentrated sulfuric acid can produce  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  and  $\mathrm{SO}_{2}$ , so statement 4 is incorrect.
