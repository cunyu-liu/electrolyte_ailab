# Question

The laboratory determines the content of titanium and iron in ilmenite containing only inert impurities using the following method:

(1) Accurately weigh a  $0.4278\mathrm{~g}$  sample of ilmenite into a  $30~\mathrm{mL}$  porcelain crucible, add  $10\mathrm{~g}$  potassium pyrosulfate, and fuse at  $700 - 750^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for decomposition. After cooling, leach the fused mass into a conical flask using  $80~\mathrm{mL}$  of 1:1 hydrochloric acid and  $20~\mathrm{mL}$  of 1:1 sulfuric acid in portions. Rinse the crucible with a small amount of hot water and transfer the washings to the same conical flask.  
(2) Add  $3\mathrm{g}$  aluminum foil to the conical flask containing the leachate, and heat on an electric hotplate while slowly bubbling carbon dioxide until vigorous bubbling occurs. Cool to room temperature, add  $10~\mathrm{mL}$  of 400 g/L ammonium thiocyanate solution, and titrate with standard ferric ammonium sulfate solution until a blood-red color indicates the endpoint. The consumption of ferric ammonium sulfate solution is  $25.49~\mathrm{mL}$ .  
(3) Weigh  $0.2174\mathrm{g}$  of high-purity titanium dioxide, dissolve it in acid, and treat it under the same two-step conditions described above. Titrate with standard ferric ammonium sulfate solution, consuming  $26.79~\mathrm{mL}$ .

The correct options are (error less than  $1\%$  is considered correct):

A. The titanium content in the mineral is  $15.46\%$ .  
B. The titanium content in the mineral is  $24.83\%$ .  
C. The titanium content in the mineral is  $29.44\%$ .  
D. The titanium content in the mineral is  $35.75\%$ .  
E. The iron content in the mineral is  $20.99\%$ .  
F. The iron content in the mineral is  $26.30\%$ .

G. The iron content in the mineral is  $35.88\%$ .  
H. The iron content in the mineral is  $40.22\%$ .  
1. None of the above options is correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: I

# Detailed Explanation

Ilmenite generally refers to  $\mathrm{FeTiO_3}$ ; when fused with potassium pyrosulfate, it forms the highest-valence sulfates, namely  $\mathrm{Fe_2(SO_4)_3}$ ,  $\mathrm{Ti(SO_4)_2}$ .

$\mathrm{FeTiO_3}$  forms  $\mathrm{Fe}_2(\mathrm{SO}_4)_3$ ,  $\mathrm{Ti}(\mathrm{SO}_4)_2$  when fused with potassium pyrosulfate

$\mathrm{Ti}^{4+}$  is unstable in solution and hydrolyzes to  $\mathrm{TiO}^{2+}$ :

$$
\mathrm {T i} ^ {4 +} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} = \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + 2 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$

$$
\mathrm {T i} ^ {4 +} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} = \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + 2 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$

$\mathrm{TiO}^{2+}$  is strongly oxidizing and can oxidize elemental aluminum:

$$
3 \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + \mathrm {A l} + 6 \mathrm {H} ^ {+} = 3 \mathrm {T i} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {A l} ^ {3 +} + 3 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}
$$

$$
3 \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + \mathrm {A l} + 6 \mathrm {H} ^ {+} = 3 \mathrm {T i} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {A l} ^ {3 +} + 3 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}
$$

The resulting  $\mathrm{Ti}^{3+}$  is then converted back to  $\mathrm{TiO}^{2+}$  by  $\mathrm{Fe}^{3+}$  in the system:

$$
\mathrm {T i} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {F e} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} = \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + \mathrm {F e} ^ {2 +} + 2 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$$
\mathrm {T i} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {F e} ^ {3 +} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} = \mathrm {T i O} ^ {2 +} + \mathrm {F e} ^ {2 +} + 2 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$

Thus, it is equivalent to  $\mathrm{Fe}^{3+}$  oxidizing aluminum first, followed by  $\mathrm{TiO}^{2+}$  oxidizing aluminum.

Ultimately, the system contains only  $\mathrm{Fe}^{2+}$  and  $\mathrm{Ti}^{3+}$ , and ammonium ferric sulfate standard solution is added to titrate  $\mathrm{Ti}^{3+}$  to the endpoint.

The standard solution of ammonium ferric sulfate is calibrated using pure titanium dioxide, with the reaction equation being the same as above and the stoichiometric ratio being 1:1, thus:

$$
\mathrm {c} \left(\mathrm {F e} ^ {3 +}\right) = 0. 2 1 7 4 / 7 9. 8 7 / 0. 0 0 2 6 7 9 = 0. 1 0 1 6 \mathrm {m o l} / \mathrm {L}
$$

1 PTS

The concentration of the ammonium ferric sulfate standard solution  $\mathrm{c(Fe^{3+})} = 0.1016\mathrm{mol / L}$

The titanium content in the sample can be calculated as:

$$
\omega (\mathrm {Ti}) = 0.1016 \times 0.02549 \times 47.87 / 0.4278 \times 100 \% = 28.99 \%
$$

$$
\omega (\mathrm {Ti}) = 28.99 \%
$$

Based on the chemical formula of ilmenite and its inert impurities, the iron content is:

$$
\omega (\mathrm {Fe}) = 0.1016 \times 0.02549 \times 55.85 / 0.4278 \times 100 \% = 33.81 \%
$$

$$

$$

Consequently, options A-H are all incorrect, and option I is correct.
