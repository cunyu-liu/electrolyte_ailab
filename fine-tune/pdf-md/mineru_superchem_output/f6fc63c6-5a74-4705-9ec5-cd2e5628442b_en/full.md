# Question

A certain organic solvent can extract salicylic acid from water, given its distribution coefficient  $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{D}} = 100$ , and the dimerization equilibrium constant of salicylic acid  $\mathrm{K} = 50$ . It is known that the  $\mathrm{pK}_{\mathrm{a1}} = 2.98$ ,  $\mathrm{pK}_{\mathrm{a2}} = 13.66$  of salicylic acid.

The following questions are asked:

a. Calculate the distribution ratio D of salicylic acid extracted by an equal volume of the solvent in water when the total concentration of salicylic acid in the original water is  $0.1\mathrm{mol / L}$  at  $\mathrm{pH} = 1.0$ . (The answer should be taken to three significant figures)  
b. Calculate the distribution ratio D of salicylic acid extracted by an equal volume of the solvent in water when the total concentration of salicylic acid in the original water is  $0.1\mathrm{mol / L}$  under human physiological conditions. (The answer should be taken to two significant figures)  
c. Calculate the pH of the aqueous system when the distribution ratio  $D = 1$ . (The answer should be taken to one significant figure)

Which of the following statements is correct (calculation error within  $2\%$  can be considered correct):

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The answer to problem a is greater than 400  
C. The answer to question a is less than 360.  
D. The answer to problem b is 0.0021  
E. The answer to problem b is 0.0051

F. The answer to problem c is 5.2.  
G. The answer to problem c is 5.6  
H. The answer to problem c is 5.4.

# Answer

Correct Answer: H

# Detailed Explanation

According to the second-level ionization constant of salicylic acid, the second-level ionization of salicylic acid is extremely weak and can be ignored. Therefore, it is denoted as HA.

$$
