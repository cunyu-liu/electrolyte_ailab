# Question

A and B are metallic elements, with B being a transition metal, and X is a non-metallic element; A, B, and X form a salt with the chemical formula  $A_{2}BX_{4}$ , which does not contain water of crystallization.  $A_{2}BX_{4}$  decomposes upon heating in a hydrogen atmosphere, undergoing an endothermic reaction at  $365^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ; further heating to  $515^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  results in a weight loss of  $12.8\%$ ; continued heating to  $930^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  leads to an additional weight loss of  $51.9\%$ , leaving a residual elemental solid. Heating  $A_{2}BX_{4}$  to  $600^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in a hydrogen atmosphere results in a total weight loss of  $25.7\%$ , with the remaining solid being the alloy  $A_{2}B$ . Calculate the sum of the atomic numbers of the three elements A, B, and X.

A. 133  
B. 160  
C. 134  
D. 114  
E. 142  
F. 82  
G. 73  
H. 105

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

From the second equation of weight loss in the problem, the following relationship can be obtained:

$$
(1 - 25.7\%) * [2M(A) + M(B) + 4M(X)] = 2M(A) + M(B)
$$
