# Question

The following reaction conditions are present:

1. Cu precipitates Bi from  $\mathrm{Bi(OH)}_3$  in the presence of NaCN.  
2. The reaction of a methanol solution of Karl Fischer reagent  $(\mathrm{I}_2: \mathrm{SO}_2: \mathrm{pyridine} = 1:3:10)$  with sodium hydroxide solution yields a hydrogen sulfate ester (ignoring the formation of salts of hydrogen sulfate ester).  
3.  $\mathrm{NaBiO_3}$  oxidizes  $\mathrm{Mn(II)}$  under acidic conditions to produce a purple-red solution.  
4.  $\mathrm{Hg}_2(\mathrm{NO}_3)_2$  undergoes ammonolysis and disproportionation in ammonia water, and the product contains a four-membered ring cation.  
5.  $\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$  reacts with  $\mathrm{PtF}_5$  to produce an ionic compound and release gas.

Regarding the balanced and simplified chemical or ionic equations for the above reactions, which of the following statements is correct:

1. In reaction 1, Cu is oxidized to  $+2$  valence.  
2. In reaction 2, pyridine does not participate in the construction of the reaction equation.  
3. After balancing the ionic equation of reaction 3, the sum of the coefficients on the left side is smaller than that on the right side.  
4. The four-membered ring cation of reaction 4 is composed of four elements.  
5. In reaction 5, more than 1 type of gas is produced.

A. 1,2  
B. 1,4  
C. 1,5

D. 2,3  
E. 2,4  
F. 2,5  
G. 4, 5

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

The following reactions occur:

Reaction 1:  $3\mathrm{Cu} + 6\mathrm{NaCN} + \mathrm{Bi(OH)}_3 = 3\mathrm{NaCu(CN)}_2 + 3\mathrm{NaOH} + \mathrm{Bi}$

Therefore, in reaction 1,  $\mathrm{Cu}$  is oxidized to the  $+1$  oxidation state. Statement 1 is incorrect.
