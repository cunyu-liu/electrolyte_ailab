# Question

An oxyacid A is an inorganic strong acid with a highly stable corresponding anion. X is an extremely unstable, explosive gas that can be prepared using A or its salts as substrates. Two methods of preparation are as follows:

(1) Under cooling, a  $60\%$  aqueous solution of acid A reacts with a pale yellow gas B to produce gas X.  
(2) In hydrogen fluoride at  $-78^{\circ}C$ , the reaction of the cesium salt of A with  $\mathrm{NF_4SbF_6}$  yields a precipitate C and a readily decomposable D; D slowly decomposes into E and X at  $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .

Vapor density determination experiments show that at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the density of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $4.85\mathrm{g / L}$ .

A. The configuration of the corresponding anion of substance A is trigonal pyramidal.  
B. The substance B contains elements from the third period.  
C. The configuration of substance  $\mathbf{E}$  is a trigonal pyramid  
D. The anionic configuration of substance  $\mathbf{C}$  is not octahedral  
E. Only covalent bonds exist in material  $\mathbf{D}$ .  
F. Substance X contains 3 oxygen atoms  
G. The anions in substance A and substance D are not identical.

# Answer

Correct Answer: C

# Detailed Explanation

According to the ideal gas equation  $PM = \rho RT$ , with the default pressure set to 1 atmosphere, the relative molecular mass of substance  $\mathbf{X}$  is calculated to be 118.65.

Since oxygen-containing acid  $\mathbf{A}$  reacts with  $F_{2}$  to produce  $\mathbf{X}$ , it must contain at least  $O$  and  $F$  elements. Based on the relative molecular mass of  $\mathbf{X}$ , a table is constructed for possible combinations of  $O$  and  $F$ . Starting with  $1$ $F$  atom and  $n$ $O$  atoms, it is found that when  $n = 4$ , the corresponding relative molecular mass is 35.65, which is close to that of  $Cl$ . Given that  $\mathbf{A}$  is an inorganic strong acid with a highly stable anion, it is deduced that  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $HClO_{4}$  and  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $FClO_{4}$ .
