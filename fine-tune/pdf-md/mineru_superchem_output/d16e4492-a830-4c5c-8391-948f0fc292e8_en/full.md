# Question

Element  $\mathbf{X}$  can react with  $\mathrm{AgF}$  under strictly dry and low-pressure conditions to obtain an isomer  $\mathbf{A}_1$  of compound  $\mathbf{A}$  with  $C_2$  symmetry. Another isomer  $\mathbf{A}_2$  can be obtained by reacting  $\mathrm{KSO}_3\mathrm{F}$  with  $\mathbf{B}$  in liquid  $\mathrm{SO}_2$ .  $\mathbf{A}_2$  is thermodynamically unstable and can disproportionate into compound  $\mathbf{C}$  and elemental  $\mathbf{X}$  under HF catalysis.  $\mathbf{C}$  can form a 1:1 adduct  $\mathbf{D}$  with  $\mathrm{CsF}$ . Chlorination of  $\mathbf{B}$  in the presence of  $\mathrm{FeCl}_3$  yields compound  $\mathbf{E}$ . Reacting  $\mathbf{E}$  with  $\mathrm{NaF}$  in warm diethyl ether yields a mixture of  $\mathbf{B}$  and  $\mathbf{C}$ . Both  $\mathbf{B}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$  can react with  $\mathrm{NH}_4\mathrm{Cl}$  to produce a cyclic compound  $\mathbf{F}$ .  $\mathbf{F}$  is insoluble in water but soluble in concentrated  $\mathrm{NaOH}$  solution to form two sodium salts  $\mathbf{G}$  and  $\mathbf{H}$ . The mass fractions of  $\mathrm{Na}$  in  $\mathbf{G}$  and  $\mathbf{H}$  are  $29.09\%$  and  $36.48\%$ , respectively. It is known that all substances represented by letters in the question contain  $\mathbf{X}$ . From the following options, choose the one that best fits the meaning of the question.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B.  $\mathbf{A}_1$  and  $\mathbf{A}_2$  have different structures, but both possess a diad axis.  
C.  $\mathbf{A}_{2}$  and  $\mathbf{B}$  have different structures, but their symmetry elements are completely identical.  
D. The structure of  $\mathbf{C}$  and the corresponding structure of the substance in  $\mathbf{D}$  that forms a 1:1 adduct, although slightly different, share the same hybridization mode of the central atom.  
E. The structure of  $\mathbf{C}$  and the corresponding structure of the substance in  $\mathbf{D}$  that forms a 1:1 adduct, although slightly different in the hybridization mode of the central atom, share the same symmetry elements.  
F. The valence states of  $\mathbf{C},\mathbf{E}$  are consistent with those of the cyclic compound  $\mathbf{F}$  formed.  
G. The valence states of  $\mathbf{C}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$ , as well as the valence state of the formed cyclic compound  $\mathbf{F}$ , are all distinct from each other.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

Calculating the molar mass of  $\mathbf{G}$  and  $\mathbf{H}$ :

If  $n = 2$ ,  $M(\mathbf{G}) \approx 158.06\mathrm{g / mol}$ , the mass of the anion part  $\approx 158.06 - 45.98 = 112.08\mathrm{g / mol}$ .

If  $n = 3$ ,  $M(\mathbf{G}) \approx 237.09\mathrm{g / mol}$ , the mass of the anion part  $\approx 237.09 - 68.97 = 168.12\mathrm{g / mol}$ .

Because it is treated in concentrated NaOH solution, and the anion is composed of elements X and O, G is  $\mathrm{Na_2S_2O_3}$ .
