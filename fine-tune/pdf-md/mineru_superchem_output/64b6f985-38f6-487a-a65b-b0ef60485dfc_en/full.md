# Question

Deduce and write the equations for the following chemical reactions (requiring the coefficient ratio to be the simplest integer ratio).

1-1 Phosphorus trifluoride decomposes upon heating, producing a molecule with a tetrahedral structure.  
1-2 Manganese(II) nitrate hexahydrate reacts with acetic anhydride to produce manganese(III) acetate and release a reddish-brown mixed gas.  
1-3 Iodine phosphate  $(\mathrm{IPO}_4)$  decomposes upon contact with water, with the only reduction product being  $\mathrm{I}_2$  and no gas is generated.  
1-4 In dichloromethane,  $\mathrm{Br}_2$  reacts with  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{S}$  in a 1:1 molar ratio, and one of the products is an unstable acid; no element is formed.  
1-5 Reactions in non-aqueous solvents can be used to prepare many novel species. Gold(III) fluorosulfate reacts with CO in fluorosulfonic acid,

resulting in a mononuclear complex molecule with Au being sp hybridized; the reaction also releases  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  gas. Note the form of ions in non-aqueous solvents.

# Answer

Correct Answer: S

# Detailed Explanation

1-1 The disproportionation reaction occurs during the decomposition process. The tetrahedral structure molecule is likely  $\mathrm{P_4}$ . The remaining phosphorus element is oxidized to a stable  $+5$  valence state:

$$
1 0 \mathrm {P F} _ {3} \rightarrow 6 \mathrm {P F} _ {5} + \mathrm {P} _ {4}
$$

The product of the coefficients of all chemical formulas on both sides of the equation is 60, 1 is correct.

1-2 Acetic anhydride  $(\mathrm{CH}_3\mathrm{CO})_2\mathrm{O}$  acts as a dehydrating agent and reaction medium, removing water of hydration and providing an anhydrous environment, promoting the oxidation of nitrate to  $\mathrm{Mn(II)}$  to  $\mathrm{Mn(III)}$ . Nitrate oxidizes divalent manganese to produce reddish-brown  $\mathrm{NO}_2$ . Excess nitric acid is unstable and decomposes under acetic anhydride dehydration. Another component in the mixed gas may be  $\mathrm{O}_2$ :

$$
4 \left[ \mathrm {M n} \left(\mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}\right) _ {6} \right]\left(\mathrm {N O} _ {3}\right) _ {2} + 3 0 \left(\mathrm {C H} _ {3} \mathrm {C O}\right) _ {2} \mathrm {O} \rightarrow 4 \mathrm {M n} \left(\mathrm {O O C C H} _ {3}\right) _ {3} + 8 \mathrm {N O} _ {2} + \mathrm {O} _ {2} + 4 8 \mathrm {C H} _ {3} \mathrm {C O O H}
$$

The product of the coefficients of all chemical formulas on both sides of the equation is 184320, 2 is correct.

1-5 sp-hybridized gold atoms are two-coordinated. With  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  released, it indicates that gold is reduced to the most likely  $+1$  valence state. Considering the difficulty of ion solvation in non-aqueous solvents, a fluorosulfate ligand is needed to directly coordinate and balance the charge, then the other is CO. The oxygen atoms in  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  are provided by the fluorosulfate, forming fluorosulfonic anhydride in a non-aqueous solvent:

$$
\mathrm {A u (S O _ {3} F) _ {3}} + 2 \mathrm {C O} \rightarrow \mathrm {A u (C O)} (\mathrm {S O _ {3} F}) + \mathrm {C O _ {2}} + \mathrm {S _ {2} O _ {5} F _ {2}}
$$

The product of the sum of the number of atoms in each type of molecule on the left and right sides of the equation is 6912, which is correct.
