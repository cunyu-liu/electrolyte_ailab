# Question

There are the following six chemical processes:

1. Under alkaline conditions, reduce  $\mathrm{Cu(NH_3)_4}^{2+}$  with  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  to obtain a colorless complex.  
2. Add excess sodium cyanide to a solution containing copper ions, causing the blue color of the solution to disappear, while releasing a highly toxic gas.  
3. Boil copper(I) chloride in concentrated NaOH solution, where the copper element is completely converted into a red solid.  
4. The hydrolysis disproportionation of  $\mathrm{IF}_3$  yields three common inorganic substances.  
5. Dissolve elemental Pb in sodium hydroxide and write the ionic equation for the reaction.  
6. Treat  $\mathrm{Mn(IO_3)_2}$  with bromine trifluoride at  $800\mathrm{K}$  to prepare a fluoride, with another fluoride also formed in the products.

Write and balance the equations for the above processes, simplifying the coefficients to the smallest integer ratios. For chemical processes in aqueous solutions, use ionic equations. The following statements are correct:

A. Chemical Process 1 produces  $\mathrm{SO}_2$  gas.  
B. Chemical process 2 produces complex ions of copper, where copper has a coordination number of 2.  
C. The gas produced by chemical process 2 is colorless and odorless.  
D. After balancing the chemical equation for Process 3, the sum of coefficients on the left and right sides is unequal.

E. One of the products in Chemical Process 4 exhibits reducing properties and can undergo a displacement reaction with bromine.  
F. Chemical process 5 produces a lead complex in which lead is in the  $+4$  valence state.  
G. One of the products of Chemical Process 6 has a square pyramidal structure, and the sum of the product coefficients on the right side of the equation is 49.  
H. Chemical Process 6 produces a pale yellow gas.  
In the above six processes, three involve gaseous products.  
J. Among the six equations above, there are two reactions where the sum of coefficients on both sides is equal.  
K. The sum of product coefficients in the chemical process 2 equations is the smallest among the 6 equations.  
L. None of the above options is correct
