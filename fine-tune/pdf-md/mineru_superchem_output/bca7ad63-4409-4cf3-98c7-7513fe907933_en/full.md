# Question

Treatment of  $\mathrm{NSF}_3$  with  $\mathrm{BCl}_3$  yields an ionic compound A containing N, B, S, and Cl. In A, the ratio of N to B is 1:1. The theoretical mass fraction of N in the cation is  $9.40\%$  (relative atomic masses are retained to two decimal places), and the anion is a tetrahedral ion. The decomposition of A produces two liquids with the same elemental composition but different colors at room temperature. Regarding the decomposition reaction, which of the following statements is correct?

A. There are a total of four products; the sum of the stoichiometric coefficients after balancing the reaction equation is 8; elemental substances exist among the products.  
B. There are three products in total; the sum of stoichiometric coefficients after balancing the reaction equation is 8; elemental substances are present among the products.  
C. There are four types of products in total; the sum of stoichiometric coefficients after balancing the reaction equation is 4; no elemental substance exists among the products.  
D. There are three types of products in total; the sum of stoichiometric coefficients after balancing the reaction equation is 4; elemental substances exist among the products.

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Since there is no F element, the most probable anion is  $\mathrm{BCl}_4^-$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The anion is  $\mathrm{BCl}_4^-$ .

Assuming the presence of one N in the cation, after removing N, the remaining mass is 135.03, which may correspond to S and Cl elements. Through simple enumeration, it can be found that this precisely matches 2S and 2Cl.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Enumeration calculations show that  $\mathbf{A}$  with  $2\mathrm{S} + 2\mathrm{Cl}$  excluding the N atom best fits the mass fraction.

Thus,  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $[\mathrm{N}(\mathrm{SCl})_2][\mathrm{BCl}_4]$ .

The two liquids with identical elemental compositions correspond to the classic  $\mathrm{SCl}_2$  and  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$ . In a decomposition reaction, it is unlikely for N to form compounds with B and Cl as products; instead,  $\mathrm{N}_2$  should be produced, while B can only combine with Cl to yield  $\mathrm{BCl}_3$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The products are  $\mathrm{SCl}_2$ ,  $\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2$ ,  $\mathrm{N}_2$ , and  $\mathrm{BCl}_3$

Therefore, the equation is:  $2[\mathrm{N}(\mathrm{SCl})_2][\mathrm{BCl}_4] = \mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{Cl}_2 + 2\mathrm{SCl}_2 + 2\mathrm{BCl}_3$ , and the answer is A.
