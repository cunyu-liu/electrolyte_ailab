# Question

$\mathbf{N}$  is a rare element whose mineral resources are sparsely distributed, often occurring as associated minerals or as impurities in other minerals. Elemental  $\mathbf{N}$  is insoluble in water or hydrochloric acid, but can be oxidized to  $\mathbf{A}$  by fuming nitric acid;  $\mathbf{A}$  is a white amorphous powder, slightly soluble in water, and easily dehydrated; compound  $\mathbf{B}$  is obtained after complete dehydration of  $\mathbf{A}$ .  $\mathbf{B}$  reacts with carbon tetrachloride at  $723\mathrm{K}$  to give a white hygroscopic solid  $\mathbf{C}$ .  $\mathbf{C}$  combines with elemental  $\mathbf{N}$  to form  $\mathbf{D}$ ;  $\mathbf{D}$  can also be obtained by reacting elemental  $\mathbf{N}$  with sulfuryl chloride at  $310\mathrm{K}$  in a  $1:1$  molar ratio.  $\mathbf{D}$  reacts with liquid nonmetallic element  $\mathbf{E}$  to obtain a yellow solid  $\mathbf{F}$ , in which the mass fraction of  $\mathbf{N}$  is  $35.61\%$ . When isolated from air, elemental  $\mathbf{N}$  reacts with molten KCN to produce  $\mathbf{G}$  and release gas  $\mathbf{H}$ ;  $\mathbf{G}$  reacts with acid to produce a highly toxic, foul-smelling gas  $\mathbf{I}$ ;  $\mathbf{I}$  spontaneously decomposes to elemental  $\mathbf{N}$  at  $273\mathrm{K}$ .

The following options are correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. Element  $\mathbf{N}$  is a subgroup metal element.  
C. Element  $\mathbf{N}$  is a main group metal element.  
D. The number of binary compounds in  $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{I}$  is 5  
E. The number of binary compounds in  $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{I}$  is 7.  
F.  $\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{I}$  contains 4 substances whose molecular formula contains greater than or equal to 4 atoms.  
G.  $\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{I}$  contains 4 substances with molecular formulas containing 3 atoms.

# Answer

Correct Answer: G

# Detailed Explanation

N is a rare element, insoluble in hydrochloric acid and water, exhibiting properties of an inert metal or a high-period main group nonmetal element;

When  $\mathbf{N}$  is fused with KCN, a gas is produced, then the remaining G is basically certain to be a potassium salt containing  $\mathbf{N}$ ; therefore,  $\mathbf{N}$  is likely negatively charged, excluding metallic elements.
