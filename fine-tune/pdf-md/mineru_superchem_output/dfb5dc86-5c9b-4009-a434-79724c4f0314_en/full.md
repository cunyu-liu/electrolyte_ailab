# Question

Sodium azide is a commonly used reagent in organic chemistry. In the laboratory, the purity of sodium azide is often determined by titration analysis, and the specific steps are as follows:

1. Accurately weigh  $0.4620\mathrm{g}$  of dried arsenic trioxide into a  $250~\mathrm{mL}$  beaker, add  $100~\mathrm{mL}$  of  $100\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  sodium hydroxide solution to dissolve it, then transfer to a  $1\textrm{L}$  volumetric flask. Add  $250~\mathrm{mL}$  of water and  $250~\mathrm{mL}$  of  $3\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  sulfuric acid, and dilute to the mark.  
2. Precisely pipette  $50.00\mathrm{mL}$  of arsenic trioxide standard solution into a  $500~\mathrm{mL}$  conical flask, add 3 drops of  $2.5\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  osmium tetroxide solution, then add  $25.00\mathrm{mL}$  of the ceric ammonium nitrate solution to be standardized. Dilute with water to  $150~\mathrm{mL}$ , add  $30~\mathrm{mL}$  of  $3\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  sulfuric acid and 2 drops of ferroin indicator, and titrate with the ferrous ammonium sulfate solution to be standardized until the endpoint is reached. The titration consumes  $39.90~\mathrm{mL}$  of ferrous ammonium sulfate standard solution.  
3. Using all the reagents mentioned above except arsenic trioxide, the titration consumes  $49.18~\mathrm{mL}$  of ferrous ammonium sulfate standard solution.  
4. Accurately weigh  $2.7233\mathrm{g}$  of sodium azide sample, dissolve it in water, and dilute to  $250~\mathrm{mL}$ . Pipette  $20.00~\mathrm{mL}$  of this solution into a  $500~\mathrm{mL}$  conical flask, add  $40.00~\mathrm{mL}$  of ceric ammonium nitrate standard solution, and allow the reaction to proceed in a cold water bath. After the reaction is complete, dilute to  $150~\mathrm{mL}$ , add  $30~\mathrm{mL}~3~\mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1}$  sulfuric acid and 2 drops of ferroin indicator, then titrate with ferrous ammonium sulfate standard solution until the endpoint is reached. The titration consumes  $15.32~\mathrm{mL}$  of ferrous ammonium sulfate standard solution.

Regarding this analytical process, the following statements are made:

A. Dried arsenic trioxide can be directly used to prepare the standard test solution.  
B. The osmium tetroxide added undergoes a change in oxidation state during the analysis.  
C. The ionic equation for the chemical reaction occurring after adding ceric ammonium nitrate in step 2, when balanced with the simplest integer coefficients, has a sum of product coefficients equal to 5.

D. The concentration of the prepared arsenic trioxide solution is  $0.002335 \, \mathrm{mol/L}$ .  
E. The concentration of the ceric ammonium nitrate solution is  $0.09900\mathrm{mol / L}$  
F. The concentration of ferrous ammonium sulfate is  $0.04032\mathrm{mol / L}$  
G. The content of sodium azide is  $95.16\%$ .

Among these statements, the number of correct ones is (for calculation-related options, an error less than  $1\%$  is considered correct).

A. 0  
B. 1  
C. 2  
D. 3  
E. 4  
F. 5  
G. 6  
H. 7

# Answer
