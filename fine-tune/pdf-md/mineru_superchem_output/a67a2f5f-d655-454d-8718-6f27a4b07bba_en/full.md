# Question

Thermogravimetric experiments were performed on the synthesized potassium tris(oxalate)ferrate(III) crystals. The thermogravimetric curve in the range of  $0 - 650^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  showed four weight loss peaks: a weight loss of  $10.71\%$  at  $99^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , a weight loss of  $8.90\%$  at  $287^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , a weight loss of  $14.85\%$  at  $435^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and a weight loss of  $8.83\%$  at  $450 - 580^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .

Due to the systematic errors present in thermogravimetric experiments, there may be some differences between the theoretical and experimental weight losses. Based on the thermogravimetric data, the four-step chemical equations for the thermal decomposition of potassium tris(oxalate)ferrate(III) crystals can be written (with the simplest integer ratio coefficients). Regarding these four weight loss peaks and the corresponding decomposition chemical equations, the following options are correct:

A. The difference between the theoretical weight loss value and the experimental weight loss value of the first weight loss peak is  $0.39\%$ .  
B. The decomposition chemical equation corresponding to the second weight loss peak has two gaseous products.  
C. The sum of the stoichiometric coefficients of the products in the decomposition chemical equation corresponding to the second weight loss peak is an odd number.  
D. The decomposition chemical equation corresponding to the third weight loss peak has only one gaseous product.  
E. The decomposition chemical equation corresponding to the third weight loss peak has two kinds of oxide products.  
F. The theoretical weight loss value of the fourth weight loss peak is less than the experimental weight loss value.

G. The chemical formula of the final remaining solid product in the fourth weight loss peak contains five atoms.

# Answer

Correct Answer: C
