# Question

Heating powdered metallic thorium in a nitrogen atmosphere to above  $500^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  produces a reddish-brown powder. This reddish-brown powder is a thorium nitride, which exhibits good diamagnetism and electrical insulation. In the nitride crystal, both  $Th$  and  $N$  atoms have two chemical environments. The  $Th$  atoms are in a close-packed arrangement, with a stacking sequence of ...ABABCACABABCAC...; all tetrahedral voids or all octahedral voids between adjacent close-packed layers are occupied by  $N$  atoms; the ratio of these two types of  $N$  atoms is  $1:1$ . The unit cell parameters are  $a = b = 387 \mathrm{pm}$ ;  $c = 2739 \mathrm{pm}$ ;  $\alpha = \beta = 90^{\circ}$ ;  $\gamma = 120^{\circ}$ . Which of the following options is correct?

A.  $\frac{1}{2}$  of the octahedral voids are occupied,  $\frac{1}{2}$  of the tetrahedral voids are occupied.  
B. The density of the crystal is  $7.030 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{cm}^{3}$  
C. Crystals have Th atoms with coordination number 6 only.  
D. There are two types of Th atoms with different coordination numbers in the crystal, which are 6 and 7, respectively. The number of atoms with the two different coordination numbers is the same.  
E. The chemical composition of the smallest unit of the crystal that repeats with translational periodicity is 1 formula unit.  
F. The chemical composition of the smallest unit of this crystal that repeats with translational periodicity is 3 chemical formulas.  
G. There are exactly 3 correct answers among options A to F.  
H. Only 2 of options  $\mathbf{A}$  to  $\mathbf{F}$  are correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

Because the ratio of the number of octahedral voids to tetrahedral voids in two adjacent close-packed layers is  $1:2$ , the ratio of the number of nitrogen atoms filling the two types of voids is  $1:1$ ,

There are 9 layers of  $Th$  atoms in the unit cell, corresponding to 9 close-packed layers. Each layer can be filled with one octahedral void or two tetrahedral voids. Assume that  $x$  layers are filled with octahedral voids and  $(9 - x)$  layers are filled with tetrahedral voids. Therefore,  $1 \times x = 2 \times (9 - x)$ , solving for  $x = 6$ , there are 6 octahedral void layers (6 octahedral voids filled), and 3 tetrahedral void layers (6 tetrahedral voids filled).

Therefore,  $2/3$  of the octahedral void layers and  $1/3$  of the tetrahedral void layers are filled with nitrogen atoms. There are 9 Th atoms in the unit cell, forming 9 octahedral voids and 18 tetrahedral voids;  $N$  fills 6 octahedral voids and 6 tetrahedral voids, that is, there are 12  $N$  atoms in the unit cell, and the chemical formula of the crystal is  $Th_{3}N_{4}$ .

Then 3  $Th$  will be located between two layers of  $N$ -filled octahedral void layers, with a coordination number of 6, and the other 6  $Th$  will be located between a layer of  $N$ -filled octahedral void layers and a layer of  $N$ -filled tetrahedral void layers, with a coordination number of 7. Therefore, there are 3  $Th$  atoms with a coordination number of 6 and 6  $Th$  atoms with a coordination number of 7.
