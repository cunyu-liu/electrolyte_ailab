# Question

Diverse novel species containing peroxo bonds are prepared using different methods.

Compound A can be prepared by bubbling  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  into a concentrated aqueous hydrogen peroxide solution containing KOH, crystallizing, while controlling the temperature at  $-5^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$  to  $-20^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ .

Compound B can be prepared by adding potassium bicarbonate to saturation in a  $30\%$  aqueous hydrogen peroxide solution, then adding a certain amount of ethanol, and cooling to crystallize, while controlling the temperature at  $-10^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to  $-15^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .

Crystal structure determination and thermal analysis indicate that:

In the crystal of  $\mathbf{A}$ , there is only one type of anion;

In the crystal of  $\mathbf{B}$ , there is only one type of cation, one type of anion, and one type of neutral molecule.

A loses  $30.3\%$  of its weight when heated above  $160^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  (Reaction 1);

B loses  $53.9\%$  of its weight when heated above  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  (Reaction 2).

The following statements are made:

1. The anion structure of  $\mathbf{A}$  contains a mirror plane.

2. The ratio of the number of cations, anions, and neutral molecules in the composition of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $1:1:1$ .

3. The solid products of the thermogravimetric analysis of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B}$  are consistent.

4. The coefficient of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  in the equation of Reaction 2 (with coprime integer coefficients) is 3. (If there is no  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  in the reaction equation, this statement is considered correct)

5. Arranging  $\mathbf{A},\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{O}_2,\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$  according to the length of the peroxo bond, the order is  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{O}_2 > \mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2 > \mathrm{A}$

What is the sum of the serial numbers of the correct statements?

A. 2  
B. 3  
C. 5  
D. 6  
E. 8  
F. 9  
G. 11  
H. 12  
1. 14  
J. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

First consider the final product of thermogravimetric analysis.  $\mathrm{KHCO}_3$  decomposes at  $120^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , so the final solid product of thermogravimetric analysis is  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$ . The solid products of thermogravimetric analysis of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B}$  are consistent, so statement 3 is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The final solid products of thermogravimetric analysis of  $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B}$  are both  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$

Assume  $\mathbf{A}$  has 2 potassium atoms, then its molecular weight is  $138.31 \div (1 - 0.303) = 198.44$ . Combining the preparation conditions of strong alkalinity, we know that  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{O}_6$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

A is  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{O}_6$

Its anion structure is two  $\left[\mathrm{CO}_3^-\right]$  groups connected by a peroxo bond, similar to the structure of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$ , with a twofold rotation axis, but no mirror plane or center of symmetry, so statement 1 is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

The anion structure of  $\mathbf{A}$  does not have a mirror plane

Similarly, assume  $\mathbf{B}$  has 2 potassium atoms, then its molecular weight is  $138.31 \div (1 - 0.539) = 300.02$ . Its cation is  $2\mathrm{K}^{+}$ , and according to the preparation process, it is inferred that the anion is  $2\mathrm{HOOCO}_{2}^{-}$ , and the remaining molecular weight of about 68 is  $2\mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{O}_{2}$ , which is consistent with the description of the question, so  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $\mathrm{KHCO}_{4} \cdot \mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{O}_{2}$ . Other compositions cannot satisfy the thermogravimetric data. The ratio of the number of cations, anions, and neutral molecules in the composition of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $1:1:1$ , so statement 2 is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

B is  $\mathrm{KHCO}_4\cdot \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$

Reaction 2 is the decomposition of  $\mathrm{KHCO}_4\cdot \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$  that is  $2[\mathrm{KHCO}_4\cdot \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2]\rightarrow \mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{CO}_3 + \mathrm{CO}_2 + 3\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O} + 2\mathrm{O}_2.$  The coefficient of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  is 3, so statement 4 is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

$$
2 \left[ \mathrm {K H C O} _ {4} \cdot \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} \right]\rightarrow \mathrm {K} _ {2} \mathrm {C O} _ {3} + \mathrm {C O} _ {2} + 3 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + 2 \mathrm {O} _ {2}
$$

The negative charges on the two oxygen atoms in  $\mathrm{O}_2^{2-}$  have repulsive force, so the peroxo bond is the longest; in  $\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$ , the lone pair electrons of oxygen are hyperconjugated with the antibonding orbital of the other oxygen-fluorine bond, which enhances the bond order of the peroxo bond, so the peroxo bond is the shortest; the hyperconjugation effect brought by the  $\mathrm{CO}_2^-$  group in  $\mathbf{A}$  is not as good as  $\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$ . Therefore, the order of the peroxo bond length is  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{O}_2 > \mathbf{A} > \mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$ , so statement 5 is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

In  $\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$ , the lone pair electrons of oxygen are hyperconjugated with the antibonding orbital of the other oxygen-fluorine bond, enhancing the bond order of the peroxo bond

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Peroxo bond length order  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{O}_2 > \mathbf{A} > \mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$

The correct statements are 2, 3, and 4, so the answer is option F.
