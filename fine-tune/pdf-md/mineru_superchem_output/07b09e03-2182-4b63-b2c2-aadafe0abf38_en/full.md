Proposition 2: Let  $\omega_{x}$  be the mass fraction of the element with the lowest mass fraction in the species with the highest stoichiometric number among the reactants; let  $\omega_{y}$  be the mass fraction of the element with the lowest mass fraction in the species with the lowest stoichiometric number among the products, then  $|\omega_{x} - \omega_{y}| < 3\%$ .

Reactant coefficients: 8, 4, 18. The species with the highest stoichiometric number is  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  (coefficient is 18).

The molar mass of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  is  $M \approx 18.016\mathrm{g / mol}$ .

$$

Product coefficients: 2, 6, 8, 25. The species with the lowest stoichiometric number is  $\mathrm{Ni}_2\mathrm{B}$  (coefficient is 2).
