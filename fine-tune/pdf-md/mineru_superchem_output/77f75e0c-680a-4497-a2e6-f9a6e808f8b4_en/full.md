# Question

People have discovered that a certain substance  $\mathbf{M}$  can be prepared through the following reactions:

$$
\mathbf {A} (1) + 3 \mathbf {B} (s) = \mathbf {M} (s) + 3 \mathbf {C} (s) + 4 \mathbf {D} (g)
$$

$$
\mathbf {A} (1) + \mathbf {E} (s) = \mathbf {M} (s) + \mathbf {C} (s) + 2 \mathbf {F} (g)
$$

$$
2 \mathbf {G} (\mathrm {s}) + \mathbf {H} (\mathrm {s}) = 2 \mathbf {M} (\mathrm {s}) + \mathbf {l} (\mathrm {s}) + 4 \mathbf {J} (\mathrm {g})
$$

$$
5 \mathbf {G} (\mathrm {s}) + 2 \mathbf {H} (\mathrm {s}) + \mathbf {B} (\mathrm {s}) = 4 \mathbf {M} (\mathrm {s}) + 2 \mathbf {I} (\mathrm {s}) + 1 0 \mathbf {J} (\mathrm {g}) + \mathbf {K} (\mathrm {s}) + 2 \mathbf {D} (\mathrm {g})
$$

$\mathbf{M}$  is composed of two short-period elements  $\mathbf{X}$  and  $\mathbf{Y}$  (the atomic number of  $\mathbf{X}$  is less than that of  $\mathbf{Y}$ ) and does not contain hydrogen. In  $\mathbf{A}$ ,  $\omega(\mathrm{Br}) = 95.7\%$ ,  $\mathbf{B}$  is a sodium salt that is relatively stable at room temperature but decomposes into a large amount of gas upon heating.  $\mathbf{E}$  is slightly more stable than  $\mathbf{B}$ , where  $\omega(\mathrm{Na}) = 58.9\%$ .  $\mathbf{G}$  decomposes upon heating to release an acidic gas and a basic gas, and  $\mathbf{G}$  contains a coordinate covalent bond.  $\mathbf{I}$  is a Mg-containing salt,  $\omega(\mathrm{Mg}) = 25.5\%$ .

The following statements about the 12 substances  $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{K},\mathbf{M}$  are correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. 12 kinds of substances contain a total of 8 kinds of elements.  
C. 12 kinds of substances contain a total of 9 kinds of elements.  
D. Among 12 substances, there are 9 binary compounds.  
E. Of 12 substances, there are 3 elements.  
F. Of the 12 substances, 4 are gases at room temperature and pressure.  
G. Of the 12 substances, three have a molecular weight exceeding 100.  
H. Of the 12 substances, 3 have a molecular weight below 30.

# Answer

Correct Answer: H

# Detailed Explanation

The main body starts from  $\mathbf{A}$  with  $\omega (\mathrm{Br}) = 95.7\%$

Since the mass fraction is large,  $\mathbf{A}$  is likely to contain multiple Br. Assume it contains n:

If  $n$  is 1, the remaining molecular weight  $= 79.9 / 0.957 - 79.9 = 3.6$ , no reasonable element.

If  $n$  is 2, the remaining molecular weight  $= (2 \times 79.9) / 0.957 - 2 \times 79.9 = 7.2$ , no reasonable element.

If  $n$  is 3, the remaining molecular weight  $= (3 \times 79.9) / 0.957 - 3 \times 79.9 = 10.8$ , which is element B.

If  $n$  is 4, the remaining molecular weight  $= (3 \times 79.9) / 0.957 - 4 \times 79.9 = 14.3$ , no reasonable element.

If  $n$  is 5, the remaining molecular weight  $= (5 \times 79.9) / 0.957 - 5 \times 79.9 = 18$ , no reasonable element. A larger  $n$  is unlikely to exist.

Therefore,  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{BBr}_3$ ;
