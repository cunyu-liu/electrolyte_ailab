In nature, potassium has three isotopes,  $^{39}\mathrm{K}$ ,  $^{40}\mathrm{K}$  and  $^{41}\mathrm{K}$ , with abundances of  $93.2581\%$ ,  $0.0117\%$  and  $6.7302\%$  respectively.  $^{39}\mathrm{K}$  and  $^{41}\mathrm{K}$  are stable nuclides; while  $^{40}\mathrm{K}$  is radioactive, and its decay may produce  $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$  (partial half-life  $t_1 = 1.397 \times 10^9 \, \mathrm{y}$ ) or  $^{40}\mathrm{Ar}$  (partial half-life  $t_2 = 1.193 \times 10^{10} \, \mathrm{y}$ ). Granite is a rock formed by the cooling and solidification of magma from volcanic eruptions. When granite forms, it seals a very small amount of atmosphere inside the rock.
