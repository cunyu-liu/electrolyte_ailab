# Question

The second-period elements  $\mathbf{X}$  and element  $\mathbf{Y}$  react at  $20^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to obtain the common Lewis acid molecule  $\mathbf{A}$ . When  $\mathbf{A}$  passes through the crystal of  $\mathbf{X}$  at  $1850^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and a pressure lower than  $133\mathrm{Pa}$ , a reactive gas  $\mathbf{B}$  is obtained, which is isoelectronic with CO.  $\mathbf{B}$  and  $\mathbf{A}$  co-condense at  $-196^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in a 2:1 stoichiometric ratio to obtain compound  $\mathbf{C}$ .  $\mathbf{C}$  is unstable and disproportionate above  $-50^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to form the yellow compound  $\mathbf{D}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$  in a ratio of 1:2.  $\mathbf{E}$  exists in two conformations in the solid and gaseous states.  $\mathbf{D}$  has a similar structure and bonding mode to the hydride of element  $\mathbf{X}$ . Reacting  $\mathbf{D}$  with CO can cause symmetrical cleavage to obtain the adduct  $\mathbf{F}$ , and there is a  $C_3$  axis in  $\mathbf{F}$ .

According to the above description, which of the following statements is incorrect (including calculations, error within  $1\%$ )?

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. Y element is a pale yellow gas  
C. The relative molecular mass of C is approximately 127  
D. There exist 3 chemical environments of  $\mathbf{X}$  in  $\mathbf{D}$  
E. The simplest formula of  $\mathbf{E}$  is  $\mathbf{X}\mathbf{Y}_2$  
F. The mass fraction of C in F is  $6.5\%$

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

For a second-period element to form a common Lewis acid, then  $\mathbf{A}$  can only be  $\mathrm{BF}_3$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

A is  $\mathrm{BF}_3$

If  $\mathbf{B}$  and  $\mathrm{CO}$  are isoelectronic, then  $\mathbf{B}$  can only be BF. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  is B, and  $\mathbf{Y}$  is F. The simple substance of  $\mathbf{Y}$  is a pale yello gas, so B is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

B is BF

1 PTS

X is B, Y is F

According to the question stem "B and A co-condense at  $-196^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  with a stoichiometric ratio of 2:1 to obtain compound C", C should be  $\mathrm{B}_3\mathrm{F}_5$ , with a relative molecular mass of approximately 127, so C is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

C is  $\mathrm{B}_3\mathrm{F}_5$

Since "  $\mathbf{E}$  exists in two conformations in the solid and gaseous states", it can be determined that  $\mathbf{E}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_2\mathrm{F}_4$ , and the two conformations are planar and trigonal pyramidal, respectively. The simplest formula is  $\mathbf{X}\mathbf{Y}_2$ , so  $\mathbf{E}$  is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

E is  $\mathrm{B}_2\mathrm{F}_4$

According to the question stem "D has a similar structure and bonding mode to the hydride of element X", there should be a three-center two-electron bond in D. Under this condition, B has 3 forms of existence: terminal  $\mathrm{BF}_2$ , bridging  $\mathrm{BF}_2$ , and central B. Therefore, for any structure  $\mathrm{B_a(BF_2)_b}$ , F must be an even number. Let there be  $x$  terminal  $\mathrm{BF}_2$  groups, which should satisfy  $4a = x + 2(b - x)$ , i.e.,  $4a = 2b - x$ . It should be ensured that  $0 < 2a < b$  and  $x < b$  and  $a < b - x$ , i.e.,  $0 < 2a < b < 3a$ .

To balance the equation with a product stoichiometric ratio of 1:2, when the reactant stoichiometric number is 2,  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_2\mathrm{F}_2$ , i.e.,  $\mathrm{B}_0(\mathrm{BF}_2)_2$ , which cannot be satisfied; when the reactant stoichiometric number is 4,  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_8\mathrm{F}_{12}$ , i.e.,  $\mathrm{B}_2(\mathrm{BF}_2)_6$ , which is feasible; when the reactant stoichiometric number is 6,  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_{14}\mathrm{F}_{22}$ , i.e.,  $\mathrm{B}_3(\mathrm{BF}_2)_{11}$ , which cannot be satisfied; when the reactant stoichiometric number is 8,  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_{20}\mathrm{F}_{32}$ , i.e.,  $\mathrm{B}_4(\mathrm{BF}_2)_{16}$ , which cannot be satisfied; higher stoichiometric numbers also cannot satisfy the conditions. Therefore,  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{B}_8\mathrm{F}_{12}$ , i.e.,  $\mathrm{B}_2(\mathrm{BF}_2)_6$ , which has 2 central B atoms, 4 terminal  $\mathrm{BF}_2$  groups, and 2 bridging  $\mathrm{BF}_2$  groups, and there are 3 kinds of chemical environments of  $\mathbf{X}$ , so  $\mathbf{D}$  is correct.
