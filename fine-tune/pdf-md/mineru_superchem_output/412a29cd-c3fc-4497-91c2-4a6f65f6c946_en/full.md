# Question

A student conducted a titration analysis on a sample of  $K_{3}Fe(C_{2}O_{4})_{3} \cdot 3H_{2}O$ . The method involved: weighing an appropriate amount of the sample, dissolving it in dilute sulfuric acid, titrating it with  $KMnO_{4}$  under heating until the endpoint was reached, then reducing the above titrated solution with  $Zn$  until no color reaction with  $KSCN$  was observed. After filtering out the excess  $Zn$ , the solution was titrated with  $K_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  until the endpoint was reached. Based on the results of these two titrations, the molar ratio of  $Fe$  to  $C_{2}O_{4}^{2-}$  in the sample could be calculated.

The student might have committed certain improper operations during the experiment:

1. The sample was left in the air for too long before titration, leading to efflorescence.  
2. The sample was exposed to sunlight before titration, causing a color change.  
3. A small amount of the solution splashed out while dissolving the sample.  
4. A transparent burette was used instead of a brown one for the  $KMnO_4$  titration.  
5. Inadequate washing after filtering the  $Zn$ .

Assuming no other errors beyond the issues mentioned above, how many of these improper operations could potentially result in an elevated molar ratio value of  $n(Fe) / n(C_2O_4^{2-})$ ?

A. 0  
B. 1  
C. 2  
D. 3  
E. 4

F. 5

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

Titration of  $C_2O_4^{2-}$  content with  $KMnO_4$ .

1 PTS

$KMnO_{4}$  titration of  $C_2O_4^{2-}$  content

Titration of  $Fe^{2+}$  (i.e., Fe content) with  $K_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$K_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  titration of Fe content

If the ratio  $n(Fe) / n(C_2O_4^{2-})$  is high, it means the actual amount of  $KMnO_4$  used is high or the actual amount of  $K_2Cr_2O_7$  used is low.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Actual amount of  $KMnO_4$  used is high or actual amount of  $K_{2}Cr_{2}O_{7}$  used is low

Operation 1 does not affect the ratio. Weathering only affects the water of crystallization and does not influence the redox titration part.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Operation 1 does not affect the ratio

Operation 2 does not affect the ratio.

The photolysis reaction is

$$
2 K _ {3} F e \left(C _ {2} O _ {4}\right) _ {3} \cdot 3 H _ {2} O = 3 K _ {2} C _ {2} O _ {4} + 2 F e C _ {2} O _ {4} + 2 C O _ {2} + 6 H _ {2} O
$$

Although it superficially reduces the amount of  $C_2O_4^{2-}$ , since  $Fe^{2+}$  is generated, the total electron transfer number remains unchanged during  $KMnO_4$  titration, and thus the calculated  $C_2O_4^{2-}$  content is unaffected.

Operation 2 does not affect the ratio

Operation 3 does not affect the ratio. If the solution splashes out before titration, the values for both titrations decrease proportionally, leaving the ratio unaffected.

1 PTS

Operation 3 does not affect the ratio

Operation 4 causes the ratio to decrease. Photodecomposition of  $KMnO_4$  reduces its concentration, leading to higher titration usage.

1 PTS

Operation 4 causes the ratio to decrease

Operation 5 causes the ratio to decrease. If the residue is not fully washed,  $Fe^{2+}$  does not completely enter the next titration step, resulting in a lower measured Fe content.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Operation 5 causes the ratio to decrease

In conclusion, no operation causes the ratio to increase.
