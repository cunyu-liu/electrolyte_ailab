# Question

The transition metal center  $\mathbf{A}$  located in the d-block or ds-block can form a complex structure of the form  $[\mathbf{AB}_6\mathbf{C}_{12}]$ , where the order of its point group is 24, and there are no  $C_4$  or  $S_4$  axes present. # Answer

Correct Answer: L

# Detailed Explanation

The point groups of order 24 include

$$
C _ {2 4}, C _ {1 2 \mathrm {v}}, C _ {1 2 \mathrm {h}}, D _ {1 2}, D _ {6 \mathrm {h}}, D _ {6 \mathrm {d}}, S _ {2 4}, T _ {\mathrm {h}}, T _ {\mathrm {d}}, O
$$

and others. Among these, only the two point groups  $D_{6\mathrm{h}}$  and  $T_{\mathrm{h}}$  satisfy the condition of having no  $C_4$  or  $S_4$  axis.

If the point group is  $D_{6\mathrm{h}}$ , then with  $\mathbf{A}$  at the center, six  $\mathbf{B}$  atoms form a regular planar hexagon, with each  $\mathbf{B}$  atom connected to two  $\mathbf{C}$  atoms. The  $\mathbf{C}$  atoms are symmetrically distributed above and below the plane, forming a convex polyhedron that is a hexagonal prism.
