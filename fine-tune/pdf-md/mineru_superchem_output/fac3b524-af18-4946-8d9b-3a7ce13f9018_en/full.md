# Question

Heating the hydrated salt  $\mathbf{A}$  of an active main group metal  $\mathbf{M}$  under an air atmosphere begins to decompose at  $471.2\mathrm{K}$ , forming compound  $\mathbf{B}$  with a mass loss of  $5.39\%$  (theoretical mass fraction, same below; relative atomic masses are retained to two decimal places). Continued heating to  $660.2\mathrm{K}$  produces compound  $\mathbf{C}$  with a mass loss of  $34.74\%$ . Finally, decomposition begins at  $968.7\mathrm{K}$  to form compound  $\mathbf{D}$ , releasing a colorless and odorless gas  $\mathbf{X}$ , with a mass loss of  $26.32\%$ . All mass loss percentages are based on  $\mathbf{A}$ . Regarding  $\mathbf{A} \sim \mathbf{D}$ , which of the following statements is correct:

A. The chemical formula of A is  $\mathrm{B}\cdot 2\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  
B. The chemical formula of A is  $\mathbf{B}\cdot \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  
C. B reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce a colorless and odorless acid.  
D. C does not produce gas when reacting with hydrochloric acid  
E. D does not react with water  
F. D reacts with hydrochloric acid to yield a toxic salt.  
G. Passing  $\mathbf{X}$  into an aqueous solution of  $\mathbf{B}$  yields  $\mathbf{C}$  
H. The thermal decomposition of  $\mathbf{B}$  into  $\mathbf{C}$  can be regarded as the release of compound  $\mathbf{E}$ , whose ideal point group is  $\mathrm{C}_{2\mathrm{v}}$ .

1. All other options are incorrect

# Answer

Correct Answer: H

# Detailed Explanation

Highly reactive main group metals indicate that  $\mathbf{M}$  is an alkali metal or alkaline earth metal.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

M is an alkali metal or alkaline earth metal

Oxides of alkali metals or alkaline earth metals are difficult to decompose, so  $\mathbf{X}$  is carbon dioxide,  $\mathbf{C}$  is carbonate, and  $\mathbf{D}$  is oxide.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$\mathbf{X}$  is carbon dioxide,  $\mathbf{C}$  is carbonate, and  $\mathbf{D}$  is oxide

$$
\frac {2 M + 1 6}{4 4 . 0 1} = \frac {0 . 3 3 5 5}{0 . 2 6 3 2}
$$

or

$$
\frac {M + 1 6}{4 4 . 0 1} = \frac {0 . 3 3 5 5}{0 . 2 6 3 2}
$$

Solving gives  $\mathbf{M} = 40.09$ ,  $\mathbf{M} = \mathrm{Ca}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{Ca}$

A mass loss of  $34.74\%$  corresponds to 58.09, which is  $\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$  or  $\mathrm{C}_3\mathrm{H}_6\mathrm{O}$ ; the latter is reasonable, meaning the anion is acetate. Consequently,  $\mathbf{A}$  has 0.5 water molecules of crystallization, so  $\mathbf{A} = (\mathrm{CH}_3\mathrm{CO}_2)_2\mathrm{Ca} \cdot 0.5\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ ,  $\mathbf{B} = (\mathrm{CH}_3\mathrm{CO}_2)_2\mathrm{Ca}$ ,  $\mathbf{C} = \mathrm{CaCO}_3$ ,  $\mathbf{D} = \mathrm{CaO}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

$$
\mathbf {A} = \left(\mathrm {C H} _ {3} \mathrm {C O} _ {2}\right) _ {2} \mathrm {C a} \cdot 0. 5 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O}, \mathbf {B} = \left(\mathrm {C H} _ {3} \mathrm {C O} _ {2}\right) _ {2} \mathrm {C a}, \mathbf {C} = \mathrm {C a C O} _ {3}, \mathbf {D} = \mathrm {C a O}
$$

Options A and B are incorrect; B reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce the pungent-smelling acetic acid, so C is incorrect; C reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce carbon dioxide, so D is incorrect; D reacts with water, so E is incorrect; D reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce non-toxic calcium chloride, so F is incorrect; B cannot react with  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  to form C, so G is incorrect.

B decomposes into  $\mathbf{C}$  and loses acetone, which belongs to the  $\mathrm{C}_{2\mathrm{v}}$  point group, so H is correct.
