# Question

There are five chemical/ionic equations as follows:

1. Aluminum metal, sodium azide, and ammonium chloride react at high temperature to produce aluminum nitride AlN, with hydrogen gas as the sole gaseous product.  
2. Carbon atoms generated by low-temperature arc discharge react with excess  $\mathrm{B}_2\mathrm{Cl}_4$ , with no cleavage of B - Cl bonds during the reaction.  
3.  $\mathrm{CaAl_2O_4}$  undergoes a high-temperature hydrothermal reaction with phosphoric acid, yielding hydroxylapatite  $\mathrm{Ca}_{5}(\mathrm{PO}_{4})_{3}\mathrm{OH}$  and aluminum phosphate.  
4. Tetraethoxysilane decomposes at  $1023\mathrm{K}$  to form silicon dioxide.  
5. Assuming the carbon source is  $\mathrm{HCO}_3^-$  and the nitrogen source is  $\mathrm{NH}_4^+$ , these react with hydrogen gas in high-pressure seawater to produce glutamic acid.

After balancing these five equations with the simplest whole-number coefficients, for each equation, let the sum of the product coefficients be  $\mathbf{a}$  and the number of product species be  $\mathbf{b}$ . The number of equations among these five where both  $\mathbf{a}$  and  $\mathbf{b}$  are odd is:

A. 0  
B. 1  
C. 2  
D. 3  
E. 4  
F. 5

G. 6

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

Equation 1: This is clearly a redox reaction, where sodium azide acts as a typical oxidizing agent for elemental aluminum, while itself being reduced. Since the only gaseous product is hydrogen, the formation of nitrogen gas and ammonia is not considered.

Therefore, the sum of the product coefficients is 7, and the number of products is 3, both being odd.

Equation 2: The structure of  $\mathrm{B}_2\mathrm{Cl}_4$  consists of two  $\mathrm{BCl}_2$  units connected by a B-B bond. Since the B-CI bonds do not break during the reaction, it indicates that  $\mathrm{BCl}_2$  itself remains unchanged, meaning only the B-B bond is cleaved.

The sum of the product coefficients is 1, and the number of products is 1, both being odd.

Equation 3: The products are already given in the problem as  $\mathrm{Ca}_{5}(\mathrm{PO}_{4})_{3}\mathrm{OH}$  and aluminum phosphate  $\mathrm{AlPO_4}$ . No redox process occurs, and hydrothermal reactions typically involve the formation of water. Thus, the balanced equation is:

The sum of the product coefficients is 30, and the number of products is 3, including an even number.

Equation 5: The chemical formula of glutamic acid is  $\mathrm{C}_5\mathrm{H}_9\mathrm{NO}_4$ . The left side of the equation requires five  $\mathrm{HCO}_3^-$  ions and one nitrogen source,  $\mathrm{NH}_4^+$ . Excess oxygen atoms are balanced with hydrogen to form water. Seawater has a pH of about 8, so additional  $\mathrm{NH}_4^+$  is needed to neutralize the alkali produced in the reaction. In this state, glutamic acid exists as a monovalent anion with both carboxyl protons dissociated.
