# Question

A compound A with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C_8H_{14}}$  contains a quaternary carbon. After heating and refluxing it with  $\mathrm{KMnO}_4$ , only a product with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C_7H_{12}O}$  is detected in the reaction solution. A reacts with hydrobromic acid to give B, while reaction with hydrobromic acid in the presence of peroxide gives isomer C. B in sodium ethoxide-ethanol gives D. D reacts with  $\mathrm{BH}_3$  and is further treated with acetic acid to give compound E containing a five-membered ring structure. D can decolorize  $\mathrm{Br}_2$  and produce F. Treatment of F with sodium ethoxide-ethanol solution yields two isomeric products G and H. Deduce the structures of all species based on the above information.

Based on your deduced results, choose the correct option. If all options are incorrect, select option A: All other options are incorrect.

A. All other options are incorrect.  
B. Compound A has 5 different chemical shift hydrogens in the nuclear magnetic resonance hydrogen spectrum.  
C. Among compounds  $\mathbf{A} \sim \mathbf{H}$ , there are 4 types of hydrocarbons.  
D. There are 2 pairs of isomers among compounds  $\mathbf{A} \sim \mathbf{H}$  
E. The substituents on the ring in compound  $\mathbf{G} / \mathbf{H}$  are completely identical to those in compound A.  
F. The different kinds of substituents on the ring in compound A are located on adjacent C atoms.

# Answer

Correct Answer: A

# Detailed Explanation

To solve this problem, we first need to deduce the structure of  $\mathbf{A}$  based on the known information and the reaction properties of  $\mathbf{A}$ .

The molecular formula of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{C_8H_{14}}$ , and its degree of unsaturation  $\Omega = 2$ .

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

The degree of unsaturation of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\Omega = 2$ .

This indicates that it contains an unsaturated  $\mathrm{C} = \mathrm{C}$  double bond or a cyclic structure. Since  $\mathbf{A}$  reacts with  $\mathrm{KMnO}_4$  under reflux with heating, only a product with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C}_7\mathrm{H}_{12}\mathrm{O}$  is detected in the reaction solution. The ratio of the species after the reaction shows that  $\mathbf{A}$  has one less carbon, which indicates that it contains only one unsaturated  $\mathrm{C} = \mathrm{C}$  double bond and it is a terminal alkene double bond (  $[J[CH4][CX3](= [CH2])[CX4]]$ , hereinafter referred to as  $^{\prime}[JCC(= C)C]$  for short), and the other degree of unsaturation is contributed by a cyclic structure.

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

A contains a terminal alkene double bond and a cyclic structure

A reacts with hydrobromic acid to give B, then the product after the latter tertiary carbocation combines with a bromide ion is  $\mathrm{[}JCC(Br)(C)C[\mathrm{]}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

B contains the fragment  $\backslash []CC(Br)(C)C[\backslash ]$

The reaction with hydrobromic acid in the presence of peroxide gives the isomer  $\mathbf{C}$ . This is a free radical reaction, and the direction of hydrobromic acid addition to the double bond is opposite to that of the ionic reaction. The product obtained should be  $\mathrm{[}JCC(CBr)C\mathrm{]}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

C contains the fragment  $\lceil JCC(CBr)C\rceil$

$\mathbf{B}$  gives  $\mathbf{D}$  in sodium ethoxide-ethanol. This is a one-step elimination reaction. It should be noted that  $\mathbf{A}$  cannot be generated here, but an isomer  $\mathbf{D}$  with a different double bond position from  $\mathbf{A}$  should be generated. Therefore, the structure of  $\mathbf{D}$  can be written as  $\mathrm{[JC = C(C)C]}$ .

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

D contains the fragment \([]C = C(C)C[\]

D reacts with  $\mathrm{BH}_3$  and is further treated with acetic acid to obtain a compound E containing a five-membered ring structure. This step is a hydroboration reduction reaction, which reduces the unsaturated double bond to a saturated bond. Obviously, no ring formation has occurred. Therefore, the "five-membered ring" described here was originally present in the substrate. This also confirms the assumption that "A has a cyclic structure". Therefore, the cyclic structure here is a five-membered ring. The structure of E contains a cyclic structure like `C1C(C)CCC1` while A has an additional terminal alkene double bond outside the ring `C1C(=C)CCC1`

1 PTS

E contains the `C1C(C)CCC1` structure, and A contains the `C1C(=C)CCC1` structure

D can decolorize  $\mathrm{Br}_2$  and generate  $\mathbf{F}$ . This step is a dibromination of an alkene. According to the previous inference,  $\mathbf{F}$  contains the `C1(Br)C(Br)(C)CCC1` structure.

# CHECKPOINT

0.5 PTS

F contains the  $\mathrm{C}1(\mathrm{Br})\mathrm{C}(\mathrm{Br})(\mathrm{C})\mathrm{CCC}1$  structure

$\mathbf{F}$  is treated with sodium ethoxide-ethanol solution to generate two isomeric products  $\mathbf{G}$  and  $\mathbf{H}$ . This is a one-step elimination reaction to obtain a diene. Rewriting  $\mathrm{C1(Br)C(Br)(C)CCC1^{\prime}}$  as the equivalent  $\mathrm{[C:1]1[C:2]}$  ([Br:a])[C:3]([Br:b])([C:4])[C:5][C:6]1` indicates that the hydrogen on  $\mathrm{[C:1]^{\prime}}$  can undergo elimination with  $\mathrm{[Br:a]^{\prime}}$  and there is only one way, which indicates that  $\mathrm{[C:1]^{\prime}}$  is not the quaternary carbon in  $\mathbf{A}$  as indicated in the question. Similarly, the hydrogen on  $\mathrm{[C:4]}/\mathrm{[C:5]^{\prime}}$  can undergo two different elimination modes with  $\mathrm{[Br:b]^{\prime}}$  to obtain two isomeric dienes, which also indicates that  $\mathrm{[C:4]}/\mathrm{[C:5]^{\prime}}$  is not a quaternary carbon either. Therefore, only  $\mathrm{[C:6]^{\prime}}$  can be a quaternary carbon. Combining with the molecular formula  $\mathrm{C_8H_{14}}$  of  $\mathbf{A}$  in the question, it is not difficult to find that the quaternary carbon is connected to two methyl groups. Therefore, the structure of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{^{\prime}C1C(=C)CC(C)(C)C1^{\prime}}$ .
