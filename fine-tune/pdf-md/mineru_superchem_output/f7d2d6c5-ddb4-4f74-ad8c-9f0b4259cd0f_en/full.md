# Question

For aqueous solutions containing iodide ions (excluding other ions that may affect the determination), the following method can be used to determine the content of iodide ions:

1. Under neutral or weakly acidic conditions, add excess  $\mathrm{Br}_2$  for oxidation. After removing the remaining  $\mathrm{Br}_2$ , add excess KI;  
2. Extract with  $\mathrm{CCl}_4$  (approximately considered complete extraction). After separating the aqueous phase, back-extract with an aqueous solution of  $\mathrm{N}_2\mathrm{H}_4$ ;  
3. Under neutral or weakly acidic conditions, oxidize with excess  $\mathrm{Br}_2$ . After removing the remaining  $\mathrm{Br}_2$ , add excess KI;  
4. Appropriately acidify the solution, use  $\mathbf{X}$  as an indicator, and titrate with  $\mathbf{Y}$  standard solution.

In a certain determination,  $25.00\mathrm{mL}$  of the test solution containing KI was taken. After the above operations,  $20.06\mathrm{mL}$  of  $0.1000\mathrm{mol/L}$  Y standard solution was consumed.

Which of the following options is correct?

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. X should be sodium diphenylamine sulfonate, and Y should be sodium thiosulfate.  
C. Under neutral or weakly acidic conditions, the purpose of adding excess  $\mathrm{Br}_{2}$  is to oxidize  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$ to  $\mathrm{I}_{2}$  
D. The target substance for back extraction using an aqueous solution of  $\mathrm{N}_{2} \mathrm{H}_{4}$  is  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  in the organic phase  
E. The concentration of  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  in the original solution is  $13.37\mathrm{mmol / L}$ .  
F. The concentration of  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  in the original solution is  $2.229\mathrm{mmol / L}$ .

G. The concentration of  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  in the original solution is  $80.24\mathrm{mmol / L}$ .

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

Under neutral or weakly acidic conditions, the addition of excess  $\mathrm{Br}_2$  can oxidize  $\mathrm{I}^{-}$  to  $\mathrm{IO}_3^-$ :

$$
\mathrm {I} ^ {-} + 3 \mathrm {B r} _ {2} + 3 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} \rightarrow \mathrm {I O} _ {3} ^ {-} + 6 \mathrm {B r} ^ {-} + 6 \mathrm {H} ^ {+}
$$
