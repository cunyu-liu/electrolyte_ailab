# Question

A is a monoprotic weak acid that is liquid at room temperature, which reacts with sodium hydroxide to obtain salt B. A reacts with sodium borohydride to obtain salt D, while B reacts with a chloride C of a short-period main group element M to obtain salt E. It is known that the mass fractions of nitrogen in D and E are both greater than  $80\%$ .

A fluoride  $\mathbf{F}$  of another short-period main group element reacts with antimony pentafluoride in liquid hydrogen fluoride to obtain  $\mathbf{G}$ . The mass fraction of  $\mathrm{Sb}$  in  $\mathbf{G}$  is  $43.06\%$ .  $\mathbf{G}$  reacts with  $\mathbf{A}$  in liquid hydrogen fluoride to obtain an unstable high-energy material  $\mathbf{H}$ .  $\mathbf{H}$  reacts with  $\mathbf{D}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$  in liquid sulfur dioxide to obtain  $\mathbf{I}$  and  $\mathbf{J}$  respectively. The cations of these two substances are the same, and the mass fractions of nitrogen in the anions are  $95.66\%$  and  $91.23\%$  respectively, and the stability of  $\mathbf{I}$  and  $\mathbf{J}$  is lower than that of  $\mathbf{H}$ .

From the above, the following options are correct:

A. The chemical formula of D contains only two elements.  
B. The structure of  $\mathbf{C}$  is a trigonal pyramidal structure.  
C. The mass fraction of nitrogen in  $\mathbf{E}$  is approximately  $85\%$  
D. F has four conformational isomers.  
E. There are no  $\pi$  bonds in the cation of  $\mathbf{G}$ .  
F. The mass fraction of nitrogen in  $\mathbf{H}$  is approximately  $27\%$ .  
G. The cations of  $\mathbf{I},\mathbf{J}$  have a linear structure.  
H. J and E have different anions.

1. All of the above options are incorrect.
