# Question

A certain mixture  $\mathbf{A}$  is purple-red and contains two common transition metal elements  $\mathbf{M}, \mathbf{N}$ , with the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{M}$  being smaller. The concentrations of these two metals in  $\mathbf{A}$  are close. Concentrated hydrochloric acid is added to  $\mathbf{A}$  and gently heated to obtain a blue solution  $\mathbf{B}$  and a gas  $\mathbf{C}$ . If sodium hydroxide solution is added to  $\mathbf{A}$  with stirring, a brownish-red precipitate  $\mathbf{D}$  and a green solution  $\mathbf{E}$  are obtained. When excess sulfur dioxide is bubbled into  $\mathbf{A}$ , the solution eventually turns into a pink solution  $\mathbf{F}$ . Ammonia water is quickly poured into  $\mathbf{F}$  to obtain a white precipitate  $\mathbf{G}$  and a brownish-yellow solution  $\mathbf{H}$ .  $\mathbf{G}$  slowly turns into a brown precipitate in the air. When  $\mathbf{D}$  and  $\mathbf{G}$  are mixed and dilute sulfuric acid is added, solution  $\mathbf{A}$  is obtained again.

Note: The solution in this problem may contain multiple major ions (ignoring hydrolysis reactions).

Which of the following statements is correct?

A. The relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{N}$  is less than 58.  
B. The formula weight of a certain major ion contained in B is greater than 200.  
C. C is a paramagnetic gas.  
D. D is completely insoluble in dilute hydrochloric acid.  
E. Solution  $\mathbf{E}$  contains  $\mathbf{M}$  and  $\mathbf{N}$  
F. F solution contains only  $\mathbf{M}$  or only  $\mathbf{N}$ .  
G. The central atom of the major ions in  $\mathbf{H}$  has three unpaired electrons.  
H. None of the above options are correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: B

# Detailed Explanation

A is a purple-red solution, most likely containing permanganate, indicating that one of the metals is Mn;

Chlorine gas is a diamagnetic molecule, so option C is incorrect.
