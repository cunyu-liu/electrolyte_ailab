# Question

$\mathbf{X}$  is a 1:1 type salt (quaternary compound) with a carbon mass fraction of  $4.35\%$  and a boron mass fraction of  $43.09\%$ . Treatment of  $\mathbf{X}$  with excess ICl in glyme yields the anion  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$ in good yield, which has a boron mass fraction of  $13.24\%$ . The reaction of  $\mathrm{CPh}_3^+ \mathbf{B}^-$  with  ${}^{i}\mathrm{Pr}_3 \mathrm{SiH}$  in toluene gives pale yellow microcrystals of  $\mathbf{C}$ , and the reaction of  $\mathbf{C}$  with anhydrous liquid hydrogen chloride gives  $\mathbf{A}$ .

Which of the following statements are correct:

1. The quotient of the number of possible structures with the highest symmetry of  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  and its point group order is 0.4.  
2. The mass fraction of boron in  $\mathbf{A}$  is approximately  $13.22\%$ .  
3. Under different reaction conditions,  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Cl}}$ ,  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Br}}$ , and  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{I}}$  can be obtained respectively, then the order of the magnitude of the cation-anion interaction in  $\mathbf{C}$  is  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{I}}$ .  
4. Similarly,  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Cl}}$ ,  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Br}}$ , and  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{I}}$  exist, and the order of their acidity is  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{I}}$ .

# Answer

Correct Answer: M

# Detailed Explanation

From the mass fraction, it can be known that in  $\mathbf{X}$ ,  $\mathrm{B}:\mathrm{C} = 11:1$ . At this time, one can think of carborane. Since the following is a halogenation process, it can be assumed that the anion is  $\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{12}^{-}$  first. Substituting into the mass fraction calculation, it can be known that the remainder is about 133, i.e., Cs, which is consistent with the conditions of the problem. Therefore,  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $\mathrm{Cs}[\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{12}]$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

X is  $\mathrm{Cs}[\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{12}]$

According to the question,  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  must contain C, B, and I elements, and may also contain H element.

Consider from the simplest case, considering that the carborane skeleton remains unchanged, that is,  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  still contains 11 Bs. Since the mass fraction of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $13.24\%$ , the molar mass of  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  is  $898.1\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{mol}^{-1}$ .

Removing 1 C and 11 Bs, the remainder is  $767.2\mathrm{g}\cdot \mathrm{mol}^{-1}$ , corresponding to 6 Is and 6 Hs. Therefore, the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  is  $\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_6\mathrm{I}_6^-$ .

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

$\mathbf{B}^{-}$  is  $\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_6\mathrm{I}_6^-$

When  $\mathrm{CPh}_3^+\mathbf{B}^-$  reacts with  ${}^{i}\mathrm{Pr}_{3}\mathrm{SiH}$  in toluene, the negatively charged hydrogen combines with  $\mathrm{CPh}_3^+$ , and  $\mathbf{C}$ :  ${}^{i}\mathrm{Pr}_{3}\mathrm{Si}[\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{6}\mathrm{I}_{6}]$  is obtained at the same time.  $\mathbf{C}$  reacts with anhydrous liquid hydrogen chloride to undergo protonation to obtain  $\mathbf{A}$ :  $\mathrm{H}[\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{6}\mathrm{I}_{6}]$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

A: H[CB<sub>11</sub>H<sub>6</sub>I<sub>6</sub>]

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

C:  ${}^{i}\mathrm{Pr}_{3}\mathrm{Si}[\mathrm{CB}_{11}\mathrm{H}_{6}\mathrm{I}_{6}]$

Statement 1: The highest symmetry requires placing  $1 \, \text{H}$  and  $1 \, \text{I}$  on the C or B that are farthest apart, with the 5 Bs close to H connected to I, and the 5 Bs close to I connected to H; or the 5 Bs close to H connected to H, and the 5 Bs close to I connected to I. This is the  $\mathrm{C}_{5\mathrm{v}}$  point group, a total of  $2 \times 2 = 4$  types, and the quotient is  $4/10 = 0.4$ . Statement 1 is correct.

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

The quotient of the number of possible structures with the highest symmetry of  $\mathbf{B}^{-}$  and the order of its point group is 0.4

Statement 2:  $(11\times 10.81) / 898.1\approx 13.22$  , statement 2 is correct.

Statement 3: The order of polarizability is  $\mathrm{I} > \mathrm{Br} > \mathrm{Cl}$ , so the electron donating ability is  $\mathrm{I} > \mathrm{Br} > \mathrm{Cl}$ . The interaction between anions and cations is  $\mathrm{Si} - \mathrm{X}$  coordination, so  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Cl}} < \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Br}} < \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{I}}$ , statement 3 is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

The order of the magnitude of the interaction between anions and cations is  $\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{I}}$

Statement 4: The electron withdrawing ability is  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{I}}$ , so the order of acidity is  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{I}}$ , statement 4 is correct.

The order of acidity is  $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Cl}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{Br}} > \mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{I}}$
