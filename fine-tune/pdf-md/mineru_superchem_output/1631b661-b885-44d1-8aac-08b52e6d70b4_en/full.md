# Question

Analyze the following chemical reactions:

1. A small amount of methane reduces manganese(III) oxide, forming manganese(II,III) oxide.  
2. Nitroethane is added to aqueous sulfuric acid, and the mixture is heated to produce a monobasic acid and a 1:1 salt with nitrogen in the -1 oxidation state.  
3. Iron is added to molten sodium hydroxide, yielding two elemental substances, with the iron transforming into a solid with a spinel structure.  
4. Phenylhydrazine and AtI are dissolved in a  $\mathrm{KI} - \mathrm{I}_2$  solution to produce astatobenzene.  
5. Sulfur dioxide is bubbled into a black suspension of  $\mathrm{Cu(SCN)_2}$ , converting the system into a white suspension.  
6. Arsenic-bearing beudantite  $\mathrm{PbFe}_3(\mathrm{AsO}_4)(\mathrm{SO}_4)(\mathrm{OH})_6$  reacts with calcium bicarbonate dissolved in mine water, transforming into four minerals: scorodite ( $\mathrm{FeAsO}_4 \cdot 2\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ ), goethite ( $\mathrm{FeOOH}$ ), cerussite ( $\mathrm{PbCO}_3$ ), and gypsum ( $\mathrm{CaSO}_4 \cdot 2\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ ).

Among these, which reactions have chemical equations (with coefficients in the simplest integer ratio) where the sum of the product-side coefficients is even and the sum of the reactant-side coefficients is odd? Select the option that includes all such reactions.

A. 2  
B. 4  
C. 6  
D. 3,5

E. 1,4

F. 2,6  
G. 1,3,4  
H. 1,2,5  
I. 3,4,6  
J. 2,5,6  
K. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: F
