# Question

There is currently an alloy primarily composed of  $\mathrm{Pb}$  and  $\mathrm{Zn}$ , which may contain other metals. To determine the content of each component, an experimenter designed the following experimental procedure:

(1) Dissolve the  $0.7800\mathrm{g}$  sample in an excess mixed acid, dilute to  $250.0~\mathrm{mL}$ , and transfer  $25.00~\mathrm{mL}$  of the solution to a conical flask. Add  $0.1\mathrm{gNaOH}$  and  $10\mathrm{mL}$  of  $1:1$  ammonia buffer solution, followed by a small amount of tartaric acid and  $0.2\mathrm{g}$  KCN. Using Eriochrome Black T as the indicator, titrate with a standard EDTA solution of concentration  $c_{1}(\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1})$ . When the solution changes from purple-red to blue, record the reading  $V_{1}(\mathrm{mL})$ . Then, take another  $25.00~\mathrm{mL}$  of the solution, add NaOH and ammonia buffer solution, and titrate with the EDTA solution, recording the reading  $V_{2}(\mathrm{mL})$ .

In the first experiment, the experimenter prepared a standard EDTA solution with  $c_{1} = 0.02016$  (mol·L $^{-1}$ ). When the titration volume reached 17.52 mL, he realized there was an issue with the experiment and abandoned it.

In light of this, he designed a second experimental procedure:

(2) Subsequently, the experimenter revised the procedure as follows: Dissolve the  $0.7800\mathrm{g}$  sample in an excess mixed acid, dilute to  $250.0\mathrm{mL}$ , and transfer  $25.00\mathrm{mL}$  of the solution to a conical flask. Add  $0.1\mathrm{gNaOH}$  and  $10\mathrm{mL}$  of  $1:1$  ammonia buffer solution, followed by a small amount of tartaric acid and  $0.2\mathrm{g}$  KCN. Then, introduce  $0.2\mathrm{g}$  of triethanolamine, and using Eriochrome Black T as the indicator, titrate with an EDTA solution of concentration  $c_{1}(\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}^{-1})$  until the endpoint is reached, recording the reading  $V_{3}(\mathrm{mL})$ . Next, add a slight excess of  $1:1$  formaldehyde solution and continue titrating until the solution just turns blue, recording the reading  $V_{4}(\mathrm{L})$ .

Regarding the above analysis process, which of the following statements is correct? (For options involving calculations, an error within  $1\%$  is considered acceptable.)

A. In Scheme 1, the mass fraction of  $\mathrm{Pb}$  can be expressed as  $2.478c_{1}V_{1}$  
B. In Scheme 1, the mass fraction of Zn can be expressed as  $-0.8583c_{1}(V_{1} - V_{2})$ .

C. The experimenter believes that the reason for the failure of Scheme 1 is that the solution turned blue prematurely when titrated to  $17.52 \mathrm{~mL}$ .  
D. In Scheme 2, when formaldehyde is added, the sum of the coefficients on the product side of the ionic equation for the reaction of formaldehyde (with the coefficients in the simplest whole-number ratio) is 8.  
E. In Scheme 2, replacing triethanolamine with ascorbic acid + potassium cyanide will not affect the titration process.  
F. If the sample contains the metal cadmium, according to Method 2, cadmium will block the indicator and affect the measurement results.  
G. In Scheme 2, the mass fraction of Zn can be expressed as  $0.8983c_{1}(V_{4} - V_{3})$  
H. None of the above statements are correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: H

# Detailed Explanation

Titration process of Method 1:

After adding potassium cyanide,  $\mathrm{Zn}^{2+}$  forms  $\mathrm{Zn(CN)}_4^{2-}$  and is masked. EDTA only titrates  $\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}$ , with Eriochrome Black T as the indicator. The solution changes from purple-red to blue. Under an ammonia buffer solution, both  $\mathrm{Zn}^{2+}$  and  $\mathrm{Pb}^{2+}$  are titrated by EDTA.
