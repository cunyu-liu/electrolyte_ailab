# Question

The platinum-containing salt A with  $40.14\%$  platinum decomposes upon heating to produce elemental platinum, a salt B, and a yellowish-green gas C. The anion in A has an octahedral structure, and its anion reacts with  $\mathrm{K}_2\mathrm{C}_2\mathrm{O}_4$  to produce a square planar anion D. D reacts with a binary compound to form an anion E. The mass fraction of Pt in E is  $59.22\%$ , and it contains a ligand with more than 3 atoms. E can dimerize to form a neutral compound F.

Which of the following statements is incorrect?

A. The cation in A corresponds to an element that reacts violently with water.  
B. The sum of the simplest integer ratios after balancing the chemical equation for the decomposition of A is 6.  
C. Potassium salt of  $\mathbf{D}$  is a red crystal.  
D. The mass fraction of  $\mathrm{Pt}$  in  $\mathbf{F}$  is  $66\%$  (correct within a  $1\%$  error margin).  
E. All options A-D are correct  
F. At most three of options A-D are correct.

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation
