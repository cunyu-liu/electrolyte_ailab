# Question

$\mathbf{M}$  is an element. The high-valent oxide  $\mathbf{A}$  of  $\mathbf{M}$  is orange-yellow and can be obtained by the thermal decomposition of salt  $\mathbf{B}$  containing  $\mathbf{M}$  at  $43.55\%$  (retaining four significant figures).  $\mathbf{A}$  serves as an important catalyst in industrial production. When  $\mathbf{A}$  is fused with oxalic acid, it forms a deep blue compound  $\mathbf{C}$  while releasing gas.  $\mathbf{C}$  adopts a rutile structure at room temperature. The reduction of  $\mathbf{C}$  with hydrogen yields oxide  $\mathbf{D}$ , which contains  $\mathbf{M}$  at  $63.16\%$  (retaining four significant figures). Further reduction of  $\mathbf{C}$  produces the elemental form of  $\mathbf{M}$ . The direct reaction of  $\mathbf{M}$  with chlorine gas yields  $\mathbf{E}$ , which is unstable in air. Under a CO atmosphere,  $\mathbf{E}$  is reduced by Na in pyridine to form the highly symmetric anionic salt  $\mathbf{F}$ . Upon acidification of  $\mathbf{F}$ , a volatile blue-green crystal  $\mathbf{G}$  is obtained. A similar low-temperature compound  $\mathbf{H}$ , containing  $\mathbf{M}$  at  $23.25\%$  (retaining four significant figures), belongs to the  $O_h$  point group.

From the following options, select the correct statement.

A. B has three more elements than A  
B. In substance D, the ratio of the number of atoms of the element with the highest atomic count to that of the element with the lowest atomic count is greater than 2.  
C. F molecular weight less than 200  
D.  $\mathbf{G}$  contains one more type of element than  $\mathbf{H}$ .  
E. The sum of the valence states of the metal elements in  $\mathbf{C}$  and  $\mathbf{E}$  is greater than 7.  
F. In this problem, there are a total of 5 valence states for the metal elements among all substances.

# Answer

Correct Answer: D
