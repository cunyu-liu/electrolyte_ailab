# Question

$\mathbf{M}$  is a silver-gray, hard and brittle transition metal with metallic luster. It is the main additive of stainless steel, which can make steel have high corrosion resistance. The reaction of nitric acid and  $\mathbf{M}$  amalgam can obtain a deliquescent black oxide powder  $\mathbf{A}$ , which can burn in the air to obtain  $\mathbf{B}$ . A 1:1 mixture of carbon monoxide and chlorine gas at high temperature can convert  $\mathbf{B}$  into  $\mathbf{C}$ . The fusion reaction of  $\mathbf{B}$  with potassium chloride can obtain a yellow solid  $\mathbf{D}$ . After dissolving  $\mathbf{D}$  in water and acidifying, the solution will turn orange due to the formation of  $\mathbf{E}$ . Further addition of concentrated sulfuric acid to the solution will form an orange-red solid  $\mathbf{F}$ .

It is known that there is the following process:

1. C can react with  $\mathrm{Ph}_4\mathrm{AsCl}$  and sodium azide under certain conditions to generate a mononuclear complex, in which the mass fraction of nitrogen is  $17.34\%$ .  
2. Powdered  $\mathbf{E}$  is heated to boiling with excess concentrated hydrofluoric acid and then cooled to obtain a substance  $\mathbf{G}$  containing M  $32.89\%$ .  
3.  $\mathbf{E}$  reacts with  $\mathbf{C}$  under molten conditions, and one of the products is similar to  $\mathbf{G}$ .

The following statement is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The mass fraction of M in A is  $68.4\%$  
C. The mass fraction of M in C is  $33.6\%$  
D. The mass fraction of M in F is  $76.5\%$  
E. The complex in Process 1 is a 1:1 type ionic compound.

F. In Process 1, the hybridization of  $\mathbf{M}$  in the complex is  $\mathfrak{sp}^3$  hybridization.  
G. The sum of the coefficients of all species in the chemical equation for Process 3, balanced to the simplest integer ratio, is 12.  
H. The sum of the coefficients of all species in the chemical equation of Process 2, when balanced to the simplest integer ratio, is 8.

# Answer
