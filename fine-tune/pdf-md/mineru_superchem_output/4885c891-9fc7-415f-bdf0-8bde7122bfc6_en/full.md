# Answer

Correct Answer: P

# Detailed Explanation

By default, it is known that the wavelength of Cu-K  $\alpha$  radiation is 154.1pm.

First, according to the Bragg diffraction equation:

$$
2 \mathrm {d} \sin (\theta) = \mathrm {n} \lambda
$$

the interplanar spacings corresponding to the given angles can be calculated as:

278 pm, 241 pm, 170 pm, 145 pm, 139 pm

Since

$$
\mathrm {d} _ {\left(\mathrm {h k l}\right)} = \frac {\mathrm {a}}{\sqrt {\mathrm {h} ^ {2} + \mathrm {k} ^ {2} + \mathrm {l} ^ {2}}}
$$

by squaring the above interplanar spacings and taking their ratios, we obtain a set of approximate integer ratios, which correspond to the ratios of  $\mathrm{h}^2 + \mathrm{k}^2 + \mathrm{l}^2$ :

3:4:8:11:12
