# Question

The basic process of crude salt purification is as follows:

Dissolve  $10g$  of crude salt in  $40mL$  of deionized water with heating, then add  $25\%$ $\mathrm{BaCl}_2$  solution to it until no more precipitate is produced, keep warm for  $10min$ , and perform the first normal pressure filtration. Then add saturated  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$  solution until no more precipitate is produced, and perform the second normal pressure filtration. Add  $6M$  hydrochloric acid, then evaporate by heating in a water bath, filter under suction to remove the mother liquor, and finally dry by stir-frying to obtain refined salt.

Assume that  $10g$  of crude salt is composed of  $9g$  NaCl,  $0.3g$ $\mathrm{MgCl}_2$ ,  $0.3g$ $\mathrm{CaCl}_2$ ,  $0.2g$ $\mathrm{FeCl}_3$  and  $0.2g$ $\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}$ . It is known that  $\mathrm{BaCl}_2$  and  $\mathrm{Na_2CO_3}$  are added with a  $50\%$  excess,  $1mL$  of solution is lost in each normal pressure filtration, and  $5mL$  of mother liquor is lost in vacuum filtration. Estimate the theoretical yield and theoretical yield rate based on the following solubility data.

Hint: It is considered that the volume of the solution remains unchanged and remains at  $40~mL$  before evaporation and concentration, that is, deionized water will be added after the volume decreases (such as after normal pressure filtration), and the excess water will evaporate after the volume increases; vacuum filtration is performed immediately after evaporation and concentration, and it can be considered that the temperature at this time is about the boiling point of water; it is considered that  $1~mL$  of aqueous solution contains  $1~mL$  of water.

The amounts of substance of  $\mathrm{Cl}^-$  and  $\mathrm{Ba}^{2+}$  after adding an excess of  $50\%$ $\mathrm{BaCl}_2$  are 173.6 mmol and 0.704 mmol, respectively.

After filtration with a loss of 1/40, the composition becomes:

Then, an excess of  $50\%$ $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$  was added. First, write out the equations for the reactions of each ion:

$$
\mathrm {F e} ^ {3 +} + 3 \mathrm {C O} _ {3} ^ {2 -} + 3 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} \rightarrow \mathrm {F e} (\mathrm {O H}) _ {3} + 3 \mathrm {H C O} _ {3} ^ {-}
$$

$$

$$

0.5 PTS

$$
2 \mathrm {M g} ^ {2 +} + 3 \mathrm {C O} _ {3} ^ {2 -} + 2 \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} \rightarrow \mathrm {M g} _ {2} (\mathrm {O H}) _ {2} \mathrm {C O} _ {3} + 2 \mathrm {H C O} _ {3} ^ {-}

$$
\mathrm {B a} ^ {2 +} + \mathrm {C O} _ {3} ^ {2 -} \rightarrow \mathrm {B a C O} _ {3}

Therefore, the stoichiometric ratio is  $1\mathrm{Fe}^{3+} - \frac{3}{2}\mathrm{CO}_3^{2-}, 1\mathrm{Ca}^{2+} - 1\mathrm{CO}_3^{2-}, 1\mathrm{Mg}^{2+} - 1\mathrm{CO}_3^{2-}, 1\mathrm{Ba}^{2+} - 1\mathrm{CO}_3^{2-}$

At this point, only focus on  $\mathrm{Na}^+$  in the reaction. After adding  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$ , all metal cations in the solution except  $\mathrm{Na}^+$  are converted to the corresponding precipitates, but the total amount of charge carried by their anions remains unchanged. Therefore, the amount of substance of cations can also be determined by charge conservation. Then, according to the stoichiometric ratio,  $\mathrm{Na}^+$  in the solution is:  $152.9 + (2 \times 3.072 + 2 \times 2.635 + 3 \times 1.202 + 2 \times 0.686) \times 1.5 = 177.5 \, \text{mmol}$

The amount of  $\mathrm{Na}^+$  in the solution after adding  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{CO}_3$  is 177.5 mmol

Then, after filtration with a loss of 1/40 and addition of concentrated hydrochloric acid, 173.1 mmol NaCl is obtained, which is  $10.11\mathrm{gNaCl}$ .

1 PTS

There is  $10.11\,g$  NaCl in the solution after adding concentrated hydrochloric acid

Then, vacuum filtration loses  $5mL$  of saturated NaCl solution, and the mass of NaCl in it is  $5g \times \frac{28.05\%}{1 - 28.05\%} \approx 1.95g$ .

1 PTS

1.95 g NaCl is lost by vacuum filtration

Therefore, the final theoretical yield is  $8.16g$ , and the theoretical yield is  $81.6\%$ .
