# Question

Given the existence of a heteroatom-containing cage-like polyvanadate, where  $\mathrm{V}:\mathrm{O} = 3:7$ , the content of non-metallic elements is  $46.56\%$ , bearing 5 negative charges, but not all  $\mathrm{V}$  are in their highest oxidation state and the heteroatom is located at the center of the cage, and it is known that all  $\mathrm{V}$  are in square pyramidal coordination. The descriptions of vertices, edges, and faces in the options refer to polyhedra with vanadium atoms and non-terminal oxygen atoms as vertices.

Which of the following statements is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The heteroatom contained in this acid radical is located in the fourth period of the periodic table.  
C. The heteroatom contained in this acid radical is a short-period element.  
D. The mass fraction of oxygen in this acid radical is  $37.8\%$  
E. The mass fraction of oxygen in the acid radical is  $44.8\%$  
F. The polyvanadate has 64 edges.  
G. The polyoxovanadate has 28 edges.  
H. The polyvanadate has 26 faces.  
1. The polyoxovanadate has 32 faces.  
J. The polyoxovanadate has 18 vertices.

# Answer

Correct Answer: I
