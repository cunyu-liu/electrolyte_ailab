# Answer

Correct Answer: R

# Detailed Explanation

This is a relatively basic iodometric titration question. Among them, step (1) is the titration of  $\mathrm{Na}_2\mathrm{S}_2\mathrm{O}_3$  standard solution; step (2) is the titration of the unknown sample of resorcinol.

The analysis is as follows:

For statements 1 and 2, the principle of resorcinol titration in step (2) is that the phenolic hydroxyl group, as an electron-donating group, can undergo aromatic electrophilic substitution reaction, replacing all three H atoms at the ortho- and para- positions with I, thereby performing iodometric back titration.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

Phenolic hydroxyl group is an ortho-para directing group and will undergo electrophilic substitution

However, phenol has a low electron-donating ability, making it difficult for aromatic electrophilic substitution to occur at all three positions, so it cannot be used for quantitative analysis; phloroglucinol has a stronger electron-donating ability, so a similar method can be used for titration. Statement 1 is incorrect, statement 2 is correct.
