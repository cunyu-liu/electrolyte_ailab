# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

The general formula of  $\mathbf{A}$  should be  $\mathbf{M}(\mathrm{CO})_x$ , where  $x$  can be 4, 4.5, 5, etc. Through trials, it is found that when  $x = 4$ ,  $M(\mathbf{M}) = [4 \times 28.01 / (1 - 0.3447)] \times 0.3447 = 58.94(\mathrm{g / mol})$ . The molar mass is closest to that of cobalt, followed by nickel.

If  $\mathbf{M}$  is cobalt, then  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $C o_{2}(C O)_{8}$ , which can be reduced to  $[C o(C O)_{4}]^{-}$  by metallic sodium in liquid ammonia, which meets the condition of "tetrahedral mononuclear anion" in the question.

If  $\mathbf{M}$  is nickel, then  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $N_i(CO)_4$ , which can be reduced to  $[N_iH(CO)_3]_2$ ,  $[N_i(CO)_9]^{2-}$  and other species by metallic sodium in liquid ammonia, all of which do not meet the condition of "tetrahedral mononuclear anion" in the question.

Therefore,  $\mathbf{M}$  is cobalt,  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $C o_{2}(C O)_{8}$ , and  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $N a[C o(C O)_{4}]$ .
