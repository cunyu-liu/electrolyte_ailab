# Question

Using triphenylmethyl tetrakis(pentafluorophenyl)borate  $(\mathrm{Ph}_3\mathrm{CB}(\mathrm{C}_6\mathrm{F}_5)_4)$  as an initiator, polyvinyl chloride  $(-[\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{CHCl}]_n-)$  can react with triethylsilane in chlorobenzene, where most of the chlorine on the polyvinyl chloride is reduced to hydrogen by triethylsilane, and the remaining chlorine is replaced by chlorobenzene, which acts as a solvent, to form  $-\mathrm{C}_6\mathrm{H}_4\mathrm{Cl}$ , eventually generating a polymer product similar to ethylene-chlorostyrene copolymer.

The product was dissolved in deuterated tetrachloroethane for high-temperature NMR hydrogen spectroscopy. The spectrum only shows signals in the following chemical shift  $(\delta)$  ranges:

-  $\delta = 0$ , single peak, peak area is 0.24  
-  $\delta = 0.5\sim 3.5$  , multiple peaks, peak area is 10.88  
-  $\delta = 6.0$ , single peak, peak area is 0.96  
-  $\delta = 6.8 \sim 7.5$ , multiple peaks, peak area is 1.00

Which of the following statements is correct:

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. The reaction follows a radical mechanism  
C. The proportion of  $-\left[\mathrm{CH}_{2} \mathrm{CH}\left(\mathrm{C}_{6} \mathrm{H}_{4} \mathrm{Cl}\right)\right] -$  units in the product is  $8.98 \%$  
D. The proportion of  $-\left[\mathrm{CH}_{2} \mathrm{CH}\left(\mathrm{C}_{6} \mathrm{H}_{4} \mathrm{Cl}\right)\right]-$  units in the product is  $8.42 \%$  
E. If anisole is used as the solvent, a similar reaction can also occur.  
F. The proportion of  $-\left[\mathrm{CH}_{2} \mathrm{CH}_{2}\right] -$  units in the product is  $82.8\%$ .

# Answer

Correct Answer: C

# Detailed Explanation

The  $\mathrm{Ph}_3\mathrm{C}^+$  in  $\mathrm{Ph}_3\mathrm{CB}(\mathrm{C}_6\mathrm{F}_5)_4$  can abstract H from triethylsilane to generate  $\mathrm{Et}_3\mathrm{Si}^+$ , which as a Lewis acid combines with the chlorine of  $-[\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{CHCl}]_n-$  on polyvinyl chloride to generate a carbocation. If anisole is used as a solvent, the O in it will interfere with the formation of silyl cation and carbocation intermediates, preventing the reaction from proceeding normally. Therefore, options B and E are incorrect.

The carbocation can be reduced to  $-\mathrm{H}$  by  $\mathrm{HSiEt}_3$ , or it can undergo a Friedel-Crafts reaction with chlorobenzene as a solvent, eventually forming a polymer product similar to an ethylene-chlorostyrene copolymer. The product can be represented as:

$$
- \left[ \mathrm {C H} _ {2} \mathrm {C H} _ {2} \right] _ {\mathrm {x}} - \left[ \mathrm {C H} _ {2} \mathrm {C H} \left(\mathrm {C} _ {6} \mathrm {H} _ {4} \mathrm {C l}\right) \right] _ {\mathrm {y}} -
$$

where  $x$  is the number of  $-[\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{CH}_2] -$  units, and  $y$  is the number of  $-[\mathrm{CH}_2\mathrm{CH}(\mathrm{C}_6\mathrm{H}_4\mathrm{Cl})] -$  units.

The high-temperature nuclear magnetic resonance hydrogen spectrum of the product was tested using deuterated tetrachloroethane as a solvent. The spectrum has signals in four chemical shift  $(\delta)$  ranges, where:

- The peak around 0.0 comes from the internal standard substance.  
- The peak around 6.0 is the solvent peak of deuterated tetrachloroethane.  
- The peak at  $0.5 \sim 3.5$  comes from H connected to the alkyl group. There are 4 such H in each ethylene unit and 3 in each chlorostyrene unit.  
- The peak at  $6.8 \sim 7.5$  comes from H connected to the benzene ring. There are 4 such H in each chlorostyrene unit.

$$
