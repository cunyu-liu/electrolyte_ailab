# Question

Compound A can be prepared by reacting 2,4-disulfophenol with concentrated nitric acid. In 1873, Hermann Sprengel discovered that A could be used as a primary explosive, and it was widely employed in subsequent conflicts including the First Sino-Japanese War, the Russo-Japanese War, and World War I. In 1848, Scottish chemist John Stenhouse mixed calcium hypochlorite with a small amount of A to produce B, which was used by the German military as a tear gas agent during World War I and is now utilized as an insecticide. B contains no H atoms or  $C - C$  bonds, with  $\omega_{C} = 7.31\%$ . Reducing B with Sn in hydrochloric acid yields another blister agent C, which was prepared but never deployed by the German military; its relative molecular mass is less than 150. # Answer

Correct Answer: J

# Detailed Explanation

Clearly,  $\mathbf{A}$  is trinitrophenol  $(C_6H_2(OH)(NO_3)_3)$ . Since  $\mathbf{B}$  contains no  $H$  atoms or  $C - C$  bonds,  $\mathbf{B}$  likely contains only one  $C$  atom. The relative molecular mass of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $12.01 / 0.0731 = 164.3$ . After subtracting the mass of  $C$ , the remaining mass corresponds to  $3Cl$  atoms and  $1NO_2$  group, so  $\mathbf{B} = CCl_3NO_2$ .
