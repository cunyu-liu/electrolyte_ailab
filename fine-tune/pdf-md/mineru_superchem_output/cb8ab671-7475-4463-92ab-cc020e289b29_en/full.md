# Question

The element X exhibits stable properties with strong corrosion resistance, capable of withstanding corrosion from hydrochloric acid, sulfuric acid, nitric acid, and aqua regia at room temperature. Fusing the elemental X with potassium nitrate and potassium hydroxide yields A. After cooling the melt, it is extracted with water and treated with chlorine gas, followed by heating to volatilize B. Introducing B into a hydrochloric acid solution and heating it, then adding ammonium chloride precipitates element X in the form of C, which, upon ignition in hydrogen gas, yields spongy elemental X. In a hydrochloric acid solution of potassium chloride, reducing B with ethanol produces the red crystalline compound D, in which the mass fraction of X is  $26.99\%$ . Heating D to  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  results in a weight loss of  $4.81\%$ , yielding the brown crystalline compound E. If B is replaced with A as the reactant, a deep-red diamagnetic dinuclear complex F is obtained, in which the mass fraction of X is  $27.73\%$ . Dissolving F in concentrated hydrochloric acid and adding ammonia water generates a 1:3 ionic compound G. Exposing the solution of G to air produces a vivid red color, from which the diamagnetic compound H can be isolated. H is named for its intense color. Oxidizing H with ammonium cerium(IV) sulfate yields the paramagnetic brown compound I. It is known that both H and I are trinuclear complexes with identical cationic chemical formulas, where the mass fraction of X in the cation is  $52.86\%$ , while the anion count ratio is 6:7.

Given that  $\mathbf{A} \sim \mathbf{I}$  are all compounds containing  $\mathbf{X}$ , which of the following options are correct?

A. In A, the X element has a valence of  $+4$ .  
B. B is a regular octahedral structure.  
C. C is a 2:1 type ionic compound  
D. E has a molecular weight of approximately  $392 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{mol}$  
E. There is only one set of three-center  $\pi$  bonds in F

F. The ratio of nitrogen atoms to chlorine atoms in  $\mathbf{H}$  is  $7:3$ .  
G. In I, the average oxidation state of  $\mathbf{X}$  is  $+3$  
H. None of the above options are correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: F
