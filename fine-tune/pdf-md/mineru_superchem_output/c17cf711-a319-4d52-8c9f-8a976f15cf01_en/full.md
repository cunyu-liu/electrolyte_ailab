# Question

$9.34\mathrm{g}$  of elemental A was heated in concentrated  $\mathrm{HNO}_3$ , and after complete reaction,  $12.48\mathrm{g}$  of solid B (an oxide of A) was obtained, with the release of reddish-brown gas (Reaction 1). A can also react with molten KOH in the presence of  $\mathrm{O}_2$  to yield solid C (Reaction 2), where the oxidation state of element A in C is lower than that in B. B is a commonly used oxidizing agent in organic chemistry. Treating the KOH solution of B with ammonia yields a light-yellow complex D.

Choose the correct option from the following.

A. The ratio of the sum of the stoichiometric coefficients of reactants to products in Reaction 1 is 7:11 (the coefficients are in their simplest integer ratio).  
B. The oxygen content of B is  $20.15\%$ .  
C. Reaction 2 produces two products in a 2:1 ratio.  
D. The anion in D belongs to the  $T_{d}$  point group.  
E. The potassium content in C is  $23.52\%$ .  
F. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

The composition of  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $\mathrm{AO_x}$ , and the relative atomic mass of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{MA}$ . Then,  $\mathrm{MA} / (\mathrm{MA} + 16\mathrm{x}) = 9.34 / 12.48$ . Simplifying, we obtain  $\mathrm{MA} = 47.59\mathrm{x}$ . Enumerating the values in a table, when  $x = 4$ ,  $\mathrm{MA} = 190.36$ . Combining the fact that  $\mathbf{B}$  is a commonly used oxidizing agent in organic chemistry,  $\mathbf{A}$  is determined to be  $\mathbf{Os}$ .
