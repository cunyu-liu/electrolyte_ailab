# Question

The following chemical processes occur:

1. A carbon dioxide-saturated solution that has dissolved siderite is oxidized by oxygen to produce a reddish-brown precipitate.  
2. A triatomic gas can be prepared by mixing  $\mathrm{CH}_4$ ,  $\mathrm{NH}_3$ ,  $\mathrm{O}_2$  at high temperature in the presence of a  $\mathrm{Pt} / \mathrm{Tr}$  catalyst.  
3.  $\mathrm{ClO}_3\mathrm{F}$  undergoes solvolysis in liquid ammonia, producing two 1:1 type salts.  
4. Phosphorus pentafluoride reacts with  $\mathrm{O}_2\mathrm{F}_2$  at low temperature, producing a 1:1 type salt and releasing a gas.  
5. 2,4,6-trinitrophenol and excess bleaching powder solution are added to a calcium hydroxide suspension. An oil-like liquid insoluble in water is obtained in the lower layer of the suspension, and the molecular weight of its components is approximately 164.

For the chemical equations involved in the 5 processes, when the coefficients are balanced to the simplest integer ratio, the following statements are made:

1. The sum of the coefficients of the reactants in process 1 is less than the sum of the coefficients of the products.  
2. Two of the four numbers, the sum of the coefficients of the reactants and the sum of the coefficients of the products in process 2, and the sum of the coefficients of the reactants and the sum of the coefficients of the products in process 3, are prime numbers.  
3. The sum of the coefficients of the products and the sum of the coefficients of the reactants in process 4 are equal.  
4. The sum of the coefficients of the products and the sum of the coefficients of the reactants in process 5 are both prime numbers.

The option containing all the correct statements is:

A. All other options are incorrect

# Answer

Correct Answer: J
