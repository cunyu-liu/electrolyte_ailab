Proposition 2: AUG encodes methionine (M), UCU encodes serine (S)

However, when the ribosome encounters the stop codon UAA, under normal circumstances, this termination signal is recognized by release factor proteins, which cleave and release the synthesized polypeptide chain, thereby terminating translation. In a system lacking release factors, this termination step cannot be completed. More importantly, there is no leucine tRNA in the cell that can pair with the UAA stop codon (leucine codons are UUA/G or CUX). Therefore, the ribosome cannot recruit leucine tRNA to extend the peptide chain, nor can it release the existing methionine-serine (M-S) peptide chain. The final result is that the ribosome stalls at the UAA codon, and the entire translation process halts at this point, preventing the synthesis of the M-S-L polypeptide.
