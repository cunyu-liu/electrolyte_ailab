# Question

A pure sample of a certain inorganic salt  $\mathbf{X}$  is placed in air and heated to  $220^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . The thermogravimetric analysis curve shows that the sample begins to lose weight. When  $66.7\%$  (mole fraction) of  $\mathbf{X}$  has reacted, the temperature is rapidly increased to  $240^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and the remaining substances interact, and the sample loses weight according to another reaction. When heated to constant weight, there is only one inorganic salt in the sample. Continuing to increase the temperature to  $315^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the sample enters a new weight loss platform. At the end of the platform, the final sample mass is  $86.76\%$  of the initial mass.

The following information is available:

1. It is known that during the weight loss process, the reduced mass is only removed in the form of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  molecules.  
2. After the  $315^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  weight loss platform ends, the residual solid does not contain the element H.  
3.  $\mathbf{X}$  contains only one metal element.

Based on the above information, select the correct statement(s):

1.  $\mathbf{X}$  is composed of three elements.  
2. Four substances are involved in the entire reaction process.  
3. After balancing and simplifying the reaction equation for the first heating stage, the coefficients on both sides are the same.  
4. The numerical ratio of the metal element to the oxygen element in the chemical formula of the inorganic salt product produced in the second heating stage is  $3:10$ .  
5. The ratio of the mass of water lost in the second heating stage to the third heating stage is  $2:1$

# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

Let the chemical formula molar mass of  $\mathbf{X}$  be  $\mathrm{M}(\mathrm{X})\mathrm{g / mol}$ , and the initial amount of substance be  $\mathfrak{n}$  mol. The final product is represented as  $\mathbf{Y}$ .

Initial mass:  $\mathbf{m}(\mathbf{X}) = \mathbf{n}\times \mathbf{M}(\mathbf{X})$

Final mass:  $\mathrm{m(Z)} = 0.8676\times \mathrm{m(X)} = 0.8676\times \mathrm{n}\times \mathrm{M(X)}$

Total mass loss  $(\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O})$  ..  $\Delta \mathrm{m} = (1 - 0.8676)\times \mathrm{n}\times \mathrm{M}(\mathrm{X}) = 0.1324\times \mathrm{n}\times \mathrm{M}(\mathrm{X})$

According to the law of conservation of mass, the mass loss is equal to the mass of water removed. Let each molecule of  $\mathbf{X}$  ultimately lose  $k$  molecules of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ .

$$
