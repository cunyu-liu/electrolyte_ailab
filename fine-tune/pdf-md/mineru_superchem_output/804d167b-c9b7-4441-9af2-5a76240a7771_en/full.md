# Question

The experimenters attempted to investigate the decomposition mechanism of hydrogen peroxide. When carbon monoxide is introduced into the system, the decomposition mechanism of hydrogen peroxide is considered to be as follows:

$$
\mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} + \mathrm {M} \rightarrow 2 \mathrm {O H} + \mathrm {M}
$$

$$
\mathrm {O H} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} \rightarrow \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathrm {H O} _ {2}
$$

$$
\mathrm {2 H O _ {2} \rightarrow H _ {2} O _ {2} + O _ {2}}
$$

$$
\mathrm {O H} + \mathrm {C O} \rightarrow \mathrm {C O} _ {2} + \mathrm {H}
$$

$$
\mathrm {H} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} \rightarrow \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathrm {O H}
$$

$$
\mathrm {H} + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} \rightarrow \mathrm {H} _ {2} + \mathrm {H O} _ {2}
$$

(In fact, there is also the reaction  $\mathrm{OH} + \mathrm{H}_2 \rightarrow \mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O} + \mathrm{H}$ , but it is known that there is no  $\mathrm{H}_2$  at the initial stage of the reaction, and the rate of  $\mathrm{H}$  producing  $\mathrm{H}_2$  is much lower than the rate of producing  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  (about 350 times), so this elementary reaction is not considered in this question.)

The rate constants of these reactions are  $\mathbf{k}_1\sim \mathbf{k}_6$  ([M] has been included in  $\mathbf{k}_1$ , and [M] does not need to be involved in the calculation). Derive the rate equation for the decomposition of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$  using the steady-state approximation method, and express it in terms of  $[\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2],[\mathrm{CO}],\mathrm{k}_1,\mathrm{k}_2,\mathrm{k}_4,\mathrm{k}_5,\mathrm{k}_6$ . The following statements are made:

1. The rate equation for the decomposition of  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2$  is  $\mathbf{r} = 2\mathbf{k}_1[\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2]\left[\frac{1 + \frac{\mathrm{k}_2[\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2]}{\mathrm{k}_4[\mathrm{CO}]}}{\frac{\mathrm{k}_6}{\mathrm{k}_5 + \mathrm{k}_6} + \mathrm{k}_2[\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}_2]}\right]$

# Answer

# Detailed Explanation

$$
\mathrm {d} [ \mathrm {O H} ] / \mathrm {d t} = 2 \mathrm {k} _ {1} [ \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} ] - \mathrm {k} _ {2} [ \mathrm {O H} ] [ \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} ] + \mathrm {k} _ {5} [ \mathrm {H} ] [ \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} _ {2} ] - \mathrm {k} _ {4} [ \mathrm {O H} ] [ \mathrm {C O} ] = 0 (1)
