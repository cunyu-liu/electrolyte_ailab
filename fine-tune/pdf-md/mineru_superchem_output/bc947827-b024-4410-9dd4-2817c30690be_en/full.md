# Question

Given that  $K_{\mathrm{a1}} = 1.1 \times 10^{-7}$ ,  $K_{\mathrm{a2}} = 1.3 \times 10^{-13}$  for  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{S}$ ;  $K_{\mathrm{sp}}(\mathrm{ZnS}) = 2.5 \times 10^{-22}$ . Dissolve  $0.01\mathrm{mol}\mathrm{ZnS}$  in  $1.0\mathrm{L}$  hydrochloric acid. The closest value for the minimum concentration of hydrochloric acid required is

A.  $0.06 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  
B.  $0.07 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  
C.  $0.08 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  
D.  $0.09 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  
E.  $0.10 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$  
F.  $0.11 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{L}$

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

This is a very classic physical chemistry problem. The equation for the reaction is  $\mathrm{ZnS} + 2\mathrm{H}^{+} = \mathrm{Zn}^{2+} + \mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{S}$ . It can be calculated that the equilibrium constant for this reaction is:

$$
K = \frac {[ Z n ^ {2 +} ] [ H _ {2} S ]}{[ H ^ {+} ] ^ {2}} = \frac {[ Z n ^ {2 +} ] [ H _ {2} S ] [ S ^ {2 -} ]}{[ H ^ {+} ] ^ {2} [ S ^ {2 -} ]} = K _ {s p} (Z n S) \frac {[ H _ {2} S ]}{[ H ^ {+} ] ^ {2} [ S ^ {2 -} ]} = \frac {K _ {s p} (Z n S)}{K _ {a _ {1}} K _ {a _ {2}}} = 1. 7 \times 1 0 ^ {- 2}
$$

$$
K = 1. 7 \times 1 0 ^ {- 2}
$$

When  $\mathrm{ZnS}$  is completely dissolved,  $0.01\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}Zn^{2 + }$  and  $0.01\mathrm{mol}\cdot \mathrm{L}H_{2}S$  are generated. The equilibrium  $[H^{+}]$  can be calculated:

$$
[ H ^ {+} ] _ {e q} = \sqrt {\frac {0 . 0 1 \times 0 . 0 1}{K}} = 0. 0 7 7 \mathrm {m o l / L}
$$

$$

Since dissolution occurs,  $[H^{+}]_{total} = 0.02 + [H^{+}]_{eq} = 0.097\mathrm{mol / L}$
