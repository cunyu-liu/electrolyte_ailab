# Question

Isotope labeling is a classical method used to study metabolic processes in living organisms. We consider culturing mammalian cells under oxygen-sufficient conditions using glucose labeled with different  $^{13}C$ , and adding different exogenous molecules to regulate cellular metabolism. Below is a description of the corresponding experimental methods and expected results. Please select the correct one.

A. 1. Cultivate cells with glucose labeled at the C4 position with  $^{13} \mathrm{C}$ , without adding any other exogenous small molecules, and the first carbon in pyruvate to be isotopically labeled will be the keto carbonyl carbon.  
B. After pretreating cells with iodoacetate, the culture medium was removed. When cells were cultured with glucose labeled with  $^{13}\mathrm{C}$  at the C6 position, the final isotopic labeling remained on the aldehyde carbon of glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate or the phosphate carbon of dihydroxyacetone phosphate.  
C. After pretreating the cells with sodium arsenate, the culture medium was removed. Cells were then cultured with glucose labeled with  $^{13}C$  at the C3 position, without adding any other exogenous small molecules, resulting in pyruvate not being isotopically labeled.  
D. When cells are cultured with glucose labeled at the C6 position using  $^{13} \mathrm{C}$  and a certain concentration of sodium fluoride is added to the culture medium, the final isotopic labeling is concentrated on the methylene carbon of phosphoenolpyruvate.  
E. After culturing cells with glucose labeled at the C4 position with  $^{13}C$ , the cells were collected within a short period, and saturated fatty acids were isolated from the cells. The  $^{13}C$  isotopic label could be detected in these fatty acids.  
F. After culturing cells with glucose labeled at the C1 position with  $^{13}\mathrm{C}$ , isolating the intracellular ketone bodies and detecting the isotopic labeling, the isotopic label should appear in the methyl and methylene groups of 3-hydroxybutyrate and acetoacetate, as well as in the carbonyl carbon of acetone.

G. Cells were cultured with glucose labeled at the C6 position with  $^{13}\mathrm{C}$ , and a high concentration of fluoroacetate was added to the culture medium. This treatment did not affect the detection of isotopic labeling in oxaloacetate.  
H. When cells are cultured with glucose labeled with  $^{13}C$  at the C3 position, the isotopic label can be detected in the carbon dioxide produced by cellular metabolism after at least one round of the citric acid cycle.  
1. When cells are cultured with glucose labeled with  $^{13}C$  at the C6 position, a minimum of 3 rounds of the citric acid cycle are required to generate isotopically labeled carbon dioxide in the citric acid cycle.  
J. None of the above statements are correct

# Answer

Correct Answer: I

# Detailed Explanation

First, the glycolysis process is examined. In glycolysis, the C1-C6 of glucose remain unchanged until the formation of fructose-1,6-bisphosphate. Subsequently, fructose-1,6-bisphosphate undergoes cleavage: carbons 1, 2, and 3 enter dihydroxyacetone phosphate (DHAP), with carbon 1 bonded to a phosphate group, carbon 2 as a carbonyl group, and carbon 3 bonded to a hydroxyl group; carbons 4, 5, and 6 enter glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P), with carbon 4 as an aldehyde group, carbon 5 bonded to a hydroxyl group, and carbon 6 bonded to a phosphate group. Under the action of triosephosphate isomerase, dihydroxyacetone phosphate isomerizes to glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate. This process does not involve the migration of the phosphate group, so carbon 1 retains the phosphate group, carbon 2 is bonded to a hydroxyl group, and carbon 3 remains an aldehyde group. Therefore, in glucose labeling, after the formation of glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, the pairs  $1/6$ ,  $2/5$ , and  $3/4$  are equivalent within their respective groups.

In acetyl-CoA, the methyl carbon carries the isotopic label, which subsequently enters the ketone body synthesis pathway. Three molecules of acetyl-CoA synthesize HMG-CoA, where the isotopic label appears on the  $\alpha$ -carbon of the thioester, the  $\beta$ -methyl carbon, and the  $\alpha$ -carbon of the carboxyl group. The citric acid cycle is blocked, so oxaloacetate will not show isotopic labeling.

Finally, examine isotopic transfer in the citric acid cycle. When the methyl group of acetyl-CoA carries an isotopic label, it appears on one of the methylene groups of succinate in the cycle. Note that succinate is a symmetric molecule; after hydration to malate and oxidation to oxaloacetate, the isotopic label may appear on either the carbonyl or methylene group of oxaloacetate. If the methylene group is labeled, in the next cycle, the label will appear on the carboxyl group of oxaloacetate. If the carbonyl group is labeled, in the next cycle, the label will appear on the hydroxyl carbon of citrate, return to the methylene group of succinate, and then reappear on the carbonyl or methylene group of oxaloacetate. Considering only the carboxyl group of oxaloacetate, one carboxyl group is released as  $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$  during the conversion of  $\alpha$ -ketoglutarate to succinyl-CoA. Thus, option I is correct.
