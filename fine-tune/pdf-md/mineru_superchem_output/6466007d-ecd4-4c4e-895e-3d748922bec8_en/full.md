# Question

Element X is mainly derived from associated minerals such as molybdenite. When molybdenite is roasted, X is converted to the volatile oxide A. Dissolving A in ammonia yields salt B, which can be reduced with hydrogen to obtain X. Reducing A with CO produces oxide C, whose crystal is cubic and has very low resistivity. Spongy X reacts with chlorine at  $400 \sim 500^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to obtain D, and D disproportionate upon contact with water, forming E and oxide F in a 1:2 ratio. The oxidation state of X in E is the same as in A. The structure of D is actually a dimer. In a nitrogen atmosphere, D undergoes thermal decomposition to produce G (containing X  $63.65\%$ ) and gas H.

Deduce the substances represented by the above letters and determine the correctness of the following statements.

1.  $\mathbf{X}$  belongs to Group 8  
2. The valence of the metal in  $\mathbf{A}$  is greater than or equal to 6  
3. The formula weight of  $\mathbf{B}$  is greater than 250  
4. The low resistivity of  $\mathbf{C}$  is due to the presence of metallic bonds in the crystal  
5. D is stable in air  
6. The formula weight of  $\mathbf{G}$  is greater than 300  
7.  $\mathbf{H}$  can be used for water purification

Let  $a$  be the sum of the squares of the serial numbers of all correct statements, and  $b$  be the square of the sum of the serial numbers of all incorrect statements. Find the value of  $a / b$ , taking three significant figures.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. 0.242

C. 0.542  
D. 0.280  
E. 2.10  
F. 0.147

# Answer

Correct Answer: B

# Detailed Explanation

The entry point of this question is  $\mathbf{G}$ .  $\mathbf{D}$  is obtained from the reaction of  $\mathbf{X}$  element and chlorine gas, and is a chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$ .  $\mathbf{D}$  decomposes upon heating to yield  $\mathbf{G}$  and gas  $\mathbf{H}$ . It is evident that  $\mathbf{G}$  is also a chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$ , while  $\mathbf{H}$  is a chloride of  $\mathbf{X}$  or chlorine gas. Let  $\mathbf{G}$  be  $XCl_{n}$ . Given that  $\mathbf{G}$  contains  $\mathbf{X}$  at  $63.65\%$ , calculate the atomic weight of  $\mathbf{X}$ . Only when  $n = 3$  does the atomic weight of  $\mathbf{X}$  equal 186.2, corresponding to  $Re$ . All other calculation results do not correspond to any element.  $\mathbf{G}$  is  $ReCl_{3}$ .

$Re$  is an element of the seventh subgroup, so statement 1 is incorrect.

The formula weight of  $\mathbf{G}$  is 293, which is less than 300, so statement 6 is incorrect.

Calcination of a mineral containing  $\mathbf{X}$  yields a volatile  $\mathbf{A}$ . From common elemental knowledge,  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $Re_{2}O_{7}$ . When  $\mathbf{A}$  is dissolved in ammonia water,  $\mathbf{B}$  obtained is  $NH_{4}ReO_{4}$ . The common preparation method for rhenium is the reduction of ammonium perphenate with hydrogen gas, which is consistent with the description in the question.

The valence state of the metal element in  $\mathbf{A}$  is 7, which is greater than 6, so statement 2 is correct.

The formula weight of  $\mathbf{B}$  is 268, which is greater than 250, so statement 3 is correct.

C is obtained by reducing A with  $CO$  and is a low-resistivity oxide with a cubic crystal system. Among the oxides of rhenium, only the classic  $ReO_{3}$  possesses such properties.
