# Question

The chloride A of a first-row transition metal M is a red paramagnetic liquid with a tetrahedral molecular geometry and a magnetic moment of  $\mu = 1.7B$ . M. When A is heated in an inert atmosphere, it releases chlorine gas and forms compound B; in B, chlorine forms a hexagonal close-packed structure, and metal atoms fill one-third of the octahedral voids. Under a high-pressure carbon monoxide atmosphere, in the presence of bis(2-methoxyethyl) ether (which can be abbreviated as L), B is reduced by metallic Na to obtain a 1:1 salt C; the cation of C is an octahedral coordination ion of Na, and the anion is an octahedral coordination ion of M. C reacts with a large amount of dilute phosphoric acid, releasing hydrogen gas and obtaining a green solid D; D is paramagnetic, sublimable, and easily ignites. D undergoes disproportionation in tetrahydrofuran (which can be abbreviated as THF), and the disproportionation product E can be recrystallized from dichloromethane; E is a 1:2 salt, and its cation is connected to 2 anions through the electrostatic interaction of M···O, so that all M in its structure are octahedrally coordinated. Based on the above information, select the correct option below.

A. All other options are incorrect  
B. A contains 2 unpaired electrons  
C. M's common oxides do not include  $\mathrm{M}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{5}$  
D. B contains 2 chlorine atoms.  
E. C's cation contains three diglyme molecules.  
F. The anion of  $\mathbf{C}$  possesses strong oxidizing properties.  
G. The structure of  $\mathbf{D}$  is octahedral and contains four elements.  
H. The anion in  $\mathbf{E}$  is not the same as the anion in  $\mathbf{C}$ .

In  $\mathbf{E}$ , for the  $\mathbf{M}$  with the higher oxidation state, the ligands attached to it have two different coordination environments

# Answer

Correct Answer: I

# Detailed Explanation

A being a tetrahedral configuration chloride indicates that the chemical formula of A is  $\mathrm{MCl}_4$ . According to the magnetic moment calculation formula  $\mu = \sqrt{n(n + 2)}$ , the number of unpaired electrons is 1, so option B is incorrect.

The number of unpaired electrons is calculated to be 1 based on the magnetic moment

Tetrahedral fields are usually high-spin, and since the number of unpaired electrons is 1, the metal  $\mathbf{M}$  is in the  $+4$  valence state. Therefore, it is inferred that the metal itself has 5 valence layer electrons, so  $\mathbf{M}$  is V. V has a common oxide  $\mathrm{V}_{2}\mathrm{O}_{5}$ , so option  $\mathbf{C}$  is incorrect.

It is inferred that  $\mathbf{M}$  is  $\mathbf{V}$

$\mathbf{B}$  is obtained from  $\mathbf{A}$  by releasing chlorine gas, so it should also be a chloride of V. At the same time, V occupies  $1/3$  of the octahedral voids formed by Cl, so  $\mathbf{B}$  is  $\mathrm{VCl}_3$ , and option  $\mathbf{D}$  is incorrect.

In  $\mathbf{C}$ , the cations and anions are octahedrally coordinated and formed in a high-pressure CO atmosphere. It is speculated that the anion is the CO hexa-coordinated V. The cation is Na coordinated by the ligand L. The ligand L is a tridentate ligand, so the cation chemical formula is  $\mathrm{NaL_2}^+$ , and the chemical formula of C is  $[\mathrm{NaL}_2][\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6]$ .

2 PTS

It is inferred that  $\mathbf{C}$  is  $[\mathrm{NaL}_2][\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6]$

C reacts with acid to release hydrogen gas to generate D, indicating that C is oxidized. It should be  $\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6^-$  losing electrons to generate  $\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6$ . In option G, it is correct that D has an octahedral configuration, but it only contains three elements. G is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

It is inferred that  $\mathbf{D}$  is  $\mathrm{V(CO)_6}$

$\mathbf{D}$  undergoes a disproportionation reaction to generate  $\mathbf{E}$ . The anion should be  $\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6^-$ . Since the ratio of cations to anions is 2:1, the cation center is  $\mathrm{V}^{2+}$ . Also, because the cation is connected to the O of two anions, there are four remaining coordination sites, which should be 4 THF. Therefore,  $\mathbf{E}$  is  $[\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{THF})_4][\mathrm{V}(\mathrm{CO})_6]_2$ . So, option  $\mathbf{E}$  states that the cation only contains two diethylene glycol dimethyl ethers, which is incorrect. In option  $\mathbf{H}$ ,  $\mathbf{E}$  and  $\mathbf{C}$  have the same anion, which is incorrect.

In option  $\mathbf{F}$ ,  $\mathrm{V(CO)}_6^-$  has a negative charge and has strong reducing properties rather than oxidizing properties, so  $\mathbf{F}$  is incorrect.

The structure of  $\mathbf{E}$  is linear, with the cation coordinated by 4 tetrahydrofuran molecules sandwiched between two anionic complexes. One CO from each of the two  $\mathrm{V(CO)_6}^-$  participates in the coordination of the central cation. There are two types of O environments, THF and CO, participating in the cation coordination, so option I is correct.
