# Question

At temperature T and pressure  $p^{\circ}$ , reactants are charged into a long tube. On the left side, 3 mol of A and 1 mol of B are added, while on the right side, 3 mol of D and 1 mol of He are introduced. The gases on both sides are separated by a freely movable partition (initially positioned at 0 cm, impermeable to gases), and the right side also has a freely movable piston (initially positioned at 4 cm). The bottom of the tube (the far left end) is marked at -4 cm. The following reactions are known to occur:

$$
2 \mathrm {A} (\mathrm {g}) + \mathrm {B} (\mathrm {g}) = \mathrm {C} (\mathrm {g}); \mathrm {K} _ {1} ^ {\circ} = 9. 8 7 \times 1 0 ^ {4}
$$

$$
\mathrm {A} (\mathrm {g}) + 2 \mathrm {C} (\mathrm {g}) = \mathrm {D} (\mathrm {g}); \mathrm {K} _ {2} ^ {\circ} (1 <   \mathrm {K} _ {2} ^ {\circ} <   1 0 0)

# Answer

Correct Answer: E

# Detailed Explanation

Since  $K_1^\circ$  is much greater than  $K_2^\circ$ , it can be assumed that the first reaction proceeds to completion, and  $n(B) = 0$ .

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$$
n (B) = 0
$$

Let the amount of  $\mathrm{n}(\mathrm{D})$  at equilibrium on the left side be  $\mathbf{x}$  mol, the amount of  $\mathrm{n}(\mathrm{A})$  at equilibrium on the right side be  $\mathbf{y}$  mol, and the position of the partition at equilibrium be a cm.

On the left side:  $\mathrm{n(A)} = (1 - \mathrm{x})\mathrm{mol}$ ,  $\mathrm{n(B)} = 0$ ,  $\mathrm{n(C)} = (1 - 2\mathrm{x})\mathrm{mol}$ ,  $\mathrm{n(D)} = \mathrm{x}\mathrm{mol}$

On the right side:  $\mathrm{n(A)} = \mathrm{y mol}$ ,  $\mathrm{n(B)} = 0$ ,  $\mathrm{n(C)} = 2\mathrm{y mol}$ ,  $\mathrm{n(D)} = (3 - \mathrm{y})\mathrm{mol}$ .

$$
2 - 2 x + 4 + 2 y = 8, y = 1 + x
$$

$$

Substituting  $y = 1 + x$ , we solve for  $x = 0.332$ , and  $K_2^\circ = 7.84$ .
