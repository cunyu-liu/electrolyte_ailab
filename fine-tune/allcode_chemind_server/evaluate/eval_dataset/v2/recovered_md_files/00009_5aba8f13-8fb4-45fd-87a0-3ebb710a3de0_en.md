# Question

Sparingly soluble salt A decomposes upon heating to produce four substances B, C, D, E (reaction 1). B is a black solid, insoluble in dilute hydrochloric acid and dilute sulfuric acid, but soluble in sodium sulfide solution; B is also soluble in nitric acid, accompanied by the formation of F; B reacts with a certain nonmetallic element upon heating to  $1000^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and cooling yields C. C is a liquid at room temperature; C reacts with chlorine gas at high temperature to produce a relatively common organic solvent G, with byproduct H also obtained (reaction 2), H is an orange-yellow liquid at room temperature; H can continue to react with C to obtain G (reaction 3). D is a gas at room temperature and can be prepared by reacting a highly toxic potassium salt with copper sulfate solution (reaction 4); upon adding ferric chloride solution to the resulting suspension, gas D is still generated (reaction 5); D burns in an appropriate amount of pure oxygen to produce CO and E; E is a gas at room temperature with a density slightly less than that of air.

Reactions 1-3 are chemical equations, reactions 4 and 5 are ionic equations, all balanced with the simplest stoichiometric coefficients. The following statements are made:

1. The chemical formula of  $\mathbf{A}$  has an even number of atoms.  
2. In the chemical equation for reaction 1, the sum of the coefficients of all species is a prime number.  
3. The number of binary compounds among  $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{H}$  is equal to 4.  
4. In the ionic equations for reactions 4 and 5, the sum of the reactant coefficients are equal.  
5. The sum of the coefficients of all species in the chemical equation for reaction 2 is less than the sum of the product coefficients in the chemical equation for reaction 3.

# Answer
