# Question

It is known that  $\mathrm{[XeF][AsF_6]}$  reacts with  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  in anhydrous HF according to the following equilibrium:

$$
\left[ \mathrm {X e F} \right]\left[ \mathrm {A s F} _ {6} \right] + \mathrm {H} _ {2} \mathrm {O} + \mathrm {H F} \rightleftharpoons \mathrm {X e F} _ {2} + \left[ \mathrm {H} _ {3} \mathrm {O} \right]\left[ \mathrm {A s F} _ {6} \right]
$$

When equimolar amounts of  $\mathrm{[XeF][AsF_6]}$  and  $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$  react in anhydrous HF at  $-78^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the adduct  $\mathrm{[H_3O][AsF_6]}\cdot 2\mathrm{XeF}_2$  is initially formed. Subsequently,  $\mathrm{[XeF][AsF_6]}$  reacts with free  $\mathrm{XeF}_2$  in a 1:1 ratio to yield compound A. This adduct further reacts with free  $\mathrm{XeF}_2$  to form a 1:1 salt B. The anion of B is identical to that of A, while its cation contains oxygen with a coordination number of 2. The mass fraction of Xe in B is approximately  $60\%$ . The cation of B is obtained from the cation of A by reacting with the electrically neutral species C, a process involving a single proton transfer.

Which of the following statements is correct:

A. The cation of  $\mathbf{A}$  is linear in shape.  
B. The relative molecular mass of A is smaller than that of C.  
C. The cations of  $\mathbf{B}$  are triangular in shape.  
D. The chemical reaction equation for the formation of  $\mathbf{B}$  (with coefficients in the simplest whole-number ratio), where the sum of the coefficients on the right side of the equation is 3.  
E. C contains 3 types of elements.  
F. The number of atoms contained in C is 4

# Answer

Correct Answer: F

# Detailed Explanation

The formation of  $\mathbf{A}$  is straightforward. Since it is a 1:1 reaction,  $\mathrm{XeF_2}$  can only insert into  $\mathrm{XeF^{+}}$  to yield  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$ , which is the cation of  $\mathbf{A}$ . According to VSEPR theory, one F atom bridges the two Xe atoms and adopts  $\mathrm{sp}^3$  hybridization, resulting in a trigonal shape rather than a linear geometry. Therefore, option A is incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

The cation of  $\mathbf{A}$  is  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$  with a trigonal shape

The anion of  $\mathbf{B}$  is the same as that of  $\mathbf{A}$ , both being hexafluoroarsenate anions. The cation contains oxygen with a coordination number of 2, suggesting it is composed of  $\mathrm{Xe}$ ,  $\mathrm{O}$ , and  $\mathrm{F}$ . Combining the mass fraction, the chemical formula of  $\mathbf{B}$ 's cation is determined to be  $\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$ .

# CHECKPOINT

2 PTS

The chemical formula of B's cation is  $\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$

Considering its structure, oxygen cannot be a terminal atom and must act as a bridging atom. Based on the structure of  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$ ,  $\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$  can be derived by replacing a terminal fluorine atom in  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$  with an oxygen atom and then attaching a XeF unit, described as F - Xe - F - Xe - O - Xe - F. According to hybridization theory, it adopts a bent shape, making option C incorrect.

# CHECKPOINT

1 PTS

$\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$  has a bent shape

Thus, the reaction for forming  $\mathbf{B}$  can be written as:

$$
3 \mathrm {X e F} _ {2} + [ \mathrm {H} _ {3} \mathrm {O} ] [ \mathrm {A s F} _ {6} ] = [ \mathrm {X e} _ {3} \mathrm {O F} _ {3} ^ {+} ] [ \mathrm {A s F} _ {6} ] + 3 \mathrm {H F}
$$

The sum of the coefficients on the right side of the equation is 4, so option D is incorrect.

$\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$  is generated from  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$  and  $\mathbf{C}$  via a proton transfer process. Given that  $\mathrm{Xe}_3\mathrm{OF}_3^+$  is equivalent to replacing a terminal fluorine atom in  $\mathrm{Xe}_2\mathrm{F}_3^+$  with an oxygen atom and attaching a XeF unit, the displaced fluorine atom likely combines with the proton. Therefore, the structure of  $\mathbf{C}$  is clearly HOXeF, containing four elements and four atoms. Thus, option F is correct, while options E and C are incorrect.

The structure of  $\mathbf{C}$  is HOXeF
