# Question

The FeSe-based system is the simplest type of iron-based superconducting material. The filling of iron vacancies with Cu in the form of a univalent cation will affect the structure and electrical and magnetic properties of the superconductor. In this experiment, precipitation gravimetry and EDTA complexometric titration were used to determine the chemical composition of a Fe - Cu - Se superconductor. The specific experimental procedure is as follows:

(a) Accurately weigh approximately  $0.4639\mathrm{g}$  of the sample into a  $100\mathrm{mL}$  beaker, add  $6\mathrm{mL}$  of concentrated nitric acid and  $6\mathrm{mL}$  of concentrated hydrochloric acid, and heat. Add concentrated hydrochloric acid during heating until the sample is completely dissolved. Add  $20\%$  hydroxylamine hydrochloride solution dropwise under stirring and heating until precipitation is complete, keep warm for a period of time, carefully filter out the precipitate with pre-weighed filter paper, wash several times with deionized water, dry at low temperature until constant weight, and record the mass as  $0.2565\mathrm{g}$ .  
(b) Weigh approximately  $0.8527\mathrm{g}$  of the sample into a  $100~\mathrm{mL}$  beaker, add  $10~\mathrm{mL}$  of concentrated nitric acid and  $10~\mathrm{mL}$  of concentrated hydrochloric acid, and heat. Add concentrated hydrochloric acid during heating until the sample is completely dissolved. Concentrate the solution to about  $10~\mathrm{mL}$ , and dilute to  $100~\mathrm{mL}$  with deionized water. Transfer  $25.00~\mathrm{mL}$  of the above solution to a conical flask, and adjust the  $\mathsf{pH}$  to  $3\sim 4$  with  $1:1$  ammonia water; add  $5\mathrm{g}$  of solid ammonium fluoride, shake to dissolve, then add  $5\mathrm{mL}$  of  $2\mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{L}$  acetic acid-sodium acetate buffer solution, 5 drops of PAN indicator, and titrate slowly with  $0.02035\mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{L}$  EDTA until the endpoint is reached when the purple-red color changes to gray-blue-green, consuming  $13.94~\mathrm{mL}$ .  
(c) Transfer  $10.00 \, \mathrm{mL}$  of the  $100 \, \mathrm{mL}$  solution prepared in (b) to a  $100 \, \mathrm{mL}$  volumetric flask and dilute to the mark with distilled water. Transfer  $25.00 \, \mathrm{mL}$  of the solution to a small beaker, add  $7 \, \mathrm{mL}$  of  $1:1$  ammonia water while stirring, heat for a period of time, and filter under normal pressure after the precipitate is obviously agglomerated. Wash the precipitate three times with deionized water. Discard the filtrate. Dissolve the precipitate with  $15 \, \mathrm{mL}$  of  $0.4 \, \mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{L}$  sulfuric acid, collect the solution in a conical flask, add  $10 \, \mathrm{mL}$  of deionized water, 5 drops of sulfosalicylic acid indicator, and titrate with  $0.008140 \, \mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{L}$  EDTA until the purple-red color fades significantly, which is the endpoint, consuming  $14.53 \, \mathrm{mL}$ .

For this experiment, the following options are correct (for options involving calculations, consider errors within  $0.1\%$  as correct)

A. Ascorbic acid thiourea can be used to mask Cu to measure Fe  
B. Cyanide masking of Cu can be used to measure Fe.  
C. Sulfur ion masking of Cu can be used to measure Fe.  
D. Hydrochloric acid + hydrogen peroxide can be used as a complete replacement for nitric acid + hydrochloric acid.  
E. (a) In experiments, high-temperature drying can be used to speed up the precipitation process.  
F. (a) If there is no holding period at a constant temperature during the experiment, the calculated mass fraction of Se will be higher.  
G. (c) The purpose of adding ammonia in the experiment is to adjust the  $\mathrm{pH}$  to slightly acidic.  
H. (c) The titration temperature in the experiment was room temperature.  
1. Representing the alloy with  $\mathrm{Fe}_x\mathrm{Cu}_y\mathrm{Se}$ , then  $x + y = 0.8969$  
J. The ratio of ferrous iron to ferric iron is approximately 2.516 : 1 .  
K. The mass fraction of Se is 1.954 times that of Fe.  
L. All of the above options are incorrect.

# Answer
